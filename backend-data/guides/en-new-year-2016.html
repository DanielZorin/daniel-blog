It's time to look back 2016! This year I visited 36 countries (by the way, an absolute record: in 2013 there were 32, in 2014 - 30), of which 17 new: Myanmar, Singapore, East Timor, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Gibraltar, Aland Islands, Puerto Rico, US Virgin Islands, Sint Maarten, Saint Martin, Curacao, Trinidad and Tobago, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Barbados, Haiti , Dominican Republic. I have visited 19 countries again: Thailand, Laos, Malaysia, Indonesia, Russia, Spain, Portugal, France, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium, Poland, Belarus, Finland, Sweden, Latvia, Estonia, USA, Italy. I was in Finland in 2011, in Estonia in 2006, and there haven’t even been any blog posts about these countries yet. In three more countries (UAE, Great Britain, Saint Lucia) I was on a layover at the airport. I stayed in 115 hotels (including hostels and airbnb), in 6 houses (three in St. Petersburg and three in Moscow). Also spent 18 nights on the road: - on Russian, Belarusian and Burmese trains - on buses in Malaysia, Myanmar, Indonesia, USA - twice on the plane - five times at airports: Domodedovo, Charleroi, Helsinki, Kuala Lumpur and Chicago - and two more nights on a ship from Tallinn to Stockholm and back. The most expensive hotel was in Barbados for $54, in Trinidad I paid $46. Several more times I paid around $30-35: Stuttgart, Brussels, Turku, Boston, St. Maarten. In all other places no more than $25. The cheapest hotels were in Thailand for 200 baht ($5.5) in Chiang Rai and Chumphon. In Moscow, in a hostel once it turned out to be a little cheaper for two. Expenses for the year amounted to $19,050, or about $52 per day. Compared to last year, I spent a lot more money on air travel, plus a little more on new equipment, clothes, visas. Expenses on food and local travel are almost the same as in 2015, which confirms my hypothesis that the cost of food is approximately the same in different countries and regions. We'll see what happens in 2017.
