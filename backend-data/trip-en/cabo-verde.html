Cabo Verde: Praia, Cidade Velha

8-10 August 2024

Praia

Cabo Verde, or the Cape Verde Islands, is the most un-African country in Africa. It is more convenient to get here from Europe than from West Africa. The islands were discovered by the Portuguese in the 15th century, and before that they were uninhabited, so the population here is Creole, much like in the Caribbean. The nature of Cape Verde is beautiful, although rougher than in the Canaries. But moving between the islands is not very convenient - there are few flights, ferries are even rarer and more expensive. That's why so far I have only visited the capital island.

Cidade Velha

The historical capital of Cape Verde is only half an hour's drive from the modern one. It is called Cidade Velha (old town) since the 18th century, when the capital was moved to Praia. Before that, the city was called Ribeira Grande.

The minibus to Cidade Velha costs 1 dollar. The road goes through deserted hills.

Such a harsh landscape on the Cape Verde Islands.
