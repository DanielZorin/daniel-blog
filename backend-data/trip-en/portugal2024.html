Portugal: Lisbon, Santarem, Porto

10-12 August 2024

Lisbon

After a month in Africa, I flew to Lisbon - the most convenient destination from Cape Verde. Portugal was the first country I went to on my own in 2011. Since then, I have been there twice more - in 2014 and 2016. And now, I can't believe it, eight years later, I am walking along these streets again.

Lisbon airport. Since last time, they have extended the metro line here.

Prices for fish dishes, not expensive. In general, Portugal is a relatively cheap country even now, although not as cheap as in 2011. Over these 13 years, many have appreciated Portugal, it has become a super-touristy place and a mecca for nomads, and the cost of rent in Lisbon has gone through the roof. It has become unrealistic to get a residence permit, applications are processed for years.

Santarem

Although I didn't have much time in Portugal, I still wanted to see some new city. It had to be between Lisbon and Porto, which meant either Fatima (if going by bus) or Santarem (if going by train). I chose the train, because I missed trains. I had already been to the city of Santarem in Brazil.

Portuguese train. The bus is generally cheaper if you buy a ticket well in advance.

Traditional tiles at the train station.
