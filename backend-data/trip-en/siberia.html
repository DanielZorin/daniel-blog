Krasnoyarsk

Everything has changed in Krasnoyarsk. It suddenly became warmer, the temperature was -10. Immediately when you get off the train you can see why you need to go to Krasnoyarsk.

Krasnoyarsk is very worth a visit due to its gorgeous nature. This is a rare case when in Russia an AAA place is easy, quick and inexpensive to get to. There are two more - Elbrus and Curonian Spit. The fact is that on the outskirts of Krasnoyarsk there is the Krasnoyarsk Pillars nature reserve.

There are two ways to get there. Actually, the bus goes to the main entrance. The downside of this option is that from the main entrance to the first pillars, the walk is about 7 kilometers, that is, at least an hour. At the end you still have to go back the same way. We were not sure that with our equipment and experience in cold weather we would be able to do this, especially since there was nowhere to warm up in the reserve, there were no cafes (logical, it’s a reserve!). Therefore, you can use the second option: get off a few stops earlier and go to the Bobrovy Log fun park.

Funpark is just a ski resort, there are slopes for skiing, and there is also a chair lift from which you can go up, which is cool in itself. The lift works if the temperature is above -25, that day it was just -20.

From above you can see the panorama of Krasnoyarsk, although the center is a bit far away. But even in this photo you can see the small chapel that is depicted on the 10 ruble banknote.

Then there are two ways. If you go left, you will end up on the Takmak rock. It seems like it’s not far to go, so going there is the lightest version of Pillars. But we are not looking for easy ways, so we go to the right. There, behind the wooden gates, the actual territory of the reserve begins. In about 10 minutes you can walk to the equipped observation deck.

From the site you can already see some pillars, but the bulk is further away. And here’s the trick: from the observation deck to the group of central pillars it’s a closer walk than from the main entrance; we walked back without stopping in about 40 minutes, i.e. there are about 3-4 kilometers there.

It is difficult to get lost in the park, at least in winter the well-trodden path is clearly visible, and other people periodically appear. In some places the trail is marked with small traces of paint on the trees.

There is taiga here, its own atmosphere. Although it was not very hot, it was easy to walk.

And here are the pillars!

We even conquered one of them, the simplest one.

I think it is better to visit the pillars in winter than in summer. After all, in the summer there are, firstly, mosquitoes and midges. Secondly, if it rains even a little, it will greatly spoil the walk, and in winter only if there is a very severe blizzard.

The main disadvantage of winter is that it gets dark early. It's time to go back.
