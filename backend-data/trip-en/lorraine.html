9-10 April 2024

Paris

I was planning to fly to Paris in the middle of the day, to walk along the boulevards, but the flight was delayed for 7 hours, and in the end I barely caught the last tram to the metro from Orly airport. The only thing that warmed me was the thought of 280 euros in compensation from the airline (which is twice the price of round-trip tickets). In the morning I immediately left for Brittany, and returned to Paris only a few days later, and only passing through.

Nancy

The city of Nancy is the historical capital of the Lorraine region. The central square is called Place Stanislas in honor of Stanislaw Leszczynski, the king of Poland, who was overthrown twice, first during the Northern War in 1709, and then after the War of the Polish Succession in 1736, after which, as a consolation, he was made Duke of Lorraine (then still independent within Holy Roman Empire), and after his death Lorraine became part of France. The ensemble of the square was built precisely in that era.

It is a large square area, with entrances at the corners.

Metz

In 1871, following the Franco-Prussian War, Germany annexed Alsace and Lorraine. In 1918, these regions returned to France, then in 1940-1945 Germany annexed them again, but not for long. In fact, Germany did not receive all of Lorraine, but only the northern part, including the city of Metz, the current capital of the region. The city with such a German name is still different from other French cities. I leave the station and it’s like I’m in Frankfurt or Dusseldorf.

The station itself is also German. Built just before the war, they made it specially huge so that many trains could pass straight to Bakhmut Verdun.

The area around the station was also built in that era, which is why it is so un-French.
