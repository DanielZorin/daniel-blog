Surat Thani

A transit city where they like to trick white tourists into buying expensive transport. However, I didn't have any problems. I arrived by train, walked to the station, bought a bus + ferry ticket to Koh Samui. You don't need to give in to the touts at the station, and you don't need to come here on a tourist bus. On the way back I went south to Nakhon Si Thammarat, there are no scams in this direction. They say that if you go from Koh Samui towards Bangkok or Phuket via Surat Thani, there is a risk of getting into a minibus that will take you somewhere 10 km from the city center, from where only scam buses and expensive taxis go.

* * *

Every month on the day of the full moon, the Full Moon Party is held on Koh Phangan - probably the largest regular party in Southeast Asia. The event is significant, I went.

I was worried that there would be no ferry tickets, but you can buy them the day before without any problems, although the regular ferry from Samui runs only three times a day and does not accommodate many people. Apparently, many people don’t bother and buy a special transfer not from the main pier (it’s twice as expensive). The Full Moon Party takes place on Haad Rin Beach in the southeast of Koh Phangan. Already during the day it becomes a bit crowded there.

The beach is generally a bit small, and there are a lot of hotels around, besides FMP, I wouldn’t stay here.

Two main attributes of FMP. Firstly, fluorescent paints in acid colors.

Secondly, buckets. The bucket is a signature cocktail consisting of Thai strong drink Hong Thong or Sang Som, soda and Red bull (the bottle of Red bull in the photo lies at the bottom of the bucket). It’s inexpensive, 200 baht (only a little more expensive than buying all the ingredients at 7-11), it’s very easy to drink through a straw, and, as you might guess, you get drunk very quickly.

There are lighter drinks too.

They also sell food, the prices are higher than usual, but I wouldn’t say extortionate. Pad Thai is generally worth it.

Towards evening, everyone decorates themselves and moves towards the beach.

Anonymous masks.

Mostly people here are aged 18-23, although there is also a more respectable audience.

Alena and I got hold of paints and put them to work.

Somewhere after midnight, entry to the beach costs 100 baht. The ticket sellers can only be seen at the main entrance. And some are taken by boat directly to the beach. Although the party should generate a very respectable profit every month, the organization is very lame. There is a catastrophic shortage of trash cans and toilets.

Traditionally, the fiery inscription is set on fire.

Nakhon Si Thammarat

Narathiwat

The provinces of Yala, Pattani and Narathiwat in the south of Thailand are for some reason considered dangerous territory. On the official websites of the Foreign Ministries of various Western countries, the threat level is not even orange, but red - like in Syria or Somalia. Before that, I had been to a similar place once - in the Caucasus. In the south of Thailand there are predominantly Muslim Malays who would like to secede. Terrorist attacks occur approximately every week, but what if you read the news more closely? “In village X, a terrorist attacked a policeman with a knife,” “In village Y, terrorists burned down a school.” Over the past 10 years there has not been a single major incident; all terrorist attacks are directed against the army, police or schools (as places where the damned occupiers force you to learn the Thai language). In addition, almost all attacks occur in rural areas, because in cities there are simply more police, checkpoints, and it is more difficult to organize a terrorist attack. So don’t listen to the warnings and feel free to travel around the region.

In general, Narathiwat is not rich in attractions, but it has its own atmosphere.
