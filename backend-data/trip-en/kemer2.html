From here, the most interesting part of the canyon is still about two kilometers along the road with a large climb, but you can get there by pickup truck (this is included in the ticket price).

Çıralı

Çıralı is the southernmost point of the Kemer region. Getting here is quite difficult - there are no direct buses, you need to catch a bus on the highway that goes further to Kumluca/Finike, get off at the turn and wait there for a minibus that seems to run once an hour, but probably only in the summer, since I didn’t see it in the spring after waiting 2 hours.

If you go to Çıralı by car, you can stop along the way in the village of Ulupinar at the top near the highway. This village is famous for its restaurants (there are advertisements on the highway); you can sit in a beautiful place in the forest near the waterfalls.

There is space for cars, of course.

Shish kebab.

Next I tried to climb Mount Chimera. This is a mountain at the top of which there is a release of methane, which is why there is a fire burning there all the time.

Here I made a mistake - I followed the short path indicated on MAPS.ME. Starts at this hotel.

The path was poorly marked, I soon got lost and lost my way, scratched all my legs and arms on thorny bushes, despaired and went down.

There is no need to go here. If you have a car, drive right through Çıralı and there will be a parking closer to the top, where the climb should be much easier.

The village of Çıralı strongly contrasts with the rest of the coastal towns of the Kemer region, where there are many huge all-inclusive hotels. The atmosphere of this place is reminiscent of some backpacker resort in Southeast Asia.

Gedelme

In addition to the cities and towns along the coast, the Kemer region includes a considerable territory far from the sea, in the mountains at significant altitudes of 500 meters. There, in addition to the cable car to Tahtali, which I already wrote about, there are small villages connected by narrow mountain roads, ultimately also leading to Antalya in the north and Kumluca in the south. There is no public transport there, but there are tourists who walk the entire Lycian Way, or you can go by car.

At Çıralı, the Lycian Way splits into two alternative routes. One option is to immediately go up straight to Tahtali, and the second is to go along the places familiar to me through Tekirova, Phaselis and Çamyuva, go through Kuzdere, and then there is a road that goes first along the same Kemer river (separating Çamyuva and Kiriş) along the gorge and then up. Both versions of the Lycian Way converge near the village of Gedelme at an altitude of about 700 meters.

The gorge here is no less beautiful than in Göynük.
