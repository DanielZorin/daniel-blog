Vatican

13,15 January 2017

The Vatican is the smallest state in the world. Most of the buildings in it are inaccessible to ordinary people without special passes: there even seems to be a grocery store and other infrastructure, but that’s all for those who work for the Pope. Tourists can visit St. Peter's Cathedral and the square in front of it (free), the museum with the Sistine Chapel, Raphael's rooms, etc. (entrance 16 euros, I was there in 2009), and the gardens where you can book a special tour (32 euros). There is also a combo tour that includes a visit to the Pope's residence on the outskirts of Rome, where you are transported by train directly from the Vatican.
