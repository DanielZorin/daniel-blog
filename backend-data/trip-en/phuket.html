Thailand: Ratchaburi, Phetchaburi, Hua Hin, humphon, Phang Nga, Phuket

12-22 October 2015

Hua Hin

Hua Hin is considered a relatively expensive resort (compared to Pattaya, the islands are still more expensive). The beach here looks very attractive and photogenic.

But I didn’t like swimming here at all - there are a lot of rocks and sharp stones in the sea, the water is muddy. Fail.

These stones can be seen even from the shore.

But the beach is nice, yes.

And there are no problems with space. Though, it is inconvenient to get to the beach - there is a huge Hilton hotel along the shore, you need to go around its territory if you live in the city center. Despite the fact that there is nothing fenced on the beach - in Thailand all the beaches are state-owned, it is impossible to grab a piece of the shore.

Beach at night.

Hua Hin city is very densely built up with hotels and restaurants.

Residents of northern European countries like to hang out here; many signs are duplicated in Finnish, Norwegian, Swedish, German, and Danish.

Oktoberfest.

There are very few Russians. I saw only one Russian restaurant, and it seemed to be closed forever.

Tourist restaurants are quite expensive by Thai standards (1 dollar = 35 baht).

You'll have to pay extra for European breakfast food.

But further from the coast, I easily found a Thai canteen with regular prices of 30-40 baht per dish. Pad thai, mmm.

There is a royal palace in Hua Hin, but it is not a tourist attraction - the late Rama IX lived there most of the time in recent years. The area is fenced off, nothing is visible.

Chumphon

The main (and the only) attraction of the city of Chumphon is the Chumphon Hotel. For just 200 baht you can travel back to the 19th century!

What impressed me most was the chamber pot in the corner.

Among the attributes of the 20th century there is a TV.

There is only one socket and it is very flimsy. However, electricity already existed in the 19th century.

Hall. There is no roof over it, so when it rains the floor floods.

Reception.

View from the balcony.

The city of Chumphon seemed somehow gloomy to me.

I went to the market looking for durians, but there were none.

Six months later, I suddenly found myself passing through this city again (the cheapest train from Bangkok to Chumphon costs less than 100 baht), and again checked into the same hotel. I didn’t find anything new, except that I saw metal seagulls sitting on local poles.

Phang Nga

Phang Nga Bay is the Thai version of Vietnamese Ha Long Bay with karst cliffs in the middle of the sea.

The popular resort of Phuket is nearby, from where excursions arrive daily. I took an unconventional path and settled on the mainland in the city also called Phang Nga. You won’t be able to save much on this: a tour of the islands cost 1,100 baht, in Phuket it’s 100-200 baht more expensive. But this allowed me to gain time without spending two hours on the way there and back. In addition, there were only 4 people in the group, while the boats from Phuket were packed to capacity.

The road from the mainland begins in dense thickets of mangrove trees.

Tourists are transported on small motor boats.

There are rocks even in the city center, and near the coast too, of course.

Cave.

A village on stilts near one of the islands. Muslim, judging by the mosque.

We go out into the bay.

We approach the most famous place - Khao Phing Kan Island, popularly known as "James Bond Island".

The film "The Man with the Golden Gun" was filmed here.

There are countless tourists, including Russian-speaking ones.

Phuket is on the other side of the bay, it’s further to sail from there than from the mainland, so these are the boats that come from there.

This is a very small island, only two rocks, one of which split open. Between them there is a small isthmus where several souvenir shops operate.

It is often said that James Bond Island is called Ko Tapu, but this is not true. Ko Tapu is this famous rock at the entrance to the bay.

Phuket

I immediately liked Phuket as soon as I checked into the hotel.

All this splendor cost 240 baht per day ($7).

This place was not somewhere in the end of the world, but a 10-minute walk from the center of Patong (Jungceylon shopping center), however, it was necessary to walk through a wasteland, since the road made a huge detour. Patong is the most popular resort village on the island.

It has its own Walking street.

There are strip clubs, but fewer than in Pattaya. In general, Patong does not look like a sex resort.

Bar.

The Russian language has its place in Patong. Prices in the restaurant are high.

Uzbek establishments are also popular.

Newspaper in English, Russian and Chinese.

Restaurants with normal prices are easy to find along the highway.

Patong Beach is not outstanding.

There are taxis on every corner in Phuket. Supply greatly exceeds demand, but the cartel keeps prices at a high level. So, a trip from Patong to the neighboring city of Karon (5 kilometers) costs 250-300 baht. It's funny that the taxi says "taxi meter", although no one here uses a meter.

Moreover, there is also the problem of finding a motorcycle taxi, at least in Karon I didn’t find one at all, although I didn’t really want to walk 5 kilometers through the hills.

There is still public transport in Phuket, but it is all tied to Phuket Town - the capital of the island, where tourists usually do not live, because there is no sea there. So you can still get from Patong to Karon for less than 100 baht, but it will take a lot of time.

Phuket has the largest songthaews in Thailand, they are made from trucks.

Phuket Town itself is an interesting place. There are not many places in Thailand where old architecture has been preserved.

Phuket Town was built by the Chinese, so it is quite similar to George Town in Malaysia.
