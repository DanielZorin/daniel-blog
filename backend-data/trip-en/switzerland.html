Switzerland: Buchs, St. Gallen, Zurich, Lucerne, Zug

8-11 July 2013

St. Gallen

Until you've been to Switzerland, you don't know what "expensive" is. Yes, there are places in the world where prices are even higher for objective reasons (all sorts of remote islands in the Pacific Ocean, where everything needs to be imported), but only in Switzerland are prices high in proportion to the high incomes of the population. If in Norway the average salary is around 3000 euro, then in Switzerland it is closer to 5000. We can say that Switzerland was lucky when after the Napoleonic Wars it was declared neutral, and all other regimes were interested in its stability in order to store their gold there. Apricots cost $0.50 each, not bad, right?

The most expensive McDonald's in the world. A Big Mac costs about 240 rubles / $8, three times more expensive than in Russia.

One might assume that the people of Switzerland should look down on everyone since they are head and shoulders richer than virtually the rest of the world. But this is absolutely not true. I don’t like the high cost, but Switzerland definitely makes me feel good.

Zurich

The first impression of Zurich was strange. I completely feel like I’m in Jakarta. The parking lot is protected on all sides by huge, random coils of barbed wire, and this is in one of the safest countries in the world!

It turned out that I settled in the middle of the red light district (hence the comparative cheapness of the hotel - only 80 euros per room). At night, crowds of drunken schoolchildren walk the streets, and prostitutes stand at the entrances to brothels. There are no windows, like in Amsterdam, they sit directly on the stands along the sidewalk.

The hotel used an advanced system - no reception, you need to enter the booking code into the machine, and it will give out the key.

In Switzerland, despite the general high cost, not everywhere they try to skin you. Namely, here, more than anywhere else, you can often find drinking fountains that eliminate the need to buy water...

...and there are also free toilets.
