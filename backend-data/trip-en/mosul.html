Iraq: Kirkuk, Mosul, Erbil

1-4 November 2023

Kirkuk

The city of Kirkuk is known as the center of Iraqi oil production. Because of this, it is disputed between central Iraq and Kurdistan. Amid the ISIS offensive in 2014, the Kurds briefly captured Kirkuk, but it is now back under federal government control. The most ironic thing is that in fact most people in Kirkuk are neither Kurds, nor Arabs, but... Turks. More precisely, Iraqi Turkmens (or Turkomans). They speak the Turkmen dialect of Turkish, which is similar to Azerbaijani or the eastern dialects of Turkey.

Mosul

Mosul is the second largest city in Iraq with a sad fate. In 2014, ISIS boldly attacked Mosul, the Iraqi army was forced to retreat, and the city of a million fell under the control of the terrorist group in a couple of days. It was here that ISIS proclaimed their “world caliphate.” Half the population fled. For three years, Mosul was the capital of ISIS in Iraq; at its peak, the terrorists controlled most of northern Iraq and came close to Baghdad (Fallujah is only 50 kilometers away). In 2016-2017, the epic battle for Mosul took place, which lasted nine months and ended with the liberation of the city, and with it, essentially, ISIS in Iraq was defeated and went underground. Such a long battle meant ruthless urban warfare for every stronghold, leaving the entire central part of Mosul in ruins in 2017. Over the past six years, something has already been rebuilt, and life has returned to the city, but much remains to be done.

Erbil

Erbil is the capital of Iraqi Kurdistan. Kurdistan is somewhat isolated from the rest of Iraq, there was no war here, tourists have been allowed here for a long time on separate visas, so the infrastructure here is somewhat better - there are all sorts of chain brand stores and many hotels. The city is built in rings, and in the middle there is a citadel on a hill, listed as a World Heritage Site.

Though the citadel is under restoration, and the entrance there is completely closed.

People used to live there, I don’t know what will happen to them after the restoration.
