Saarbrücken

Saarland is one of three German states where I have not been (the other two are Mecklenburg and Saxony-Anhalt, which I actually passed through in transit). I didn’t think I’d end up here, I probably wouldn’t have made a special trip, but it turned out to be just along the way. It is the smallest state in Germany and is remarkable mainly for its history. Coal was previously mined here, and in the 19th and 20th centuries it was a strategically important region. After the First World War, under the Treaty of Versailles, Saarland was occupied by French troops for 15 years. Coal went to France as reparations. 15 years later, in 1935, a plebiscite was held in Saarland, as a result of which the region united with Germany. After 1945 it was occupied again, and finally returned to Germany in 1957 (in 1955 there was a referendum where residents voted against independence).
