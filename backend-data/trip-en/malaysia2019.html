Langkawi

Langkawi is an island in the northwest of Malaysia, north of Penang. Administratively, this is part of the state of Kedah, where I had been before, but it has a special status as a duty-free zone - alcohol is cheaper here. There are ferries to Langkawi, but it is easier to fly for 10-20 dollars by plane from Kuala Lumpur or Georgetown.

There is no public transport in Langkawi at all, you can only get around by taxi, fortunately, all the usual apps like Uber and Grab work. From the airport I immediately went to the western edge of the island to Oriental Village.

This is a theme amusement park with streets vaguely reminiscent of colonial ones.

It’s not very clear what is “oriental” here.

The main thing is that the cable car starts from here.

The cable car climbs 700 meters to the top of Machinchang Mountain.

Eastern village below.

With the exception of the village, this part of the island is almost uninhabited.
