Finland: Savonlinna, Imatra

1 October 2016

Imatra

Many residents of St. Petersburg go shopping to Finland. One of the motivations is the need to “properly use” a visa from the Finnish consulate. You can also buy a lot of goods there that have not been sold in Russia since 2014. Typically, shopping tours go to the cities of Lappeenranta and Imatra, which are closest to the border. It's a little closer to Lappeenranta, but Imatra has a little less queue at the border on weekends. Bus tours depart from St. Petersburg daily for about 700 rubles per person. To make it more than just shopping, I chose a one-day tour to the historical city of Savonlinna for 1,300 rubles. This is by far the cheapest way to get to this city, because the road from Helsinki at Finnish railway rates will be several times more expensive. Before Savonlinna there was also a stop at a store near the border. There, right next to the border terminal, there are three shops in an open field, but two of them were closed: although they sell sanctioned cheese, prices converteed to rubles doubled in 2014, and now buying food in Finland is significantly more expensive than in Russia.

Here it is - cheese! This is the famous Finnish Oltermanni cheese, it is apparently inexpensive - only 6 euros per kilogram.

The notorious Parmesan starts from 6 euros per 200 grams, however, it is not much cheaper in Italy either.

There is no jamon, but there is "fuet".

Sausages.

In general, the selection of imported goods in this store is not so large, mostly Finnish.

Very cheap meatballs at a discount.

Famous Finnish oil "Viola".

Olive oil is something that still costs much more in Russia than in Europe.

Olives don’t seem to grow in Finland, but you can buy oil for less than 5 euros/liter.

Sauces.

The choice of fish is large, but it is not cheap at all.

It's cheaper in Malaysia.

Red caviar - about 30 euros per kilogram.

Black caviar - 400-450 euros per kilogram. In Russia it is still much cheaper.

Vegetables and fruits are more expensive than in Russia.

The cheapest potatoes cost more than a euro.

Previously, people here bought household chemicals, but now there is no point - anyway, these detergents are not much different from each other, and one bottle lasts for a couple of months.

My purchases are worth 32 euros.

Four types of cheese, two sauces, two marinades, ham, meatballs, sausage, fish and canned food.

Savonlinna

In the city of Savonlinna there is the most famous castle in Finland - Olavinlinna.

Crosses made of iron rods are often found on the walls. Why, for strengthening?

You can walk freely around the rather large grounds of the castle.

You can even climb the walls.

And look into the loopholes.

The castle stands on a small island.

Savonlinna is a small city, only about 20 thousand inhabitants.

As you would expect from Finland, it is clean and cozy.

It vaguely resembles a Russian province in architecture; after all, Finland was part of the Russian Empire throughout the 19th century.

Wooden bridge.

The churches are Protestant, like in the rest of Scandinavia.

Gold autumn.

Street signs.

Bus stop.

Fast food with hamburgers from 3.50 to 5 euros.

Some people from Russia come to Finland to lose money in slot machines, which are not prohibited here.

Parikkala

Finland is very picturesque, especially if you drive from the main highway to the north, where numerous lakes begin.

The forests are especially beautiful in autumn.

Fields.

Surprisingly, many highways have bike paths, even far from big cities.
