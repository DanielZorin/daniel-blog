It differs from other Thai cities only in the shape of the largest temple - it looks like a quadrangular pyramid.

As far as I can remember, there are no other temples of this shape in Thailand.

Udon Thani

How nice is it to celebrate the New Year in Southeast Asia? It’s better not to be in Laos, where the choice of treats and entertainment is limited, so from southern Laos I moved to northeastern Thailand. Udon Thani is one of the relatively larger cities in Thailand, and it still has an expat community as there is a US military base nearby. Here you can find everything your heart desires.

The first thing you need to do is find a place to stay. You can spend the night anywhere, if it's just one night, but for a good rest you need to find a more decent place. In Thailand it can vary a lot, you may find a good hotel, but you could get a bad one too. The location in Udon Thani was simply perfect. Huge room, fast internet, refreshing air conditioning, refrigerator, hot water, freshly renovated, price $10 (350 baht per day) even on New Year's Eve - there's simply nothing to complain about.

It's called Ruysuk Guesthouse.

According to Russian tradition, on December 31st you need to set the table, at least for the two of us. Olivier salad is not sold in Udon; you need to prepare it yourself.

Tangerines, Olivier, nori chips, Pringles, cider, Song Sam, cola, pitahaya, passion fruit, strawberries, ice cream, cake.

Nong Khai

Laotian sculptor Bunleua Sulilat, having fled his homeland after the communists came to power, settled across the river from Vientiane in Nong Khai, Thailand, and continued his business of making Buddha statues. The results of his later work can be seen in Sala Keoku Park.

The craftsman's skill has improved over the years, so there is clearly better detail here.

The subject matter remained generally the same.
