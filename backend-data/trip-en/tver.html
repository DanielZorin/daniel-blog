Russia: Solnechnogorsk, Tver

27-28 July 2013

Solnechnogorsk

Solnechnogorsk is located in the Moscow region. I was there a year ago passing through, and called it “the city of millionaires” because of how fancy bus tickets looked, but this time I had a little more time to see the town. Solnechnogorsk is famous primarily for the fact that the Moscow-St. Petersburg highway passes through it, and there are always traffic jams there.

There is nothing else in Solnechnogorsk.

Tver

Tver is clearly unlucky with its leadership. While Yaroslavl, Vladimir and Kostroma have been put in order (in the center), money continues to be wasted in Tver. Guys, this is the embankment in the main city park, in the very center! Why can’t this area be completed so that it is as well-groomed as a piece of the embankment a little further away?

The center of Tver is very beautiful, but needs renovation.

Nearby stands the main modern attraction of Tver - the monument to Mikhail Krug, the most popular singer of Russian chanson (sad songs about thug life and prisons). Mikhail Krug is so popular that his portrait is even printed on souvenir refrigerator magnets.
