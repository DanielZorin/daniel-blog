Sint Maarten

13-15 November 2016

One of the most interesting Caribbean islands is divided into two parts: two thirds of the island belong to France, one third to the Netherlands, but the Dutch part is more popular, most of the infrastructure is on it. Since this is Europe, the prices here are not as outrageous as in the USA and its colonies. You can rent a hotel room for 40 dollars, i.e. comparable in prices to France and the Netherlands. I rented an entire yacht parked in the port on airbnb for $30.

The yacht from the inside. This is the wardroom.

In the nose there is a triangular-shaped bed.

There is a shower and toilet in front of it.

Electricity and water are supplied from the port and there are springs at each parking space. The yacht also has a solar battery. Even Wi-fi works if the electricity that powers the 3G access point is not cut off. By the way, the electricity was cut off every day, at such moments there was light only in the McDonald's across the road from the port, which apparently had its own generator. The owner of the yacht told me that power outages happen all the time in Sint Maarten.

Sint Maarten has its own currency - the florin (or guilder). The exchange rate is pegged to the dollar: 1 USD = 1.8 florins; you can pay in dollars everywhere. In the store near the yacht, prices are indicated only in florins. In McDonald's and many other places it is written in two currencies, with larger sign in dollars. Gouda cheese costs 10.43 florins per 485 grams.

Chicken legs cost 5 florins per kilogram, that is $2.5. Well, acceptable. If you cook, you can also live in the Caribbean; restaurants, of course, are very expensive everywhere.

The main attraction of Sint Maarten is the airport. About 6 hours before departure I went to see it on foot.
