Torzhok

Torzhok is one of the most beautiful cities in Russia, with a very large old center. This could be the second Suzdal, but Suzdal was made a tourist city back in Soviet times, and therefore was preserved, and in Torzhok they built factories right in the premises of churches , and they started putting things in order only in the 2010s, so the city still has a long way to go, but I hope everything will work out.

The train station, bus station and most businesses are located on the high bank of the Tvertsa River, in the same place as the old Moscow-St. Petersburg highway, and most of the historical center is on the other bank.

The whole trip was essentially organized in this way after I searched for transport from Ostashkov to Toropets on Yandex and was very surprised that there was a direct train between them, albeit once a day. It turns out that this railway has a cult status among train fans because it was built before the revolution, due to its remote location, old railway equipment and stations. Line Bologoe - Ostashkov - Toropets - Velikiye Luki - Polotsk was built mostly for military purposes: the French financed it to make it easier for the Russians to transport troops to the front of the First World War.

Toropets

The third and most remote historical city in the Tver region is Toropets (emphasis on the second syllable). Moscow is more than 400 kilometers away, but there are no big cities around for many kilometers: the closest ones are Tver, Smolensk, Pskov - 300 kilometers away. For some time after the war there was a separate region with the capital in Velikie Luki, but later it was split between Pskovs and Tver. Toropets is located just 20 kilometers from the Moscow-Riga highway, there are buses 3-4 times a day to Moscow (7 hours) and Tver (5 hours), trains once a day on the Velikiye Luki - Ostashkov route.

Rzhev

Finally, I stopped by the city of Rzhev - the second largest in the Tver region. This city was the scene of one of the bloodiest battles in history (according to various estimates, the number of deaths here is comparable to the Battle of Stalingrad). After the defeat near Moscow in 1941, German troops retreated and took positions in the area of ​​Rzhev and Vyazma (Smolensk region), this area was a protrusion in the front line, so the entire year 1942 Soviet troops tried to capture it. But they don’t talk about this in school, because the battle ended in nothing: on the one hand, in 1943 the Germans retreated due to defeats in the south and the threat of encirclement, on the other hand, the Red Army suffered colossal losses many times greater than the enemy, and calling it a success somehow doesn't work out. 90% of Rzhev was destroyed during the war.
