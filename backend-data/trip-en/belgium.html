Belgium: Arlon, Brussels, Ghent, Bruges, Antwerp

27-30 June 2012

Brussels

Brussels, the capital of united Europe. Rosy-cheeked, satisfied Europeans with multi-colored flags on their backpacks mingle along the wide avenues. Busy European parliamentarians leisurely stroll through beautiful parks with fountains in between working for the benefit of Europe in shiny new skyscrapers. Smart Belgian merchants boldly dump chocolate and dance the foxtrot at receptions in honor of new members of the European Union... Are you dreaming? Well, that's good. Now we can begin. So, Brussels is a huge stinking dump right in the middle of Europe.

Unfortunately it was in Belgium that I carelessly lost my camera, so I have fewer photographs from Brussels than I would like. I was in Brussels for three and a half days, from Wednesday to Saturday, but could not find any public services working. Every day I walked along the same street to my hotel, and every day I saw rows of flower beds filled with garbage. In one of them there was a mountain of spoiled bread, and nothing had changed in it for three days. The main element of the Brussels landscape is a bag of garbage standing near the wall.

Bruges

One of the reasons for my dislike towards Belgium is the artificiality of the very existence of this state. There was no Belgian nation before the 19th century when it was created as a result of political intrigues of serious powers, and before that the territory of Belgium managed to be under the rule of several different countries. The authorities of the European Union placed them in this non-state on purpose so that no one would be offended. Hence the constant political friction between Wallonia and Flanders, which, it would seem, should be impossible in such a country rich in money.

I must say, the Dutch part of Belgium is much nicer than the French part.
