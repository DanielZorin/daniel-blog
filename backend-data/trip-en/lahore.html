Lahore

Lahore is the second largest city in Pakistan and the capital of the state of Punjab. It is considered a “cultural capital”, as monuments from the era of the Mughal Empire, of which Lahore was the capital, have been preserved here.

Sukkur

I came to Sukkur with the intention of spending the night in a hotel near the station, taking a walk and then moving on to Mohenjo-Daro. However, there were no hotels near the station; I took a tuk-tuk to the area where there are hotels, and for some reason almost all of them were full, except for one, the most expensive one, where the room cost about $50. The hotel staff reported me to the police, and as a result, the next morning they started calling me from there. All calls were something like this: - As-salamu alaikum! - Wa-alaikum as-salaam - [something in Urdu] - Could you speak in English please? - [something in Urdu] - Sorry, I don't understand - [hangs up] After the fifth time I gave up and stopped responding.

Room in an expensive hotel:

The police were already waiting for me at the exit from the hotel. First they asked about my plans. They were quite satisfied with my plans to explore local attractions. After which I was put into a police car along with a security guard armed with a machine gun and taken to the local temple.

Hyderabad

I like to visit cities with the same name in different countries: - Granada (Spain) and Granada (Nicaragua) - Merida (Spain) and Merida (Mexico) - Basseterre (Saint Kitts and Nevis) and Basseterre (Guadeloupe) - Newcastle (England) and Newcastle (Australia) - Obidos (Portugal) and Obidos (Brazil) - And even Auckland (New Zealand) and Oakland (USA), although they are written differently in English, but the same in Russian. I have already been to the city Hyderabad in India. The Hyderabad pair are almost certainly the largest pair of cities with the same name by population - the Indian one has a population of almost 7 million, and the Pakistani one has a population of about 2 million. Hyderabad means "city of lions".

Hub

One of the main regions of Pakistan, Balochistan, is considered unsafe. Most often, it is crossed under armed escort by those traveling overland from the Iranian border. The main road and cities of Balochistan are closer to the Afghan border, but if you look at the map, Balochistan is huge, it is the largest region of Pakistan, and most of it is in the south along the Arabian Sea. And there is nothing there, there are practically no large cities on the coast from Karachi to the border with Iran (although there is a small city of Gwadar, which Pakistan bought from Oman back in 1958). At the same time, Balochistan begins literally just outside the city of Karachi, so I decided to mark it on the map on Nomadmania. I got into a taxi, and after about 50 minutes of driving along a poor highway with trucks, I reached the city of Hub.
