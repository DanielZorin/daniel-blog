Russia: Saint Petersburg

23-27 August 2021

Saint Petersburg

At the end of August, I went to St. Petersburg again to walk around familiar places. Little time has passed since last trip, I am pleased with the regularity. Lately, I usually fly to St. Petersburg by plane instead of taking the train, because the flight costs the same or even cheaper than a night sleeper, not to mention Sapsan. So for me, Peter now begins at the Lenin monument on Moskovskaya Square.

The hotels were pleasingly cheap; I settled in the very center on Nevsky near this church.

I have never had such a gorgeous view from a window in St. Petersburg!

Pavlovsk

Pavlovsk is the last of the cities within St. Petersburg that I did not explore (although I was there as a child in 2001-2003). Pavlovsk is literally a 10-minute drive from Pushkin and is famous primarily for the palace of the same name. The entrance to the palace park is directly opposite the station.

We find ourselves in the far part of the park with long straight alleys.
