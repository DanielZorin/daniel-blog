Russia: Tambov, Lipetsk, Yelets

25-27 December 2017

Tambov

After a pause of almost two years in traveling around the regions of Russia, my first trip, of course, was to the two regions closest to Moscow, where I had not been: Tambov and Lipetsk. First there was Tambov.

This region is one of the poorest, but I chose the right time for my visit - in winter you can’t see anything bad under the snow. Later I read a review from Varlamov and was surprised.

There are no recognizable sights known throughout the country in Tambov, but it seems that the center has been preserved quite well (perhaps because the city was behind the front line during the war).

Lipetsk

Lipetsk is very different from neighboring Tambov. This is a relatively rich region (in particular, the NLMK metallurgical plant is located here), which is immediately noticeable by the large number of new buildings and shopping centers.

First I got to the town of Gryazi ("Muds"), a suburb of Lipetsk, where the railway station is located. There is a station in the city center, but many transit trains stop only at Gryazi station.

The name of the city is certainly funny. I understand everything, the mud is probably therapeutic, but still.

There is mud here and it is not medicinal.

Yelets

The third city I visited on this trip was Yelets - one of 41 cities on the list of historical cities in Russia. As much as I was lucky with the weather in Tambov, I was just as unlucky in Yelets, where I was literally the next day. All the snow had melted, there was dirt and bare trees everywhere.
