The winter of 2019 was short for me, since on January 10 a trip to Oceania, the USA and East Asia began. But I still managed to catch both the snow and the city’s New Year’s decorations.

It’s interesting that in all of Moscow there are only 6 or 7 places where you can take exams for a driver’s license, and two of them are quite close in the Northern okrug in very inconvenient locations. Apparently, there is the highest demand for driver's licenses, since this is the largest piece of Moscow without a metro. Near Pyalovskaya Street, where I took the exam, the Seligerskaya metro station recently appeared. And international licenses are issued in even fewer places; I had to go to Lobnenskaya (Dmitrovsky district, near the Moscow Ring Road).

On March 16, 2020, I flew to Moscow from Thailand. The rules did not require "self-isolation", but just in case, I almost didn’t go out anywhere, especially since the weather was cold. At the very end of March, I managed to go get a new bank card and some other business, and a couple of days later general "self-isolation" (this is what the lockdown was called in Russian, implying that people isolate themselves on their own will) with passes began. In April 2020, I went outside my area only once. According to the pedometer, only 32,000 steps were accumulated in a month (usually 10-12 times more).

All that remained was to admire the depressive April landscapes from the balcony, appropriate to the moment.

And from time to time sunsets and clouds.
