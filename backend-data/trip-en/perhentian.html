Malaysia: Kuala Lumpur, Kuantan, Kuala Terengganu, Perhentian Islands

11-20 June 2017

Perhentian Islands

The question of where to go on holiday in Malaysia after KL was not even raised - of course, to the Perhentian Islands, to the best beaches (there are no beaches in India, so this is the only place you can recoup).

Once again I land at Coral Bay.

The hotel where I stayed last time for 60 ringit has doubled in price. Perhaps the reason is that back then March was the very beginning of the season, and now June was the peak. In the end, I settled in these bungalows on White Beach for 90 ringit, but with air conditioning. Though, the air conditioner only worked from 8 pm to 8 am, which served as a good replacement for the alarm clock - I woke up from the onset of the heat.

View from my window.

A big gecko came to visit.

White Beach is relatively crowded.

But nothing prevents you from going a little north from Coral Bay, it is still quite empty there.

They are slowly building a road from White Beach to the village where the locals live.

It's still cheaper there than on the beaches.

Village.

Children.

I was surprised that there are motorcycles in the village, although from one end to the other there is at most a kilometer, and there are no roads beyond that yet.

I reached the only beach on Small Island that I hadn’t been to last time - Romantic beach. To get here, you need to find a barely noticeable path in the jungle and go down a rather steep slope. But few people come here.

But after lunch boats can bring people here on excursions or just as taxi.

So perhaps the most secluded beach is not this one, but Isabelle beach to the north, where you need to go through the mountain in the middle of the island.

Especially if you go through the coastal cliffs, it will be quite secluded, and excursions do not land you here.

Sunsets.

In Coral Bay, a large hotel offers a dinner buffet for only 19 ringit ($5). Here you can try fried stingray, I did, but didn’t take it the second time, preferring regular fish. The stingray is edible, but not very tasty - rather rubbery, tougher than squid.

* * *

This time I had a GoPro camera with me, so I was able to take photos underwater - and the underwater world is the coolest thing in the Perhentians.

Much of this was filmed on the beaches just north of Coral Bay, as well as the previously mentioned Romantic and Isabelle. In principle, there are already a lot of interesting things on the outskirts of Coral Bay.

Different types of corals.

Tridacnas are huge shells that slam shut when you wave your hand in their direction.

Fishes.

Sea anemone, and a clown fish hiding between its tentacles.

Bottom textures.

All this is in close proximity to the shore, you don’t even need a boat.

* * *

One day I took an excursion to Greater Perhentian (costs about 7 dollars). Since the Big Island is less developed, there are even larger coral reefs and more wildlife along the coast where there are no beaches.

The schools of fish here are gigantic, and the fish are not only not afraid, but even bite, you need to constantly move.

There are not only small striped fish, but also large green-pink-blue ones.

Fishes under the raft.

Already familiar clown fish.

And this is a stingray, it was not easy to catch it because it was hiding under the stones.

Sea urchins.

Tridacna.

There are more corals here, and all types are together at once.

Coast of Greater Perhentian.

At the end there was the highlight of the program - a big turtle!

Most of the time it rested at the bottom, and the bottom there is really deep, 5-6 meters, I could dive to a maximum of three..

But periodically it surfaced.
