Kazakhstan: Nur-Sultan, Shymkent

4-8 March 2022

Nur-Sultan

On March 4, 2022, I celebrated my 32nd birthday with an overnight flight to the frosty capital of Kazakhstan. It is still called Nur-Sultan in honor of the first president, but perhaps it will be renamed back to Astana or something else.

Nur-Sultan Airport.

Nur-Sultan became the capital recently, and most of the city was built according to a regular plan, but Astana/Tselinograd was already a medium-sized city, so you can still find areas of low-rise buildings next to the wide new avenues.

Shymkent

Shymkent is the third largest city in Kazakhstan after Almaty and Nur-Sultan. Located in the south, not very far from Almaty (and very close to Tashkent)

The Soviet-style Shymkent airport is small and cramped.

Monument at the entrance.

Shymkent is a real Central Asia, I vividly remembered my long trip to this region in 2013. The atmosphere, architecture are both similar to neighboring countries.

There are some oddities, for example, for some reason in Shymkent there are English telephone booths.
