I wanted to go to the observation deck at Vysotsky, but again, like last time, I couldn’t - on the day I was going to do it, there was a blizzard and poor visibility.

Nizhny Tagil

Nizhny Tagil is an industrial city that has become a kind of meme because of the TV series “Our Russia” (itself an adaptation of "Little Britain"). There is also Verkhniy (Upper) Tagil, which is 30 times smaller than Nizhny Tagil and does not have passenger rail service, while Nizhny (Lower) Tagil is now even served by high-speed Lastochka trains.

I love traveling to Nizhny Tagil.

Nizhny Tagil welcomes you with public transport, represented mainly by these coffins on wheels, which you need to climb into while bending over. Previously, I had encountered such inconvenient transport only in the outback of Indonesia.

Having ridden this nightmare once, then I basically only rode trams, even if it was longer.

Nizhny Tagil is quite large in area. There are many large factories here, and in Soviet times they tried to build residential areas closer to them. Therefore, there is such a layout - a factory, and next to it there is a lot of houses, it turns out to be an agglomeration of several cities. From the station I traveled 7-8 kilometers by minibus; half the way there was a forest on one side and a factory on the other. As a result, I came to Uralvagonzavod.

Oldfags remember how in 2011 a worker from this plant promised Putin to come to Moscow to disperse the rallies. This worker was later made a plenipotentiary representative in the Ural Federal District. But those times are in the past.

Verkhoturye

The main goal of this trip to the north was the city of Verkhoturye - one of the few remaining unvisited historical cities in Russia. This is one of the oldest cities in the Urals (founded in 1598); initially, the main road to Siberia went through it.

From Nizhny Tagil it takes three to four hours to get here by train. View along the way:

Verkhoturye is a very small city, less than 10 thousand inhabitants. But the station is located just outside the city; you need to travel 7 kilometers by bus. I wanted to take a taxi (so as not to wait), but I couldn’t - I couldn’t find one. I called all the numbers that I found on the Internet, and the phones that were posted in advertisements at the bus stop, but all of them did not answer, either they no longer belonged to taxis, or they belonged to taxis in another city. About 40 minutes later the bus arrived.

I left a little earlier than the center, near the Lenin monument.
