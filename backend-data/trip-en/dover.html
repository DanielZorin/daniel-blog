United Kingdom: Dover, Canterbury

8 December 2019

Dover

Dover is the place where ferries go from England to France, and there are also white chalk cliffs desrrving the visit, even though they are lower than Cliffs of Moher.

The cliffs start almost immediately behind the city center and stretch for quite a long distance along the coast.

There are many ruins of old fortress walls on and around them.

The entrance to the ferry terminal is directly under the rock, to the left of it a small narrow staircase leads up.

France is visible on the horizon in the fog.

The path gradually rises to the top of the coastal cliffs.

In the distance you can see a fortress on a nearby cliff.

Promenade along the tops of the rocks. A little higher there is a more equipped path, and at some point this one split in two, and the lower part was difficult to pass, I slipped on the clay and hurt myself a little on the thorns, so I had to go back a little.

Canterbury

On the way from Dover, I decided to stop in neighboring Canterbury, which is only 20-30 minutes away. The main cathedral of the Anglican Church is located here.

The cathedral was under renovation, and the entrance fee was inhumane, so I didn’t go inside.

It is interesting that the cathedral is surrounded on all sides by administrative buildings; the façade is not visible behind them.

Only the main gate.

Cathedral towers in the distance.

The outer walls of the city have been preserved in many places.

Northern Gate.

At one of the entrances you can see the remains of the castle.

They are in such a dangerous condition that access to the inside has been closed.
