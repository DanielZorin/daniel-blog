Trinidad and Tobago

17-18 November 2016

Trinidad is the southernmost of the Lesser Antilles, and one of the largest. From here it’s a stone’s throw to the mainland; there used to be regular ferries to Venezuela, although it’s not a fact that they still work now due to serious problems in this country. There is oil in Trinidad, and there is almost no tourism, all beach lovers go straight to neighboring Tobago (which, by the way, was for some time a colony of Latvia, more precisely Courland). The capital of Trinidad, Port of Spain, is considered the third most dangerous city in the Caribbean after Port-au-Prince (Haiti) and Kingston (Jamaica).

Upon arrival, it is immediately clear that you are in a banana republic. Right at the airport there is a long row of portraits of the nation's fathers.

There is no European gloss visible in the American and European colonies that I have been to up to this point.
