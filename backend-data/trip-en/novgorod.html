Russia: Veliky Novgorod, Staraya Russa, Staraya Ladoga

21-24 August 2012

Veliky Novgorod

You can’t understand Russia with your mind, you can’t measure it with a common yardstick - F. Tyutchev Something completely unimaginable is happening in this city. Judge for yourself.

We walk down the street and see a pretentious panel.

Bah, this is a factory named after the Lenin Komsomol! We are going the right way, comrades!

We walk further down the street and see, what would you think? Church from 1360. There is nothing left at all from Moscow of those times.

Almost opposite is a newer building - a church from 1381. To enhance the effect, the Pravda newspaper with a photograph of Comrade Stalin hangs on the church fence.

Let's move a little away from the main street. Welcome to the deep forests.

There are also churches in the middle of the forests, and what churches!

Next to the church, in the middle of dense forests, there is an exemplary lilac garden.

Behind this area begin forests and fields in the most literal sense of these words. Where the Volkhov merges with the Small Volkhovtsev, and it is not possible to go further, a spring gushes from underground, water flows in a stream from the hillock and flows into the Volkhov through a wild beach, it is completely unclear how it appeared here.

And here is the source itself.

Because of the vast fields, the Kremlin can be seen in the distance. Let's go back.

Staraya Russa

A very sad place, one might say, a typical Russian outback. Once it was a resort with mineral waters, but when railways were built in the 19th century, everyone began to go to Pyatigorsk, and Staraya Russa fell into disrepair.

Immediately after leaving the station the landscape looks something like this.

Rusty mailboxes, an indispensable attribute of devastation.

The main street is somewhat subtly reminiscent of Jakarta. Probably because of a white house with a light brown roof.

Another typical Jakarta landscape.

Typical wall.

In the whole city, two buildings have been properly restored. Main church...

...and the house where Fyodor Dostoevsky lived. The city is famous for being the prototype for the setting of The Brothers Karamazov.

Staraya Ladoga

Lost among the vast northern plains is one of the oldest settlements in Russia.

Ladoga is at least 1259 years old.

Nominally this is not a city, but a village. There is only one bus.

Everything is named after ancient Russian princes.

One of them, Prophetic Oleg, is allegedly buried outside the city.

People come here from all surrounding towns to celebrate their weddings.

To attract more tourists, the fortress is being completed. You can see what it looked like before restoration inside.

There is also a monastery in Ladoga.

It is notable primarily for the design of the garden. It's not just well-groomed.

There are also life-size figurines of various animals placed here and there.

There are also a couple of interesting structures on the territory of the monastery.

The rest is pure beauty.

Such is life.
