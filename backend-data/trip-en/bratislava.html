Slovakia: Bratislava

27 May 2012

Bratislava

What is the difference between Czechia and Slovakia? In the Czech Republic, the person at the pedestrian crossing sign wears a jacket and hat, and in Slovakia, sweatpants and a cap.

After the gold of Vienna, Bratislava returns to harsh reality.

Good old Soviet style.

Buildings that have not been painted for a long time.

This is all, of course, not in the historical center, but on the way to it from the station. Everything is fine in the center.

Fountains flow at the presidential palace.

The roofs have patterns similar to Viennese ones.

Here is the building in which the Peace of Pressburg was signed after the Battle of Austerlitz (Bratislava is called Pressburg in German, and Pozsony in Hungarian).

Tentacles from underground.

Three girls and a ball.

Monument to Medusa the Gorgon.

"Monument" to the glove.

Suddenly, a monument to Andersen.

A cunning cook who can be found in other countries of central Europe.

Painted wall in the center.

The cry of the soul on the way to the hill. By the way, I was actually wearing a suit at that moment.

The castle where the kings of Hungary ascended the throne.

A view of the city from above, I’ve already seen this somewhere: red roofs, a spire, and so on.

But here is a completely different view of the city, the opposite bank of the Danube, with residential areas. I still had some time left and free travel on public transport, so I took the first bus across the bridge for a few stops.

On buses there is a request to remove backpacks from your shoulders.

On the outskirts, the following picture opened before my eyes.

Sticker at the bus stop.

After wandering among the blocks, I came to the conclusion that this place is similar to decent residential areas of Moscow, not even necessarily with new buildings. For example, Medvedkovo now looks about the same.

Some houses are still gray commercial blocks.

But local authorities are fighting this with bright colors. Bratislava left a rather positive impression; it is clear that the city is changing for the better.
