Puerto Rico: San Juan

12-13 November 2016

Puerto Rico has belonged to the United States since 1898, when the Americans took it from Spain (along with Philippines). They still speak Spanish there, but they dream of becoming the 51st state. But Congress is in no hurry, because if Puerto Rico becomes a state, it will immediately top the rankings of the poorest states, and at the same time will reduce per capita GDP in the country.

The neighboring small islands have heavenly beaches.

In the depths are tropical forests.

This is the capital, the city of San Juan.

It is the oldest city in the USA by a wide margin. If we take only full-fledged states, then the oldest city will be Saint Augustine in Florida.

San Juan is quite large, there is even a metro there, but the tourist part is only a long and narrow cape, which was originally an island.

Transportation in Puerto Rico is very, very bad. In the USA, in comparison, everything is simply divine. There is no intercity transport on the island at all. There are only “collective taxis”, like in third world countries, except their prices are more similar to the prices of first class trains in Switzerland. There are city buses in San Juan. The official website proudly promises buses from the airport every 10-15 minutes, in reality I waited for more than an hour. On the way back, the bus did not come, and I had to spend almost $20 on a taxi (for a 6 kilometers drive). Taxis in Puerto Rico are all giant jeeps, that fit a whole family of five people and ten suitcases who come here on vacation, no less.

Puerto Ricans are diligently learning to be stingy from their American overlords.

Prices are about the same as on the mainland.

The hostel was outside the old city, but it took me about an hour to get there on foot. The area is also quite nice.
