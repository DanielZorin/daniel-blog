Hexham

Near Newcastle was Hadrian's Wall, a wall that the Romans built across northern England to protect themselves from Scottish raids. Of course, this is the prototype of the Wall in Game of Thrones. The wall ran through what is now the center of Newcastle, but nothing of it survives there. The problem was that all the sites were somewhere in the countryside, and the bus specially for tourists along the wall stopped running at the end of September. Not a single railway station is located closer than 5-6 kilometers from the wall. In the end, I decided to visit the small town of Heddon (Heddon on the Wall) very close to Newcastle, which can be reached by commuter bus. Heddon is a real English village.

South of Newcastle is the city of Durham, famous for its cathedral, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The facade of the cathedral faces the cliff and the river; you can only see it from afar from the other bank, since the entrance is on the side. And here is the back part, where the altar is.

The cathedral was built at the end of the 11th century, it is one of the earliest examples of Gothic architecture, so from the outside it is not quite typically Gothic. There were more later changes inside, so the inside is very gothic.

Newark

Due to pricing, it was cheaper to break up a trip from York to London with a stop in the middle in Newark-on-Trent. The much larger American Newark, a suburb of New York, is named after this city. This is the East Midlands region, there are no particularly large cities here. Not far from Newark is Sherwood Forest, where, according to legend, Robin Hood lived. The train station here is small, but with a nice wooden decoration on the platform canopy and a clock.
