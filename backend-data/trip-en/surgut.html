Russia: Surgut, Noyabrsk

7-8 November 2019

Surgut

The city of oil workers, Surgut, is probably not the most popular place for a weekend trip, but Pobeda flies there cheaply, and I have already visited almost all of the nearby cities. Surgut is not the capital of the Khanty-Mansiysk Autonomous Okrug (a.k.a. Yugra), but it is the largest city, there is also a railway here, and to the regional center you need to travel 5 hours by bus.

I love snowy winters and hate rainy ones, as usually happens in Moscow. So it was great to cool off at -16 degrees.

Surgut is located on the banks of the Ob River (as is Novosibirsk)

The river is not frozen, and carries huge ice floes downstream, which, crashing into the shore, lose their edges with a characteristic crack sound.

As a result, piles of broken ice are formed. The spectacle is fascinating.

Here on the shore there is a monument to the victims of repression.

In the center of Surgut there is a small open-air museum where old wooden houses and a church are collected.

Walking around the city, I also discovered a non-museum old area with two-story buildings, which are now usually called “barracks”.

Noyabrsk

I visited the Yamalo-Nenets Autonomous Okrug a bit cheatingly: Noyabrsk, one of the two largest cities in the region, is located in the very south right on the border with Yugra and is easily accessible by rail. Along the way there are endless snow-covered forests of Western Siberia.

If you ride from Surgut all night, you can get to the final passenger station Novy Urengoy - this is the largest city in the region, the capital of gas production. The capital of the Yamal-Nenets Autonomous Okrug, Salekhard, is half the size of Novy Urengoy and Noyabrsk, but is also accessible by rail through Komi, although the ride is very long. Pobeda does not fly to any city in the Yamal-Nenets Autonomous Okrug.

There are two stations in Noyabrsk, Noyabrsk-2 in the city center and Noyabrsk-1 far on the southern outskirts. The train stops at Noyabrsk-1 for so long that it is often easier to get off there and take a minibus to the center.
