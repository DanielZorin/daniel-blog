USA: Las Vegas, Grand Canyon

2-5 June 2018

Las Vegas

I was in Las Vegas in 2014, but it was very short. So this time I explored this city properly. For Americans, Las Vegas is a sacred cow, a place where a self-respecting American should go so that it is expensive, luxurious and everything is like other people do. Tell an American that you lived off the strip in Las Vegas for $20 a day and rode a bus - the reaction will be about the same as telling a Ukrainian that Crimea is Russia, or a Russian that Crimea is Ukraine. Old films gave Vegas the image of a sort of nest of debauchery (What happens in Vegas stays in Vegas), but in reality it has long been turned into a resort for retirees and families with children. And who in their right mind would go to Vegas for prostitutes if one “session” there costs more than plane tickets to Thailand? Weed is also legal in a bunch of other states. However, Las Vegas is not at all threatened with desolation, local businessmen are not stupid, and they understand that debauchery will not get you far, so there are a huge number of concerts, festivals and conferences here - the place is perfect, there are a lot of huge hotels concentrated in one place, they can accommodate guests of any event.

Las Vegas has the cheapest hotels in the USA; here you can rent a regular motel room for $20 (in all other places this will cost at least $50), but only on weekdays. On Saturday and Sunday nights, prices skyrocket several times, and my first day was just Saturday, so I stayed in a hostel and then moved. The hostel was located in "old" Las Vegas.

Interesting fact: the airport and most of the attractions in Las Vegas are technically located in the city of Paradise. Not all tourists even visit nominal Las Vegas, although there is an old strip there.

Grand Canyon

From Las Vegas to the Grand Canyon it is about 400 kilometers, so a trip there in one day is quite long. The Grand Canyon is one of the most famous and visited national parks in the United States. The canyon is huge, and getting across it is not so easy, so you have to choose which side to approach it from. The vast majority of visitors to the canyon are from the south side, from Arizona, rather than from the north side, where the border with Utah is located.

The journey begins with a very early morning departure from Las Vegas. The suburbs here don't seem so deserted.

Not far from Vegas will be the city of Boulder, followed by the Arizona border and the Hoover Dam.

The Hoover Dam was built between 1931 and 1936, at the height of the Great Depression. It blocked the Colorado River, creating the artificial Lake Mead Reservoir, and contributed to the irrigation of lands in these parts.

Lake Mead.
