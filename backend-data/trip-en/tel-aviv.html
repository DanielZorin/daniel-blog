Haifa

To the south of the city center of Haifa there were areas populated by local Arabs. When Israeli independence was declared in 1948 and war began, the Arabs packed up and moved away from Israel. As in any self-respecting country at war, in Israel history is covered with a thick layer of propaganda, so that it is now difficult to establish to what extent the Arabs were "helped" to leave. Perhaps not at all. For example, if I found myself in a war surrounded by enemies, I would first of all move away from the fighting, regardless of the loss of housing. In any case, Israel won more than one war, and the truth is on the winning side, no matter what the losers say about universal injustice. The result of this is that there is now a real ghost town with destroyed houses right next to the center of Haifa.

Like any abandoned place in the center of a big city, the Wadi Salib quarter is first and foremost a garbage dump.

The broken windows theory is at work: the windows here are broken in the most literal sense, so throwing trash here is a nice thing to do.

It can be seen that this used to be an Arab city, similar in layout to Jerusalem or Nazareth, with narrow passages and multi-tiered streets.

Now it is already overgrown with grass.

Modern residential buildings and offices are being built around abandoned houses where possible.

Most houses are fenced with barbed wire. There are a lot of drug addicts and homeless people hanging out here anyway.

There is some kind of tent right under the fence.

There is an opinion that this is being done to create a semblance of legality, so that the owners still hold the private property rights, although in practice their chances of getting through the border are zero. But this is a strange explanation; Israel has enough military power and influence to influence the opinion of Arab countries, especially on such an issue of little relevance now as the homes of people who fled 70 years ago.

Rosh Hanikra

Rosh Hanikra is a village on the border between Israel and Lebanon. The border has long been tightly closed, since Israel and Lebanon are at war.

Before the independence of Israel, when these lands were colonies of England and France, for some time there was even a railway from Istanbul to Cairo itself. The railway tunnel still remains in Rosh Hanikra.

This village in Israel is famous for its white rocks and grottoes.

You can take a funicular ride.

Coast of Israel.

Lebanon is over this hill.

Rosh Hanikra is a kibbutz, that is, an agricultural commune. This kibbutz is also notable for the fact that the famous blogger puerrtto lives somewhere here.

I liked Israel (and Palestine) in everything, except for the terrible high prices. On departure at the airport I prepared for a hellish search and interrogation, but there was nothing terrible. They asked me what I was doing in Morocco, Indonesia, UAE and Jordan. I didn’t check in my luggage, so they didn’t rummage through my things. To avoid digging through your belongings, you need to remember to put all electronic devices, including charging cords, in your carry-on luggage.
