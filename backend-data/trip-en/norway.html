Drammen

I arrived in Oslo at 5 am, and, horrified by the number of multicultural emigrants at the central station, splurged on $20 and bought a train ticket to Drammen, which takes half an hour. The city of Drammen did not impress me with the colorful balls.

I wasn’t impressed by the wavy bench either.

Not impressed with the signs.

And a view of the main street.

But I was impressed by the openness and friendliness of the local ghost residents, who not only don’t lock the doors to the barn, but don’t even have doors at all.

I was impressed by the broken asphalt and puddles on the sidewalk.

And gasoline prices almost $3 per liter.

But overall it’s a normal city.

There is also free Wi-Fi throughout the city, the password for which can be obtained via SMS.

It's time to put aside fear and return to the capital of Norway.
