Italy: Palermo, Cefalu, Messina, Reggio Calabria, Catania, Syracuse

15-19 August 2024

Palermo

I've wanted to visit Sicily for a long time, but somehow it never worked out. This island was one of the most significant blank spots on my map of Europe (now, probably, the Norwegian fjords and western Ukraine remain). Sicily has a slightly "dangerous" reputation because of the mafia, but of course it's quiet here (except that your wallet can be stolen). But this is southern Italy, so it's not as rich, not as clean, not as well organized.

The first funny thing I noticed right at the Palermo airport: there is a shared taxi! Where else in Europe can you find something like this? Even in the Balkans it's a rarity. Though, it is also regulated here, but taxi drivers-barkers do stand at the exit from the airport. Getting to the center costs 8 euros per person, a couple of euros more than by bus or train.

Sicily is hillier than Sardinia. There are even really high mountains, like Mount Etna.

Cefalu

The small town of Cefalu is an hour's drive from Palermo. It was on the way, so I went out for a walk.

The Cathedral is a World Heritage Site (combined with the center of Palermo).

You can freely enter it from the street, unlike many churches in Palermo.

The frescoes are similar to the painting of Orthodox cathedrals, this is due to the influence of Byzantium, which controlled Sicily and southern Italy for a long time.

Syracuse

Syracuse is a city in the south of Sicily. I remembered from school that Archimedes lived in Syracuse, but I thought it was in Greece. But then Sicily was just one of the main Greek colonies. It is easier to get to Syracuse from Catania by bus, as they leave every 15 minutes.

The historical center of Syracuse is located on the small island of Ortigia, which can be reached by a bridge.

A monument to Archimedes.
