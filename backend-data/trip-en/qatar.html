Qatar

23-24 August 2023

Doha

I was in Qatar on a layover at the airport back in 2011 (on the way to Indonesia), but never visited the country itself. Unlike Bahrain and UAE, low-cost airlines do not fly to Qatar; tickets from neighboring countries are absurdly expensive, and the cheapest way to get here is a long stopover with Qatar Airways. This airline is usually quite expensive, but for some destinations their tickets are, on the contrary, some of the cheapest, for example to Pakistan. I took the longest connection I could - 23 hours, and here I'm finally in Qatar.

Not long ago the FIFA World Cup took place in Qatar. Thanks to it public transport has been seriously improved. In 2019, subway was opened in Qatar, and it already has three lines.

The subway is cheap - one trip is 2 rials ($0.60), unlimited rides for the whole day cost 6 rials (less than $2). In Dubai, one trip costs an average of $2. At the same time, the metro in Dubai is constantly crowded, while in Qatar it is quite free.

Transfer hub.

Another benefit of the championship is that a lot of hotels opened for it, and now accomodation is relatively cheap in Qatar: I got such a luxurious room for only $33.

As in any Muslim country, there was a Koran in the room, but here it was translated into English.

The hotel area is literally one metro station from the city center.

Like Dubai, Doha has two centers - a historical one and a modern one with skyscrapers. The historical center is between Msheireb and Souq Waqif stations.

The Msheireb metro area is a new business district, built just a few years ago. In terms of architecture, this place is very atypical for Arab countries - rather it resembles new areas somewhere in England or Australia are designed .

In fact, it's very similar to Australia in tone. The area is mostly pedestrian and there is a tram line (it doesn't seem to be running yet or runs infrequently).

Fountains on the sidewalk.

Next comes the Waqif market, this is supposedly the oldest part of the city (well, the middle of the 20th century). This is an analogue of the old part of Dubai.

Doha is similar to Dubai in many ways, but there are a lot fewer tourists and people in general. In Dubai, for example, Deira is crowded in the evening; it’s more spacious here for now.

As far as I understand, there are much fewer options to move to Qatar compared to the UAE. The vacancies for construction and service workers from Pakistan/India are the same, but for businessmen, investors, freelancers and simply employees of international companies, there are much fewer jobs.
