Ellora Caves

Ellora Caves are rock-cut Buddhist and Hindu temples from the 6th to 11th centuries, a World Heritage Site. Getting there is easy from the city of Aurangabad, about an hour by bus. Entrance, as usual, costs 500 rupees.

Directly opposite the entrance is the main and largest Kailash temple, entirely carved out of rock.

This entire structure is carefully carved from the rock under the cliff of which I am standing.

Inside the temple.

There are a total of 34 caves in the complex: 12 Buddhist, 5 Jain and 17 Hindu. The oldest ones are Buddhist.

The first caves are small and quite empty inside.

Buddha statues stand in dark corners.

Then multi-level temples begin.

What stands out here is the "carpenter's cave", in which the ceiling looks as if it was made of wooden beams.

Hindu temples, apart from the main one, are small.

The Jain temples are located at a distance from the rest; it still takes about 20 minutes to get there.

Silvassa

The Union Territory of Dadra and Nagar Haveli is another remnant of the Portuguese colonies in India. Unlike Daman and Goa, which were held for more than 10 years, this piece was lost by Portugal a little earlier. The fact is that there is no connection with this territory by sea - it is slightly inland. From 1954 to 1961, after the overthrow of the colonial administration, this territory was formally independent! Although in reality, of course, it was completely dependent on India. Initially, such a piece of land detached from the sea came to the Portuguese because the Maratha Empire in the 18th century gave them the right to collect taxes from 70 villages in the area under a peace treaty, and after the collapse of this empire, the lands went to Portugal in their entirety. Dadra and Nagar Haveli were administered from Daman. The territory consists of the small enclave village of Dadra and a larger piece to the south, with the largest city of Silvassa.

Silvassa was a small town without much strategic importance, so there is little left of Portugal here, not like in Goa. Few people came here from Europe to live. This is what the main cathedral of Silvassa looks like:
