UK: London, Windsor, Brighton

28 July - 11 August 2001

London

In the summer of 2001 I visited the UK for the first time. This trip was a reward for winning a school Olympiad (I don’t remember exactly which one now). We were a large group of about 30 such winners. We lived in a small village near the town of Dorking, south of London - there, at the boarding school in the summer, they organized something like an international camp for learning English. Besides us, there were many children and teenagers from different countries: a large group from Taiwan, much like ours, and children from different European countries who came there individually. Every day there were English classes in the morning, and in the evening all sorts of games and cultural events. Box Hill School is located in a Victorian mansion.

There were several trips from school: for half a day we went to the pool in the nearest city, and to the bowling club. There were also three full-fledged excursions: to the center of London, to Windsor Castle and to the city of Brighton. The trip to London was quite short. First we drove up to the Tower.

One of these photos is the very first photo I took, but I'm not exactly sure which one.

From there we sailed along the Thames by boat. The view from the Tower has changed a lot since then - now there are no high-rise buildings on the left, but there is a skyscraper called The Shard.
