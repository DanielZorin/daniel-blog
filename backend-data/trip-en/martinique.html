Martinique

4-5 May 2022

Martinique is a French island between Dominica and Saint Lucia. To avoid unnecessary Covid tests, I first went to Dominica and then back to Martinique. Along the way I could see the entire island.

Martinique is a mountainous volcanic island.

The southern part is less populated.

There are more cities in the north.

The main volcano is called Montagne Pelée, it destroyed the previous capital of the island about 100 years ago.

Now the capital is in the center of the island, called Fort-de-France.

Port.

An old fortress on the coast, still occupied by the army.

Fort-de-France looks more impressive than most Caribbean towns with wooden houses.

In total, Martinique has almost 400 thousand inhabitants.

The architecture is like in France on the outskirts, or like in Tahiti.

In addition to regular buses, Martinique has two lines of high-speed buses that travel on dedicated roads and ring like trams.

You can take this bus to the airport.

After sunset at 6 pm the city completely dies out, neither restaurants nor shops are open.

Outside the capital, I went to the city of Saint-Pierre in the north of the island. It is the former capital.

In 1902 it was destroyed by a volcanic eruption. Some houses have old photographs hanging on them.
