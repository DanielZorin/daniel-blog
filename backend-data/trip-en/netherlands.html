Trains in the Netherlands are painted yellow and blue. The train is the main mode of transport in the country; buses complement the trains, allowing access to all villages. It is possible to travel between cities by bus (there is even a very cheap bus ticket for the whole day), but this requires an unrealistic number of transfers, for example, Rotterdam-Amsterdam by direct train will take 40 minutes, by bus - about 8 transfers, several hours of travel. Tickets for trains are sold in vending machines that only accept coins (the locals don’t care, they have a travel card like the Moscow “Troika” for all types of transport); at the ticket office, tickets are a little more expensive.

The Hague

Holland is so small that here children's playgrounds are built on the roofs of one-story houses, and trees are planted in narrow passages.

Dutch cities are so serious that street cleaners only sweep rose petals from the sidewalk.

The Dutch are so romantic that they even decorate their bicycles with flowers.

Nothing surprising.

The capital of the Netherlands is Amsterdam, but the government and royal palace are in The Hague.

The Hague Tribunal, where war criminals are tried, is also located here.

Next to the tribunal there is an interesting installation of stones brought from each country of the world.

As in other cities of the Netherlands, the streets of The Hague are given over to exhibitions of conceptual sculptures.

Amsterdam

What is Amsterdam usually associated with? 1. Drugs 2. Gays 3. Prostitutes. Well, let's take a closer look at these three pillars.

There are not only gay bookstores, but also gay movie stores.

This place is famous as the world's first sexually tolerant cafe.

Now that area can safely be called the main area for LGBT hangouts. It is located east of the red light district.

At the same time, this is a small Amsterdam "Brussels".

From gays we smoothly flow to sex shops.

Excellent shop. They even invented a word - “condomerie”.

And we smoothly move to the red light district.

Strange place. 95% of people go there to gawk. At night, I even saw a tour group of Russian-speaking women of Balzac’s age and grandmothers who also decided to look at prostitutes. However, someone periodically sneaks inside the doors. And this despite the outrageous prices of 50 euros!

In general, Amsterdam is a completely unsuitable place for sex tourism. In Thailand it is much cheaper, and if you don’t like Asian girls, you can go to Ukraine/Russia/Moldova and other countries of Eastern Europe, where most Amsterdam prostitutes come from.

In addition to slender young girls, there are also offers for gentlemen with other tastes: African women, plump women, older ladies, and so on.

Finally, about drugs, more precisely about psychotropic substances (drugs like heroin are illegal in the Netherlands, as elsewhere). The situation with them is complicated, as I understand it, the legal status is very complex, on the one hand, marijuana does not seem to be sold to just anyone, on the other hand, its use and cultivation for personal purposes is not prosecuted.

The government has long been trying to close coffee shops to foreigners, which has already been done in the border areas, where “tourists” from Belgium and Germany came.

The most famous pop coffee shop, on the edge of the red light district.

Cannabis seeds are completely legal and are sold at the famous Amsterdam flower market.

In general, the problem with Amsterdam is that most tourists come here to get drunk and smoke marijuana. I've seen places with crowds of tourists, but nowhere has the average age been as low as in Amsterdam. The main street is filled with crowds of schoolchildren from all over Europe. Prices for beds in hostels here are sky high. Because of all this, the beautiful city has such a dubious reputation as northern Ibiza.
