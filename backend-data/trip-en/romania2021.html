Constanța

Seven years since last trip to Romania I wanted to come to this magical country again, but everything was somehow postponed. A good trip is like wine - it must mature, apparently. So now next time is in a few more years. This was the second time I entered with adventures. In 2014, I had to hitch a ride to cross a bridge at the border and then take a taxi to Bucharest. Here it turned out even more fun - the bus from Varna to Constanta stupidly didn’t come (or departed much earlier, so I didn’t see it). What to do, I had to take a taxi to the border (65 euros). There was a huge line of cars and buses (tourist, not regular) at the border; it was nice to walk past them straight to the border guards’ window.

Sibiu

From Bucharest to Sibiu it is approximately 5-6 hours by bus or train. Arriving at the station, I was surprised to find that there were no tickets for the train, because there were no seats - I had never encountered this before in Europe, usually all the trains run half empty. I had to rush to the bus station and take a bus (more precisely, a minibus). The bus route is different - if the train goes first north through Brasov and then west, the bus takes a more direct route through the city of Ramnicu Valcea, and then climbs into the mountains along the Olt River valley. These are very beautiful places - the old Făgăraș highway. Under Ceausescu, a parallel Transfăgărașan road was built several tens of kilometers to the east. Now that road is considered one of the most beautiful in Europe, but this one is also good.

Alba Iulia

Alba Iulia is a walled city in Transylvania where you can see 18th-century star-shaped fortifications, with bastions and ravelins. In this it is similar to the Portuguese city of Elvas

The first thing you encounter near the bus station is a cemetery. Romanian cemeteries are similar to others in Europe, with massive slabs, stone crosses and crypts.

Timişoara

Drobeta-Turnu Severin

Romania's southern border with Serbia and Bulgaria runs for quite a long time along the banks of the great Danube River. The last time I came from Bulgaria was on the bridge over the Danube between Ruse and Giurgiu, but there is another bridge connecting Romania with Serbia, further to the west. There the Danube passes through a picturesque gorge, and you can also take a train there on the way from Timişoara to Bucharest.

The nearest large town, about 20 kilometers downstream, is called Drobeta-Turnu Severin. Why such a strange long name? The city was founded during the times of ancient Rome and was named Drobeta, there was even a bridge over the Danube, which collapsed in the Middle Ages. Then, on the site of Drobeta, they founded a new settlement, which was called the “Northern Tower”, or maybe the “Tower of Septimius Severus” (Roman Emperor). Then the two names were combined.
