Salisbury

As a decent Russian tourist, when I arrived in England, the first thing I did was go to look at the spires in Salisbury. A Russian defector who escaped to the UK and lived in Salisbury was poisoned in 2018 with Novichok poison. Two Russian agents were caught on cameras in London, Salisbury and on the train. After that there was an interview on Russian TV with them, where the two guys ridiculously explained that they were just tourists who wanted to see the spire of Salisbury cathedral, so they came to the UK for 1 day just for that. They also rented a very cheap hotel room with one bed, so there were a lot of gay jokes about "spires" after that interview.

All thoughts are only about the spire...

Seriously, the cathedral is worth a look, it’s Gothic from the 13th century. Unfortunately, I didn't have time to go inside - it closes early.

Cardiff

Cardiff is the capital of Wales, one of the four constituent parts of Great Britain. Unlike Scotland, which was independent for a long time, and after unification was perceived as a separate state with a common king with England, Wales was annexed already in the 13th century. On the other hand, the local language has been preserved significantly better than Scottish: 20% of the residents of Wales know Welsh (only 1% know Scottish in Scotland). Wales has no particularly famous landmarks, and outside the kingdom it is remembered mainly in connection with the football championships, where teams from all parts of the UK are separate.

Welsh is an unusual language and boasts tricky spelling, much like Irish.

Bath

The ancient city of Bath in the west of England is included in the UNESCO list. It was founded in the Roman era on hot springs, hence the name.

The entire city consists of sand-colored houses.

This style from the 18th century is called Georgian architecture - then in England there were four successive kings named George.

Two circular buildings stand out in particular - the royal crescent and the Circus (circle).

The Circus

Throughout the city there are rows of such houses with characteristic palisades of chimneys.

There are Roman baths in the central square.

You can't swim there now, you just have to stand in line.

Bristol

Bristol is a port city in the west of England. For some reason, in Russia they like to name shops and hotels after it, so even if you enter “Bristol” in Russian into Google, the first link will be to a chain of alcohol stores (interestingly, in England the order of results in Google is different, and the Wikipedia article about the city comes first).

Bristol Castle was destroyed in the 17th century; now its remains can be seen in a large park in the city center.

On the outskirts of Bristol is Clifton Bridge, one of the largest suspension bridges of the 19th century.
