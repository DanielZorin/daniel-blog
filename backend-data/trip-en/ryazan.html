Russia: Ryazan

3-4 November 2014

Ryazan

They say that in Ryazan there are mushrooms with eyes.

They sell goodies in Ryazan.

Although entertainment is mainly provided by shopping centers, as elsewhere in Russia.

I was already in Ryazan in 2001. From that visit I remember the hostel of the hotel type "Teacher", which is now a hotel, and dining room number 5, where they served the most inedible food I have ever tasted. There were no pedestrian zones then.

The fate of the Lenin monument on the main square is interesting. In October 1993, at the peak of Yeltsin’s “revolution,” it was demolished, but in the late 1990s it was restored to its original location.

There are other monuments to Lenin here.

This one is especially good. Without knowing in advance, you won’t guess who it is with the curly head.

There is a Kremlin in Ryazan.

There are no walls, only ramparts remain on which you can walk freely.

There is significant devastation around the Kremlin.

In some places people are still living in barracks; the authorities promise to resettle them within a year.

Social advertising at bus stops.

Belarus became a great maritime power.

We are building a garden city.

I didn’t notice a single Uzbek in Ryazan, however, a Russian march is also held here.

Street sign.

Even minibuses are somehow displayed on the electronic board.

Wires.

From time to time you come across some incredibly nice architecture.

Ryazan has its own Shibuya intersection, when the green light turns on for pedestrians in all directions at once.

A bridge collapsed near Auchan, and a new one of crazy shape is being built.

The restaurant of a potential enemy, it’s unclear how vigilant citizens haven’t closed it yet?

And in Ryazan there is the best hotel for 1200 rubles that I have encountered in Russia.
