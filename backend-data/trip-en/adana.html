And from Beirut on the date I needed there were no flights to Antalya, but a direct one to Adana - I took it and decided to get from there by land, seeing interesting cities along the way. Tarsus

The small city of Tarsus between Adana and Mersin boasts that it is the oldest city in Turkey with a history of more than 5,000 years.

You can come here by train from Adana.

Ataturk's train.

Right from the doorstep we are greeted by an unusual monument to Ataturk.

The signature says the following. One day Ataturk arrived in Tarsus and was greeted by crowds of residents. Suddenly the woman threw herself at his feet and asked: “I want to sacrifice myself on the ground on which you walk!” Ataturk helped her get up and replied: “Dear! You deserve not to fall to the ground, but to hold it on your shoulders.”

Kızkalesi

I arrived in the town of Kızkalesi towards sunset. Initially I planned to spend the night in the larger Silifke, 20 km away, but it turned out that there are a lot of hotels in Kızkalesi, and in November they were all empty, I checked into the first large hotel near the beach for only 300 liras, from the window there was a gorgeous view of the beach.

On the island is the Kızkalesi Castle (Maiden's Castle), after which the town is named. In summer you can get there by boat.

There is also a rather large fortress on the shore.

Anamur

If you go west from Silifke, the only more or less large city for many kilometers to Alanya will be Anamur. The coast here consists entirely of steep cliffs, however, there are also many such areas in Lycia.

Mountain roads.

The main attraction of Anamur is Mamure Castle on the seashore, 5 kilometers from the city center. The highway passes by it, so I just got off the intercity bus in front of the castle.

This is one of the largest fortresses I have seen in Turkey.

There is nothing inside except the mosque, but you can climb high up the walls and into the citadel.
