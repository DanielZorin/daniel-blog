Amsterdam

I was in Amsterdam quite a long time ago, in 2012. It was definitely worth visiting one of the most iconic cities in Europe again.

As you know, Amsterdam is one of the most expensive cities in Europe for tourists due to expensive housing. Even hostels here are obscenely expensive, since there is a very high flow of young people here for obvious reasons. In principle, airbnb works in Amsterdam, but it is expensive, or sold out quickly. It is prohibited to rent out entire apartments for more than 60 days a year, but there are also few individual rooms. But a solution was found - you can live in the neighboring city of Amstelveen, which is located immediately south of the administrative border of Amsterdam, and where the metro line leads. On the metro, unlimited pass for two days costs 13.50 euros, for three - 19 euro.

Amstelveen is a residential area with mostly low-rise buildings and its own canals.

I visited the Albina restaurant - this is a very budget establishment in Amsterdam, where they serve mainly Surinamese food. Suriname is a country in South America that was formerly a colony of the Netherlands and where Dutch is still spoken. Their food is a mixture of Caribbean and Indonesia (also a Dutch colony from where many people moved to Suriname).
