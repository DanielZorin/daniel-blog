Santorini

The most beautiful and unusual Greek island is, of course, Santorini (aka Thira). All the islands in the Aegean Sea are normal islands, and this is the top of an ancient volcano. I went to Santorini one day from Crete, and this was not enough - I barely had time to see the main attractions, and did not have time to get to the southern half of the island. So it’s better to fork out for expensive accommodation and come to the island for an overnight stay.

Santorini has the shape of a crescent - this is the top of a volcano, the mouth of which went under water, and the second half of the ring also collapsed. Therefore, on the inner side the rocks drop down almost vertically, and on the other side there is a gentler descent to the water. All photographs are taken from the inside, where the two main cities stand.

There is also an uninhabited volcanic island in the middle of the bay.

Delphi

Upon arrival from Crete, without stopping in Athens, I went straight to Delphi - one of the main cities of Ancient Greece, where the Delphic oracle once was. Like many other ancient Greek cities, it was abandoned for a long time, and the modern city was built in the 19th and 20th centuries for tourists who came to see the excavations.

Delphi is located on a steep slope of a ridge of hills, below is a huge valley overgrown with forest.

In the distance you can see the sea and the city of Itea.

The city was abandoned for many centuries, somewhere from the end of Antiquity, when paganism (and accordingly the Delphic Oracle) was banned. Since the 15th century, a few travelers have described remains that were still visible without the need to dig deep. Only at the end of the 19th century did they begin to explore the territory and remove from the ground the remains of sculptures and architectural elements, which are now stored nearby in the museum.

A very rare example of metal sculpture - usually they were melted down and are not preserved for 1000 years, but this one was lying somewhere unnoticed.

Thessaloniki

I failed to explore the city of Thessaloniki properly - due to severe food poisoning, only in the afternoon, with an incredible effort, I was able to get up and make a circle around the local attractions in three to four hours.

Thessaloniki was a major city in the Byzantine era, so there are many monuments from the 5th to 15th centuries that are included in the World Heritage List - from large temples to small baths in back streets. For simplicity, I laid out the route to visit as many of these places as possible, crossing the city center along the way.
