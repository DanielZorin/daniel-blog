Hungary: Budapest, Szentendre, Eger

20-22 October 2014

Budapest

What could be better than a weekend trip to Europe when tickets cost pennies? The euro then cost less than 50 rubles. And here I am in Budapest for the fourth time, this time not passing through, unlike the previous two.

For a ridiculous 18 euros a day we rented an apartment in the Jozsefváros area. The house turned out to have a courtyard.

In different areas of Budapest the signs are slightly different.

I hadn’t reached this area on previous occasions, but it turned out to be very nice.

I like Budapest more and more every time.

There is also a crisis in Hungary; there are as many homeless people on the streets and in the passages as always.

Finally visited the Kerepesi cemetery.

In this cemetery, instead of crosses, slabs and obelisks, sculptures are placed.

And sculptural compositions.

Some graves are surrounded by greenery.

At the same time, there is still quite a lot of free space in the cemetery, which is surprising for the center of a large city.

Only in one corner are obelisks collected.

And there is also a piece of a Soviet memorial burial.

The rest of the time in the city was devoted to pleasure. One of them is spa where I didn't take my camera. I highly recommend the spa, better than any sea.

All the pleasures - Tokaj, Eger, palinka and sangria to quench your thirst.

Come to vacation in Budapest!

Szentendre

It took me four visits to Budapest to go to the closest historical town - Szentendre.

Yes, it is a beautiful, well-kept city.

The walls are covered with ivy.

Hung with gardening tools.

The streets are paved.

Narrow.

And they quickly end at the embankment.

There is also an embankment near the stream.

For some reason, the inscription on the cafe is in Serbian. In the evening the party is in Budapest, not here.

Eger

There are two most famous types of wine in Hungary. One of them is red, it is made in the city of Eger. The second is white, it is produced in Tokaj, which is even further to the east.

Overall, Eger is a fairly typical Hungarian city.

There are commie blocks here.

Turul.

Relief plaques with the unchanged Széchenyi Street.

Monuments to heroes.

Churches.

Giant churches.

Soviet souvenirs.

But Eger is the first Hungarian city I have visited that has a medieval castle (not counting Buda Castle in Budapest).

There are cannons in the castle...

...and playgrounds.

And a view of the city. By the way, here is the northernmost ancient minaret in Europe, left over from the times when Hungary was conquered by Turkey.
