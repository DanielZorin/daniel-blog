La Coruña

I have long wanted to go to Galicia - the extreme northwestern region of Spain. In general, I even set my sights on the road of St. James, but this takes a lot of time, and it is better to do in the summer. It’s cool in November, so I just flew there by plane, or rather dropped in between Barcelona and the Canary Islands. The largest city in Galicia is called La Coruña (or in Galician - A Coruña).

I recognize this city from a thousand by these grids of white frames on the facades.

Almost the entire center is in this completely unique style.

Complete delight.

I myself lived in a hotel on the main street, behind one of these windows.

Santiago de Compostela

The most famous place in Galicia is, of course, Santiago de Compostela. Here is one of the most important relics of the Catholic Church - the relics of St. James the Apostle. Before that, I was in similar burial places of Apostle Peter in Rome and Apostle Thomas in Chennai. Saint James is considered the patron saint of travelers, so visiting him is, one might say, a matter of honor. One of the most famous walking routes in the world, the Way of St. James, ends here in the square in front of the cathedral. In ancient times, people walked along the road all the way from France.

The route is still popular today, the lightest version takes about 5 days (the last 100 kilometers to Santiago).
