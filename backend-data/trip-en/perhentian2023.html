Perhentian Islands

The whole trip to Malaysia was very nostalgic and of course the final highlight was returning to the Perhentian Islands. In the 6 years since the last time, I have not found a better beach. And so I board the familiar ferry and half an hour later the familiar outlines of Little Perhentian appear.

Ferries still operate from Kuala Besut.

But now they arrive not on the western coast of the island, but on the eastern, at Long beach, first entering the village and Big Perhentian. The fact is that a full-fledged pier was built on Long Beach. And next to it there is also a rather large hotel.

This is not the only new hotel, the whole beach is more built up and there are more tourists than before.

Before the storm.

Because of this (and the pandemic, probably), housing prices have jumped, and Perhentian is no longer a budget vacation spot. In 2016 I lived in the Tropicana Inn for 60 ringit, in 2017 in the D'Rock Garden for 90 with air conditioning (though it only worked at night). Now I was paying about 200 ringit per day ($50) for a house like in D'Rock Garden, but even without air conditioning! And it’s still relatively budget-friendly; in D’Rock itself, what I paid 90 for 6 years ago now costs 400.

I think that in the future you can consider options on the Big Island - now it’s even cheaper somewhere, and a taxi boat costs 15-20 ringit per person.

I went for a walk to the other side in Coral Bay, it’s quiet here now.

I walk all the way north from Coral Bay, and what I see is that there is now a huge pier.

Built right across my favorite part of the beach!

The barge brought an excavator.

In the middle of the island, on a hill, a fairly large hotel is being built, from which there is even now a descent to one beach that previously could only be reached by swimming (I swam there and saw a ready-made stone staircase).

So this is the beach now.

But the other way is fine. In general, it feels like there are more people only on Long Beach, even here the beach is almost empty all the time, although it takes 10-15 minutes to walk here. I dived in, made sure the coral reefs and fish were there, and stopped being so upset about the price increase.
