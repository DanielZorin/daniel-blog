Paraty

The World Heritage-listed city of Paraty is located on the coast approximately midway between Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo. Before the construction of a direct road inland, this was a place to spend the night. During the Portuguese colonization, gold mined in the state of Minas Gerais was brought here. After the end of gold mining, the city lost its significance, and therefore retained its historical appearance.

This region is called the Green Coast (Costa Verde), the landscapes are similar to Rio de Janeiro.

There are many boats near the shore, you can even go to the surrounding islands to dive.

Port Square

Paraty has an interesting feature - the city is close to the sea, and during high tides the streets flood. Therefore, they are specially made so that the sidewalks are raised, in some places there are even bridges, and at first glance it is not clear why they are needed.

Vitória

Vitória is the capital of the rarely visited state of Espirito Santo. I flew there for one day from Rio de Janeiro purely to see another state.

We are approaching the airport.

The airport is new and beautiful.

As usual, I didn’t bother with public transport, which is not very good in this city, especially when you have to travel far due to the difficult terrain. I immediately took a taxi to the south. In fact, there are two cities here: Vitória and Vila Velha, separated by a narrow bay. As its name suggests, Vila Velha is the original location of the city, founded in 1535 (the third city in Brazil after Salvador and Recife). Later the capital was moved to Vitória (which was called Vila Nova da Vitória). There is almost no old architecture left in Vila Velha, except for a couple of forts and a monastery.
