Saudi Arabia: Damma, Riyadh, Jizan, Abha

2-6 December 2022

Dammam

The first city in Saudi Arabia that I visited was Dammam, the capital of the Eastern Province. This is the region where Saudi oil is produced, on the shores of the Persian Gulf, where other oil states are located - Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar and the UAE. Dammam is a new city that will soon celebrate its centenary, and it grew up, of course, thanks to oil production.

Saudi Arabia is the most car-oriented country in the world, public transport has just begun to appear in recent years, all cities are built for cars, usually on a grid and with fairly wide streets.

Nevertheless, walking on foot was, in principle, normal - there are sidewalks everywhere, and they are quite wide and empty, although traffic lights for pedestrians do not always work correctly.

Riyadh

Riyadh is the capital and largest city of Saudi Arabia. Over the past 30 years, the population has grown 5 times, from 1.5 million to 7.5! And they expect to grow to 15, they plan to build the largest airport in the world there. Riyadh is located in the heart of the country, and is home to the Al Saud family, which gives the country its name.

Riyadh is similar in layout to Dubai - there is a small historical center, and a very long wide avenue (more precisely, two parallel ones), lined with skyscrapers and shopping centers, and surrounded by vast residential low-rise areas. The easiest way to see this is from the top of Kingdom Tower, which has an observation deck.

This is a view looking north - the newly built financial district can be seen in the distance.

We move to the historical center. There is a small fort with a museum, from the capture of which the creation of Saudi Arabia began.

King Abdul Aziz first returned his ancestral lands in the Riyadh region, and then subjugated four regions: Najd (the center of the country with Riyadh), Hejaz (the Red Sea coast with Mecca and Medina), Asir (the southern part of the coast near the border with Yemen) and Al-Ahsa (the coast of the Persian Gulf), which made up Saudi Arabia.

Inside the fort.

Judging by the photographs, in the 1930s there was not much civilization here; oil had just been discovered.

Next to the fort is Deera Square, where the courthouse stands. It is famous for the fact that death sentences were previously carried out publicly on it. When I say before, I don't mean the 19th century, but before the 2010s. Now this practice seems to have stopped, and nothing in the square reminds of it.

On the outskirts of Riyadh is Al-Diriyah (formally a separate city) - the historical homeland of the Saudis. There remains a fortress and ruins of the old city.

I thought about visiting this place, I arrived, no one understood what I wanted. Finally I found an English-speaking person, it turned out that you can visit, but you need to buy a ticket online for a certain time! A very strange approach, given the near-zero number of visitors, it is unlikely that they would crowd an entire abandoned city. Though, as it turned out, the only choice was before 6 pm and after 6 pm, but still I was unable to buy a ticket, since none of my cards worked (a typical situation in Saudi Arabia, all my online purchases were very difficult, and I had to juggle cards).

Jizan

From Riyadh I traveled south to the Asir region, one of the country's four historical regions, which was an independent emirate from 1916 to 1934. Asir is the only region of the country where the landscape is not desert, but forested mountains. Very close is the border with Yemen, where the nature is similar.

Abha

The city of Abha is located in the mountains, thanks to that the climate is not as hot as in the rest of Saudi Arabia.

The road from Jizan most of the time goes along the plain, and then sharply rises for about two kilometers.

Periodically along the road you come across houses of hermits living on the tops of rocks.

Serpentine.

Although there are mountains, there is still no vegetation.
