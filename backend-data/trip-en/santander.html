The main goal in Bilbao was to go to the bars and eat pintxos.

There are a lot of people here, but there are also a lot of bars. I also found one that started working before 6 (when most locals arrive).

Pintxos are a variety of sandwiches with incredible combinations of jamon, shrimp and other ingredients.

They say that this is an analogue of tapas from other regions of Spain, but I would not concur. Tapas are a complete simple dish, just smaller in size, they cost around 7-8 euros, and are usually called simply: “shrimp”, “sausage”, “Russian salad”, “cheese plate”, etc. The Basques have these small sandwiches with unusual combinations. Each sandwich costs around 2 euros.
