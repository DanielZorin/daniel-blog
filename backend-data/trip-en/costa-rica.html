Costa Rica: Liberia, La Fortuna, San Jose, Cartago, Heredia, Alajuela, Limon, Puntarenas, Quepos

31 October - 5 November 2021

Liberia

Crossing the border, I found myself in Costa Rica, the most popular country in Central America. My plan was to go first to the Arenal volcano, and then further south to San Jose. According to the schedule, there are two buses from the border directly to my destination, at 6-30 in the morning and in the afternoon. Since the Nicaraguan border guards were interrogating me again, I left the border at 6:40 and was waiting for a bus to the nearest city of Liberia, when at 7:00 the bus I needed to San Carlos passed me, and I did not have time to react to it. I wasn’t going to wait half a day at the border; instead, I decided to try a route with three transfers, which was recommended by the website with schedules: border - Liberia - Cañas - Tilaran - La Fortuna. The first two sections did not promise any difficulties, as they go along the main highway (Pan American).

Limon

In Costa Rica, I visited the shores of both oceans for the first time. The Caribbean coast, like Nicaragua, Honduras and Guatemala, was not particularly colonized by the Spanish, and was settled mainly from the Caribbean islands. Until the mid-20th century, residents of the Caribbean region were not even allowed to move freely to the rest of Costa Rica. Only at the end of the 19th century a railroad was built from San Jose to the coast to access the Atlantic. The road ended in the city of Puerto Limon. The road there descends from the mountains into the jungle.

The journey took much longer than I expected due to traffic jams and constant repairs in different areas.

The further you go, the more tropical the nature becomes.
