Lanzarote

Lanzarote is the most unusual Canary island. It has the most volcanic activity and the least vegetation, so its landscapes are unique in many ways. Lanzarote is less populated than Tenerife and Gran Canaria, there is only one city, and the northern and western parts of the island consist of natural parks.

Apparently to contrast with the black earth, almost all the houses in Lanzarote are white.

Large hotel with swimming pool. As you can see, there is not even a semblance of a beach here, the only beach is on the southern edge of the island. But people don’t come here for the beach, of course.

* * *

In Lanzarote, public transport is not very suitable for tourists: you can only get to populated areas, but not to most parks and other attractions. So we had to improvise a little. On the first day I was in the southern half of the island, on the second - in the northern. To start, I took the bus that goes to the southern edge of the island and ferries to the neighboring island of Fuerteventura, to the village of Uga, one of the last stops.

Bus stop.

Village.

Volcanic cones immediately rise above it.

There are three roads from Uga: the main Arrecife - southern port, and a branch through the central part of the island to the north, before connecting with the main road from Arrecife to the Timanfaya National Park.

Along this third road are the famous Lanzarote vineyards.

Since it is windy and there are no trees, each vine is planted in a hole and surrounded with stones.

So on both sides of the road and right up to the slopes of the surrounding mountains.

It is also home to the Cuervo volcano, which started the Lanzarote eruption in 1730. Then the entire western part of the island was filled with lava, several cities were destroyed

On one side the wall of the volcano has collapsed, so you can easily enter it.

Inside the volcano.

I had already walked 10 kilometers from Uga, but it was still at least 15 to the national park, I had to speed up, so then I hitchhiked. First to the nearest town of Mancha Blanca.

There is just an intersection with the road to the national park. The ticket office is located at the exit from the highway, but from there it’s another 2-3 kilometers to the park’s buildings themselves, which go through fields of lava debris.

The park buildings are located on a slight hill. There is a large car park, a restaurant and a bus stop. Then everyone is equal - you can’t drive through the park by car, everyone gets on a sightseeing bus (the road is narrow, otherwise there would be traffic jams).

It’s not difficult to hitchhike from the national park, since many tourists leave there in the evening. We reached the village of Macher, from where you can get to Arrecife by bus.

While waiting for the bus, you can go to the local roadside canteen, proudly called the “Sociocultural Center”.
