China: Chongqing, Guiyang

11-17 April 2015

Chongqing

I fell in love with Chongqing when I accidentally ended up there in 2013. This is a city where old quarters that miraculously survived all the general secretaries and multi-level overpasses built right above them combine; it is a mixture of eccentric architecture and gorgeous natural landscapes. The former capital of China, the city of the most beautiful girls and the hottest food - Chongqing.

In 2013, I only saw a small part of the city - the central pedestrian area and the cable car, as well as the metro route to the airport. Now I had enough time. First, you should take a walk along the Yangtze embankment.

The embankment is laid out on both sides.

On the northern shore, where the city center is, there is a long park where people play mahjong and drink tea. There is even Wi-Fi attached to the stalls.

This way you can reach the confluence of the Yangtze and the Jialingjiang River.

Most tourists in Chongqing are passing through, because cruises along the Yangtze through the Three Gorges to the city of Yichang start here. It seems that these people are sent from the plane directly to the ship along with their luggage.

Guiyang

Guiyang is the capital of Guizhou Province. The province is bad by Chinese standards, one of the last places in terms of salaries. If you wish, you can find something to see in this region. There are many mountains, beautiful scenery and several old towns, but all this can also be found in the neighboring regions of Hunan and Guangxi, and it is more accessible and cheaper there because it is not so far to travel. That’s why Guizhou province ended in Guiyang for me.

Guiyang is immediately greeting with chaos. The station square is in complete chaos.

Cars, buses - all in one pile.

The station is located on a hill, right from there you can go down one floor, and there will be an area with cheap canteens and hotels. Guiyang is the first city in China where I saw prostitutes standing openly on the street (the second would be Shanghai).

There is an interesting self-service dining format here: for only 10 yuan!

You throw whatever you can in your plate. Not much meat - a lot of fat and bones. Everything is very spicy. But it's 10 yuan.

In general, the main feature of Guiyang turned out to be that for some reason everything here is very expensive. Maybe because the first hieroglyph in the name of the city means “expensive”? Pineapples on a stick cost 5 yuan, although throughout the country they cost 2, maximum 3 yuan.

There are also traffic jams here.

Stickers on the asphalt are suddenly not advertising prostitutes.

Among the attractions there is a good embankment.

Nearby is a park.

Everywhere in China they dance in parks, and in Guiyang they sing. When you stand between two pavilions with singers, you hear a cacophony.

Pavilion. Oddly enough, entry inside is free.

Having nothing better to do, we arranged a photo shoot on these pretentious antique chairs. There are tourists in Guiyang, however, what they do there is unclear, but they are taken on an excursion to the pavilion.

Overpass in the form of a spaceship.

Comfortable benches.

To make it less boring, they placed a golden flying saucer in front of Mao.

And that’s basically all I can say about Guiyang.
