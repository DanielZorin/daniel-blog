Vietnam: Dalat, Mui Ne, Ho Chi Minh City

16-22 August 2015

Dalat

For the average person living in Russia, where 95% of the territory is cold most of the year, a vacation is equivalent to a trip to the sea, where it is hot and you can sunbathe in the sun. If you live in the tropics, where it’s 40 outside every day, then the best vacation is to go as far as possible from the sea inland, where the temperature in the mountains is 20. European colonialists quickly realized the trick and began to build cities in such places with a comfortable climate. Welcome to Dalat - the most comfortable and pleasant city in Vietnam!

A plane ticket from Hanoi is inexpensive - about $50. The bus ride to Da Nang takes all night.

Dalat, like hundreds of other cities around the world, is called “little Paris”. Not very similar, although there is some resemblance to the Eiffel Tower.

Mui Ne

There are two main resort areas in Vietnam: Nha Trang and Mui Ne. I visited the second one. For some reason, almost exclusively Russians vacation in this city, as in Antalya and Sharm el-Sheikh. In most shops and restaurants, everything is written in Russian, and in English equally, very rarely in German.

The main part of Mui Ne consists of large hotels in which you can live without leaving the territory at all. There are shops on the second line, everything is quite similar to Egypt.

Further north, the big hotels end, and there are more cheap accommodations, mainly on the second line, that is, across the road from the sea. For some reason, it is very important for many holidaymakers that the hotel is on the first line; even a five-minute walk across the road is too much. And on the second line, such a luxurious room costs only $8.

Here, however, everything is not so rosy - in many places the descent into the sea is something like this. Of course, for a person who has been to Crimea, this is not scary, but for those spoiled by Philippines it’s somehow not very good.

And large hotels can also ask for money for access to the beach, although in fact it is public, and you can lie on the sand for free. We'll have to go around.

The sea is not very azure, there are a lot of waves. This, on the other hand, attracts surfers here.

Ho Chi Minh City

Usually, if a city has a population of more than 10 million, it means there is a lot of interesting things there, you can hang out for a long time. Saigon (aka Ho Chi Minh City) is an exception to this pattern. This is one of the most boring cities in the world, where there is almost nothing to see.

I spent three days in Saigon and did not find a single remarkable place there.

Only the Pham Ngu Lao area, where cheap hotels are concentrated, is more or less colorful.

Here you can take pictures of thick bundles of wires.

And eat a sandwich for a dollar.

I tried to walk around other areas, but found nothing there except a couple of administrative buildings and a bunch of monotonous streets.

There are no skyscrapers either, a couple of imperfections don’t count.

There is surprisingly little Ho Chi Minh in Ho Chi Minh City.

But there are all sorts of capitalist things. McDonald's in Vietnam, again, is only in this city for now.

There are night markets of moderate interest; they are placed right on the roadway, barring car passage.

I bought a cool-looking fruit to try (momondrica cochinchinensis), but it turned out that this thing is not very tasty, but rather medicinal, and it is better to drink the juice rather than eat it raw, and it looks like liver.

There is only one thing worth seeing in Saigon. This is local traffic.

If you think that you saw a lot of motorcycles in Bangkok, Jakarta or Hanoi, then you are wrong. The heaviest motorcycle traffic is in Saigon. All other cities in Vietnam are nowhere near.

Only in Saigon can real difficulties arise with crossing the street. There are several intersections (one of them is pictured in the photo) where motorcycles move in a continuous stream, also managing to drive on the crossing without colliding. In other cities there are a lot of motorcycles, but crossing the street is easy - you just walk confidently and slowly, motorcycles have enough space to go around you, fortunately they are small in width. But this won’t work in Saigon - here, as you can see, there is no gap at all between neighboring motorcycles, which means you have to practically jump over the wheels. The most reliable way is to avoid such intersections.
