Bonito

Bonito is a small town in the state of Mato Grosso do Sul, where ecotourism is very developed. The system here is very elaborate: you need to buy tickets to all places in advance, since the number of visitors per day is strictly limited (and now, due to the coronavirus, this norm has also been halved). Tickets are sold through travel agencies that have access to a unified computer reservation system. For some reason, there is no access there for ordinary people, so it is difficult to book something in advance - you need to contact a local travel agency by e-mail (for this you often need to know Portuguese). I didn’t book anything in advance, and bought everything on the first evening in Bonito, fortunately the travel agent was at the next table with the administrator of my hotel. In addition to tickets to the attractions themselves, you also need to book transport to them, since many are far away (50-70 km), and you cannot get there by bicycle. You can rent a car, but it is very expensive for 1-2 people; it is easier to buy a transfer from the same travel agency, especially since they always have enough free seats.

So, what is there in Bonito? Firstly, snorkeling in rivers. This is the most expensive (but worthwhile) entertainment, costs around 200 reais (there are several different places, they differ slightly in prices) transfer. Secondly, caves. Entrance price 40-80 reais. The most famous one, where tourism in Bonito began, is the Blue Lake (Gruta de Lago Azul), I was not able to get there, because... there were no places. There is also the Abismo Anhumas cave, also with a lake, where the entrance costs some unrealistic money - more than 1000 reais ($200, and a few years ago it was $500). How it deserved such a price, I still don’t understand; on Saipan for example you can dive in a similar cave much cheaper. Thirdly, swimming (called balneario, these are not hot springs, but swimming in the river perhaps something like a water park). It's also not very expensive, the cheapest option is only 38 reais (for the whole day). Fourthly, trips to the Pantanal with safari, which I will talk about separately later. Pantanal

The Pantanal is a huge swampy area in southwestern Brazil. This is one of the richest places on earth in diverse fauna. There are no through roads via the Pantanal, so you can only enter it from two directions - from the north from Cuiaba or from the south through Bonito. Excursions for tourists are organized in the territories of fazendas (large farms). The programs are approximately the same, and include land and water safaris, and possibly a night one. A one-day trip from Bonito costs 238 reais ($45) plus another $20 round trip transfer, but a two-day trip is sharply more expensive - 810 reais for one or 1,110 for two. Moreover, they differ in content only in the night safari, on which, although there is a chance to see a jaguar, it is not very big.

The transfer left very early, before 6 am, although there was no particular need for this - then at the fazenda we waited a long time for the start of each excursion. On the way, we passed through the city of Miranda, probably, you can also base yourself there if you drive your own car.
