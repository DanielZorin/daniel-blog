USA: Los Angeles, San Diego

5-7, 30 November 2014

Los Angeles

I asked the big guy in the yard, Who drank beer in the morning in dirty sweatpants: “Tell me, who wants you to live like this?” "The United States of America!" Los Angeles is a city from which you expect the worst. Endless square streets of the same type, zero pedestrians and traffic jams - this is roughly the picture that emerges from the guidebook. With this attitude, I really liked LA. It turned out that this is a completely civilized city, no worse than, say, Shenzhen.

The flight from Moscow to Los Angeles was not the longest for me: Dubai - Rio de Janeiro was two hours longer. We were lucky with the weather: it was cloudless, everything was clearly visible. This is Mono Lake, with Yosemite National Park in the foreground. Of course, most tourists in California go there and don't stay in Los Angeles.

Sierra Nevada.

The fields are lined with typical American squares.

And here is the city.

Like most large American cities, Los Angeles is a group of skyscrapers in the downtown area and endless one-story buildings around.

Los Angeles is famous for its highway interchanges. They look very impressive from the air.

From the ground it’s also nice.

Contrary to popular belief, Los Angeles has functional public transportation. Several metro lines and many buses. At the same time, Los Angeles transport is devoid of the main drawback that causes people in Moscow to switch to cars: there are no crowds and crush. Traveling by metro and bus is much cheaper than renting a car, so it is very suitable for tourists. If you have your own car, then the difference in price is not that big: gasoline in the USA costs around $1 per liter, 1 liter is enough travel 15-20 kilometers, one trip on the subway costs $1.75, and a monthly pass costs $100, i.e. for commuting to work within 30 kilometers from home, the car is no more expensive, provided there is free parking. Apparently, that’s why I was the only white passenger on the subway and buses.

All major Los Angeles attractions are easily accessible by public transportation. It’s also not difficult to understand the system; there are diagrams everywhere. The most convenient for tourists is a day pass for $7.

What can you see in L.A.? Paradoxically, there is also an “old town” here. It consists of three houses and one street.

California was once part of Mexico, but at that time the population there was very small; today's large cities were small monasteries. In the "old town" Mexicans sell Mexican souvenirs.

Los Angeles is, first and foremost, a place where movies are made. If after my trip to New York I began to recognize half of the background scenery, now I’ll probably recognize the other half.

I'm not a good movie connoisseur; I've never heard 90% of the names on the Avenue of Stars.

Each star has a clue about their field of activity: cinema, television, music. The Simpsons are from television.

The center of life in Los Angeles if Hollywood, not downtown. At least you can meet passers-by here in the evening. For example, three spider-men and Chewbacca.

The second thing Los Angeles is famous for is its beaches. It takes about 40 minutes to get to them from the city center.

It was hot, and I had a time difference of -11 hours, so I was exhausted on the beach and slept for a while.

Despite the warmth, no one swims; people only go into the water to ride a board.

This beach is formally located in the city neighboring Los Angeles - Santa Monica. It has its own "city center".

Even the pedestrian zone is just like in Europe.

In the flower bed with the plant, they hung a whole wall of text with rules of behavior, as well as a warning that there might be dangerous chemicals somewhere nearby.

Public offer laws in the USA work somehow differently than in Russia. Here the store may refuse to serve the client without explanation.

Well, at least taking pictures on the streets doesn’t seem to be prohibited.

Free beer, naked hairdressers and deceptive advertising.

On the last day before departure, I had some time left, so I decided to take the subway to the final stop in another suburb - Long Beach.

The weather was disappointing, and I didn’t find anything particularly interesting.

On the way to the airport, the outlines of downtown were visible in the fog.

San Diego

The southernmost city in California turned out to be the cutest place.

There are excellent trams all over the city, Europe! Travel is $5 per day.

The multi-storey buildings reminded me of the creations of the architects of southern China.

There is a base of the American fleet nearby, so all sorts of ships and submarines are displayed on the embankment.

The airport is located near the city center, and you can watch planes land from a close distance.

I went to old town San Diego. This is not a shambles, but a State Historic Park.

It looks like the set for a movie about the wild west.

Saloon.

Cacti.

Cowboys played these cards.

This old town is purely a museum and amusement park, not a residential area.

Nearby there is a small area with villas from a more recent era.

A little further away there is a monastery where the city once began.

But the most pleasant place is the area with the original name “Gas Lamp Quarter”.

To be honest, I didn’t expect to see such architectural delights in the USA.

It seems that anonymous also recently rallied here against the tyranny of Obama.

On the outskirts of San Diego is the border with Mexico. You can get there by tram, and then walk along the corridor. There are no controls at the exit from the USA, except for police with dogs. That's where I headed.
