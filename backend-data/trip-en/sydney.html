Blue Mountains

The Blue Mountains are an hour and a half train ride from Sydney. The most popular viewing point is located near Katoomba station, but in general the area is quite large, and around other stations in both directions there are also many walking tracks, and in some places, roads. I got off at Leura station, 3 kilometers before Katoomba.

It's a little cooler here than in Sydney, and in April you can clearly see the golden Australian autumn.

It reminded me a bit of autumn in Japan.

Canberra

Canberra is the capital of Australia. It's a fairly large city (population 400,000), but very sparsely populated, more than other major cities in Australia. Canberra was built from scratch to serve as a capital about 100 years ago, so like other similar cities (see Chandigarh, Washington, Putrajaya), there is a strict general plan, wide streets, and the government buildings are concentrated in one block.

The bus and train stations are located at opposite ends of the city, which determined the trajectory of my movement.

To the north of the river is an area of ​​shops and offices, to the south of the river is the government. It's busier in the north.

Hill with a flag on top.

Canberra, especially northern Canberra, is not a very pedestrian-friendly city. For example, the hill with the flag is surrounded by a large junction that you need to cross in a zigzag, otherwise you won’t get to the bridge leading to the south.

Goulburn

It's time to look at a small provincial town in Australia. In Goulburn I had a very long transfer from one train to another. It is a city in the province of New South Wales, famous for being the first non-seaside settlement in Australia to receive city status. Now the population here is 24 thousand people. I was in the city in the evening, there were few cars, but many shops were still open.
