Russia: Saint Petersburg, Priozersk, Shlisselburg

5-9 June 2014

Saint Petersburg

Arriving in St. Petersburg for the seventh time, I set out to visit all the places I had never been before, and those were mostly the outskirts. One of the most interesting outskirts is Kanonersky Island. This island on the shore of the bay is located between several factories and cargo docks, but for some reason they decided to build several residential buildings there. Moreover, until 1981, the island could only be reached by ferry, which made it probably the most inconvenient place to live in St. Petersburg (on par with Kronstadt, which also had no road at that time). Now you can get to the island through a tunnel, including on foot, although it is a bit difficult, since the pedestrian path is very narrow. The easiest way to get there is by bus from the Baltiyskaya metro station in 30 minutes.

The first thing you see on Kanonersky Island is the shipyard on Gutuevsky Island opposite.

This is the cargo port of St. Petersburg (the passenger port is at Vasilievsky Island).

Since last time, four districts remained unknown: Krasnoselsky, Kirovsky, Frunzensky and Kolpinsky. I got to know Krasnoselsky a little when I arrived at Pulkovo airport. The current airport is good, but next to the fence you can still see the old airport, which was extremely poor and looked like the airport of Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk .

If you come to St. Petersburg for the first time through the airport, you can get a culture shock from the difference between expectation and reality, arriving from the airport to the Moskovskaya metro station. However, I think very few tourists get here by plane, since the train is cheaper and more convenient.

Priozersk

Not very far from Priozersk is Putin's dacha and the Ozero cooperative. The landscapes are purely Karelian; it’s quite a short drive to the administrative border of Karelia.

The main attraction of Priozersk is the Swedish fortress.

Entrance costs only 30 rubles.

Across the river from the fortress, near the buildings of the so-called “new fortress”, built several centuries later, is a sanatorium. At the entrance to it, among the fir trees, there is a monument to Lenin, a gray copy of the monument standing near the platform of Tarkhovka.

He is also in the central square.

Apart from the fortress, there is nothing special to see in Priozersk.
