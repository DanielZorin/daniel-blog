Limassol

For the first half of their lives, global orcs fight with each other for the right to leave Urkaina for London, and the second half - they sit in London and watch Urkaina's television. (Victor Pelevin "Snuff")

Life is good, you were able to land a good job, made a fortune, you are rich and successful. You exchanged your shitty gray Khrushchev apartment for an apartment in Cyprus, and now you have everything: palm trees, blue sea, white yachts - well, you get the idea. But something goes wrong. It's so boring and sickening. You miss the familiar gloomy faces and rude voices, appearances by Dmitry Kiselev on TV and Stas Mikhailov on the radio, Olivier salad, Doshirak noodles and other traditional dishes of Russian cuisine, products written off from Europe for increased toxicity, and of course, beloved, understandable and beautiful Cyrillic alphabet. And at this moment THEY appear on the stage.

Enterprising compatriots will provide you with everything you need.

All price tags are in Russian.

And prices are five times higher than in Russia. Well, for the love of the Motherland, don’t feel sorry!

The official language of Cyprus is Greek. But in reality, you still need to look for people who speak it.

To come across an inscription in Greek is a rare stroke of luck. The plaque illustrates well the position of the Greek language in Limassol.

In principle, this is logical. Advertising should be aimed at a potential solvent audience, to which the Greek Cypriots clearly do not belong after the crisis. The Arabs are, but the Greeks are not.

A business lunch for almost $20, by the standards of Moscow - a little expensive, by the standards of Cyprus - even more so (in general, it is cheaper there than in Moscow).

Perhaps you will reproach me for the fact that in the story about Limassol I did not show anything interesting in the city except the signs. Sleep well: apart from signboards, there is nothing worthy of attention in the city. However, if you like lying on the beach under the scorching sun all day long (for me this is unbearable torture), then the conditions here are very decent. With the exception of the flight (to Turkey it's still cheaper), everything is quite cheap, the infrastructure is good and clean. The accommodation was a pleasant surprise - for 20 euros you can rent an excellent apartment.
