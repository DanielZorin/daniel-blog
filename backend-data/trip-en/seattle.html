Seattle is the second technology capital of the United States after San Francisco; two of the largest IT companies are located here: Microsoft and Amazon. The consequence of this is that Seattle is as expensive as San Francisco, and there are even a lot of homeless people on the streets, although it would seem that the climate (rainy, like in England) is not conducive to this.

In the early 20th century, all of central Seattle was raised to second-floor level to solve sewer and storm drainage problems. The streets were laid at the second floor level on stilts, and the former first floors became basements. Only the rails remained at the same level, so here you can clearly see that all the buildings start above ground level.

In the foreground, the Space Needle tower stands away from the main group of skyscrapers.

It was originally built as an observation deck and restaurant for the World's Fair, just like the Eiffel Tower in Paris almost a hundred years earlier.

There is also a tourist monorail with two stops, which, however, is part of Seattle public transport and accepts passes for payment.

There is also a metro in Seattle, or rather the Light Rail, which can also take you to the airport. On the diagram, all stations are indicated by pictograms, like in the Mexico City metro.

Train.

It is unusual that in the city center the stations are underground, and they serve both trains (more like trams here) and buses at the same time. The train pulled away, followed by the bus.

What is inconvenient is the lack of day tickets, which are so good in almost all cities in the USA and Canada, except New York. In Seattle, if you are a tourist, you will have to buy tickets for one trip for more than three dollars, however, they allow free transfers - the driver gives you a ticket, torn off at the current time.

I went to the Fremont area, primarily because there is a monument to Lenin. It turned out that someone had painted Lenin’s hands as if with blood.

History of the monument. Lewis Carpenter found it in the late 80s in Poland in a garbage dump, where it was dumped after the overthrow of communism, and brought it to his home. Somehow it ended up in Seattle, and everyone got used to it standing on the street.

Portland

All my plans for Portland were ruined by the monstrous delay of the bus from Seattle by 7 hours - as a result, instead of almost the whole day, I barely had time to walk through the very center of the city before dark, and I wantede even to go to the neighboring city of Vancouver, the namesake of the Canadian city. Portland is the largest city in Oregon, but, as is often the case in America, it is not the capital. Now Portland is following the path of its northern brother Seattle with an influx of tech companies and hipsters. Portland took a respectable second place on my list of cities with the most expensive housing in the world, since a bed in a hostel here cost more than $47, but still 16 cents cheaper than the previous day in Seattle. On the other hand, in Seattle four days earlier another hostel cost “only” $33 (but later there were no places there), but in Portland there is only one hostel and there is no choice.
