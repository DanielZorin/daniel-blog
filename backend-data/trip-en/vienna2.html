Austria: Vienna

23-25 November 2012

Vienna

Vienna begins to prepare for Christmas.

There is a Christmas market on the square in front of the town hall.

Decorations are hung over the central streets, for example, in the form of huge red balls.

As almost everywhere in Western Europe, in the evening there is no one on the streets, only on the main street there is some kind of activity.

Since I saw all the sights last time, I decided to just walk along the streets in areas where I hadn’t been much last time and see what was going on there. I came across the Viennese Orthodox Church.

Many seemingly unsightly houses are decorated with decorations. Here is a statue on the wall of a gray, dull house.

And here it is on the corner.

Golden ball on the roof, reminiscent of Madrid.

The palace, as always, is luxurious.

Coat of arms with gold.

And a female griffin.

Atlants and the Brazilian Embassy.

Strange trees with clumps of foliage that look like nests.

The Vienna web is almost in the shape of a tetrahedron.

Canceled bus stop.

Parking in the central square - 17 euros per hour. In Moscow the standard of living is no lower than in Vienna, but some people are hesitant to pay 50 rubles.

Turkish emigrants are worried about the fate of their fatherland.

Suddenly, religious people in Austria.

Monument to suitcases.

In the Vienna metro, in some places the old stop lobbies have been preserved. At least one near Schönbrunn Palace, one near the central park.

In Vienna there are also protest stickers on the walls, but they are small and inconspicuous.

Schönbrunn is also a nightclub.

In Hofer stores, the labeling of products is unusual: the price does not hang from below, but from above, on the shelf of the product.

Unknown sculptor - monument to Homer Simpson.

A scary structure that makes mystical sounds.

On Austrian Air planes, waltzes are played during boarding and disembarking. They can also be heard in the toilet under the Vienna Opera.
