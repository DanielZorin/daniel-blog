Hamelin

Does everyone know the fairy tale about the Pied Piper of Hamelin? Let me remind those who forgot. A rat catcher with a pipe set out to rid the city of rats. Playing the pipe, he lured all the rats, took them with him and drowned them in the river. But the mayor decided to ditch the rat catcher and refused to pay the reward. A year later, the rat catcher returned and, with the help of a magic pipe, took all the children with him.

There are statues of rats throughout the city.

Lüneburg

Of the dozens of German cities I have visited, Lüneburg will definitely be in the top. I liked this city so much that I even came there a second time, when I had time and a valid travel card. It's about 40 minutes ride from Hamburg.

Many centuries ago, Lüneburg was a rich city, when the salt that was mined here was expensive. Then it became an insignificant province.

Hamburg

Hamburg is home to Europe's most famous red light district, after Amsterdam of course. Of course, hotels there are cheaper, that's why I lived there.

The area is quite large, but everything mostly happens behind closed doors and there are few bright signs.

This is the same main street in the evening.

Reeperbahn street.

Laconically: sex - 39 euros.

Let's move on. In Hamburg there is an area with canals and docks - Speicherstadt.

Previously, they loaded here directly from the water, so the facades of multi-story buildings face directly onto the canal.

Now it's a cool business district.

An interesting combination of architectures from different times.

There is a strange lookout point at the edge of the area.

Strange because from it you can mainly see the neighboring junction.

There is an interesting thing here - a tunnel under the river, built in the German Empire more than 100 years ago.

For cars, the tunnel only works one way in the morning, the other in the evening, and only on weekdays. For pedestrians and cyclists around the clock.
