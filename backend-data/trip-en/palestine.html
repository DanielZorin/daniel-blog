It is not difficult for a tourist to get to Palestine. This state has no border controls, and the external borders are guarded by Israeli border guards. Israeli currency is used here. However, it is almost impossible for tourists to enter the Gaza Strip; the territory is tightly cordoned off by the army. When you go to the West Bank, there is usually no control. There are buses to Bethlehem near the Damascus Gate of Jerusalem, and a little further around the corner there are buses to Ramallah. You can drive to the wall and cross the border on foot, but then you cannot avoid inspection. When entering back into Israel, they usually check your passports, but if you don’t look Arab, no questions are asked. In general, no one checked anything with me, much less inspected my things, so if the terrorists were able to recruit a white foreigner, he could easily carry out a terrorist attack in Israel. Well, probably, there are no fools, and thank God. Tourists in Palestine, with the exception of Bethlehem, are rare visitors, so those who have overcome fear will receive a warm welcome.
