Saint Kitts and Nevis

11-13 May 2022

Saint Kitts

Saint Kitts and Nevis is perhaps the most famous of the small Caribbean countries, thanks to the fact that they were the first to arrive to the idea of selling their citizenship. For the same reason, I think it looks richer than the others. Tourists don’t seem to be really needed here, judging by the fact that of all the Caribbean countries they opened after the pandemic later than anyone else, and still I needed a test and received an entry permit online, and only a couple of weeks before my arrival they canceled the requirement to live in one of the hotels on a very short list of the 10 most expensive hotels in the country.

I checked into the cheapest hotel in the capital - the city of Basseterre. The hotel cost $89 per day - this is the most expensive hotel in my life in all 140 countries. Further from the capital there was an airbnb for 60 dollars, like in Antigua, but I preferred to play it safe, since I had to obtain an entry permit online.

In this country, the spirit of good old England is preserved the best.

Let's look at St. Kitts outside the capital. The island is shaped like a circle around a volcano, with a narrow and long peninsula to the east towards Nevis.

First, the view from an airplane towards the western part of the island. On the left you can see the neighboring island of Sint Eustatius (Statia), which belongs to the Netherlands.

Nevis

The second island of Nevis is very close to St. Kitts and only a 40-minute ferry ride away, so it's easy to get to. The waves don’t shake the boat very much, because most of the way is along the coast of St. Kitts. Nevis is round in shape, essentially a volcano protruding from the water.
