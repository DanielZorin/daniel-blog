Bulgaria: Burgas, Nessebar, Kazanlak, Shipka, Gabrovo, Veliko Tarnovo, Varna

27-30 August 2021

Burgas

I have long wanted to go to Bulgaria and Romania, since these two Balkan countries were not explored in sufficient detail by me during the big trip in 2014. There is one time when it is ideal to fly to Bulgaria - late summer, when the beach season ends, and charter airlines sell tickets there for next to nothing, since they mainly need planes to take people back. You could buy a ticket for 3-4 thousand rubles ($50) even in 2021. I flew to the city of Burgas on the Black Sea coast, in the part of Bulgaria where I had not been before.

Bulgaria is still the most similar country to Russia in the Balkans.

Nessebar

The only town in Bulgaria included in the World Heritage List is small Nessebar on the Black Sea coast near Burgas.

Nessebar stands on a small peninsula connected by a thin isthmus to the mainland (in the distance).

The beach resort Sunny Beach (Slanchev Bryag) was built near Nessebar. Judging by the map, there are only large hotels there, and all the restaurants and the party scene are in Nessebar.

Shipka

Shipka is a small town on the southern slopes of the Balkans near the pass of the same name. An important battle took place here in the Russian-Turkish War of 1877-1878.

I took a minibus from Kazanlak. Along the way I saw a Soviet-style monument with the colors of the Bulgarian flag.

The central square of Shipka and the bus stop ("spirka"). In the distance you can see the church on the hill.

There is a monument here.

In 1923, there was an attempt at a communist coup, which was suppressed, but after the war, under communist rule, monuments began to be erected everywhere in its honor.

Veliko Tarnovo

The most beautiful city in Bulgaria is Veliko Tarnovo, an ancient capital from the times when Bulgaria was great and occupied half of the Balkan Peninsula (before the Ottoman Empire appeared there).

This city has a very unusual topography - the river makes several bends, eventually turning 180 degrees, so that the city is sandwiched on both sides, and here it is not a plain, but a real canyon, so the river is far below, and the city is on the high banks . The Swiss capital has a similar layout, but here the shape of the territory skirted by the river is more complex.

You walk along a narrow street between rows of houses, then a cliff begins on one side and this view opens up.

This is the Tsarevets fortress - the ancient Bulgarian capital. On the left, on the other bank, there is a continuation of it - the remains of walls surround the former city.
