Sweden: Stockholm

9-11 October 2016

Stockholm

On my last visit to Stockholm in 2012, I barely saw the metro. I rode through several central stations, but didn’t get to any really worthwhile ones. This time I prepared thoroughly: I found a list of the most beautiful stations, and purchased a day pass for ~13 euros. Many beautiful stations are located not only not in the center, but on the northern outskirts, in areas populated mainly by refugees from Somalia.

The first station is Thorildsplan, it is decorated with squeaky graphics, like in old computer games.

Stockholm is a memorable place for me - it’s the first city I went to on my own, and it’s also where I stayed in a hostel for the first time. This time I also stayed in a hostel, it was very cheap for Scandinavia - about 12 euros. I remembered the city well, despite the fact that almost 5 years had passed, and now I know it even better.
