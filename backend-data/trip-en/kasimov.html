Kasimov

The main goal of the trip was Kasimov - one of the historical cities of Russia in the north of the Ryazan region. This is one of the most beautiful small towns in Russia with an unusual history: in the mid-15th century, Moscow Prince Vasily II gave this region (and the city, which was then called New Lower City) to Kasim, the son of the Kazan Khan, who helped him in the struggle for the throne. As a result, the Kasimov Khanate appeared in the middle of Muscovite Rus', for a long time remained a formally independent vassal of Moscow, and was fully annexed only at the end of the 17th century.

The current Kasimov is, of course, not a city of the 15th century, but rather of the 18th-19th, but with a very extensive and well-preserved historical center.

Bridge. It was built relatively recently (in 1985); before that, a ferry crossed the river.

There are almost no residential areas on the other side of the river, but a few kilometers from the shore is the Prioksky Non-Ferrous Metals Plant - the place where bullions for Russia's gold reserves are produced.

An interesting page in the history of the city is connected with the plant - in the early 1990s, through cunning machinations, workers stole gold from the plant, and in the city itself the mafia lived in incredible luxury, from time to time killing each other in the struggle for control over the gold trade. You can watch a film about these events from the series “Criminal Russia”.

Old Ryazan

Not everyone knows that the city of Ryazan received its name only at the end of the 18th century, and before that it was called Pereslavl-Ryazansky. Historical Ryazan, the capital of the principality of the same name, was in a different place, 50 kilometers from the modern one. Ancient Ryazan was destroyed during the Mongol invasion in the 13th century, and by the 14th century it was almost completely abandoned. Now near this place there is the village of Staraya Ryazan and a few kilometers away on the other side of the river the city of Spassk.

First I had to get from Kasimov to the village of Shilovo, then turn onto the Ural highway. These are local roads, but it turned out that they are of quite good quality in the Ryazan region. Shilovo is a fairly large village.
