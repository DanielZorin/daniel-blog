Schengen

The last point in Luxembourg was a town whose name is well known to every traveler. On June 14, 1985, on a ship in the middle of the Moselle River at the junction of the borders of France, Germany and Luxembourg near the town of Schengen, an agreement was signed that abolished borders within Europe. It came into force only 10 years later, in 1995, and for the first two years there were only five countries in the Schengen zone: France, Germany, Belgium, the Netherlands and Luxembourg (and borders between the Benelux countries were abolished even earlier).

Schengen is located in the south-eastern corner of Luxembourg, from the capital it takes about 30 minutes by bus. There is no railway here, although there is a line on the other side of the river in Germany. The city looks something like this:
