Russia: Krapivna, Shchyokino, Tula

29 August 2020

Krapivna

Continuing to explore the historical cities of Russia, I went to the Tula region to Krapivna. Of the entire list, only two cities now have the status of rural settlements: Krapivna and stanitsa Starocherkasskaya. However, getting to Krapivna is not particularly difficult: there are direct buses from Tula (to the large village of Arsenyevo) 6-7 times a day, and also, as I found out on the spot, from the nearest town of Shchyokino (25 km) at least once a day. Minibuses and buses run through Krapivna every hour to two neighboring villages.

Now let's go to the center. Since this is a village, everything is very close, there are no kilometers of Soviet panel or private buildings that appeared with the growth of the city's population. Everything here is like in the 19th century, when the population of any city was measured in thousands, not hundreds of thousands.

On the central square stands the main temple of Krapivna. It was closed for restoration, so I couldn't get inside. Judging by old photographs, 10 years ago this temple was operational and was in more or less normal condition (well, certainly not in the same condition as the church above).

Shchyokino

Shchyokino is an industrial city 25 kilometers south of Tula. There are two chemical plants here. I got here on the road from Krapivna, and decided to explore the city center, since going to Tula is not a problem at all - minibuses run along the main road every 15 minutes.

The southern front line passed through the Shchyokino area during the Battle of Moscow at the end of 1941.

The main street leading to Tula is lined with four-story buildings like this.

Tula

There was not much time left for Tula after Krapivna, but I still saw more than on last trip. On the way from Shchyokino, I left on the southern outskirts of Tula - the former village of Kosaya Gora, located among picturesque forests.

The chimneys of a metallurgical plant rise above the forests, it looks very colorful.

A little later I learned that the best panoramic view of the plant will be from the route along which minibuses travel to the center of Tula, however, there are no buildings or stops along the road, so you will have to walk along the edge of the route.
