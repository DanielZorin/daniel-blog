Elvas

I didn't go to this remote area in vain - I found another hidden pearl of Portugal, and indeed of the whole of Europe. Meet the city of Elvas.

Elvas is located 10 kilometers from the border with Spain. In the old days there were many wars between Portugal and Spain, so no expense was spared in building walls around the hill where Elvas stands.

The current star-shaped outer walls have been built since the 17th century.

The walls are on several levels, each level has its own gate. There are only two entrances to the city through gates (one of them, in this photo, is for cars), in another place the entrance was made at a later time.

Most of the fortifications are climbable, although one corner is fenced off for a military base and museum.

There are also forts on the neighboring hills.

On the other side of the top there is a steep cliff.

And this is the very first level of fortifications, dating back to the 12th century.

The intermediate walls are so densely covered with houses that they can only be traced through a few towers.

Sintra

I’ve already been to Sintra, but it’s always nice to take another walk, especially when the train costs 2 euros.

Sintra has a couple of streets with souvenirs and many palaces around.

In addition to the palace museums, there are many private villas with fences built around them.

To get to the two main palaces you either have to walk a long way up the hill or take a bus, which is not very cheap.

In principle, you can get into the Pena Palace without a ticket, through the fence.

I limited myself to photographing the park, although it is not difficult to climb over.

You can go down not only along the main road, but also along a goat path like this.

Then you can go straight to the most beautiful place in Sintra - the Regaleira Palace.

We took a walk, now we can go to the station.

Faro

I spent a long time deciding whether it was worth going from Lisbon for one day to Faro (45 euros round trip), but in the end I decided to go for the sake of completeness. In general, the south of Portugal is the least interesting part; it is built up mainly with resort villages. Faro is almost the only city worthy of attention in the region.

Faro is not a resort, although there is a sea here (a deep and winding bay). There is a railway along the beach.

Lisbon

If an ordinary Portuguese city stands on a hill, then Lisbon, due to its size, is on seven hills, so the number of observation platforms here breaks all records.

You take an elevator or tram and get a good view.

Lisbon is a city where I can walk endlessly.

And every time I find something.

Portugal - for sale.

It has the highest concentration of black and yellow tiles in the country. They are even at train stations.

Even on the subway.

Every self-respecting company posts its name at the entrance.

Lisbon is a city of tolerance.

In practice, this means that almost all migrants from former colonies in Africa settle here.

There are a lot of homeless people, almost like in Paris.

The Chinese are picketing.

The Brazilian gives a concert.

In Lisbon, marijuana dealers stand on every corner and offer their goods to passers-by. Portugal is one of the most liberal countries in Europe in this regard. Alcohol lovers also have where to spend money, for example, a tavern where cherry liqueur is poured. There's always a queue.

Lisbon probably has the most boring street signs in all of Portugal. Similar to Barcelona ones.

There is enough beauty here even without signs.

100% indistinguishable from San Francisco, if not for the statue of Jesus in the background.

Portugal is a country of nostalgia. Here, more than anywhere else, they love to remember the epic past, and they adore everything vintage and authentic. It's no surprise that there is a store in Lisbon that sells items from brands from when our grandmothers were young.

Where else do they sell goods in 60s packaging?

On one of the streets they hung old issues of magazines with covers reporting on important sporting events.

Residential area.

It's funny that I've been to Lisbon twice, but I didn't take the trams, only the metro. I walked everywhere in the center.

Come to Portugal.

There is always a holiday here.
