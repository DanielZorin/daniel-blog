Moldova: Chisinau

31 January - 1 February 2013

Chisinau

Which is more correct: Moldova or Moldavia? In Russian language it is a disputed question, similar to the naming of Belarus/Belorussia. In English it's simpler: the modern country is called Moldova (like in the local language), and Moldavia is the historical region, part of which is in modern Romania. In Chisinau, however, many people communicate in Russian, not Romanian, in everyday life. Russian speech can often be heard on the streets; there are many signs in Russian.

Here is some religious propaganda in Romanian.

And on the opposite wall of the same underground passage - in Russian.

Street signs are duplicated in Russian, despite the fact that the Russian language does not have official status in Moldova.

Some signs are not duplicated in Romanian.

With all this, the local government has Latvian quirks about the Soviet occupation, those shot personally by Stalin, etc. They are even going to erect a memorial opposite the presidential palace, as the sign says.

Written Romanian is not difficult to understand if you know other Roman languages ​​and read about the grammar. I understood almost all the inscriptions, despite the fact that in Romanian I only know “Nu ma nu ma iei” and some songs Zdob si Zdub. Romania is loved here.

"Bessarabia is Romania."

The city of Chisinau itself is not impressive.

It looks like a provincial town with one-story houses.

Snow removal is pretty bad. Worse than in Moscow.

Local market.

In the center there is a well-equipped park with busts of Moldovan historical figures, free fast Wi-Fi and sockets near the benches.

In contrast to Russia, there are lots of gambling establishments, that already disappeared from Russia.

There are also vending machines on the street here.

On the traffic lights the background is illuminated.

At the stops there is a bus schedule accurate to the minute. Payment on buses and trams is like in the provinces in Russia, the conductor unwinds small tickets from a roll. The fare is incredibly cheap, about 8 rubles.

Orthodoxy is widespread in Moldova, but the visual culture is somewhat different from Russia. I have not seen such a style in Russia.

We walked around, drank wine, and moved on. There is not much special flavor here, you have to go to the countryside for that, but that’s for next time.
