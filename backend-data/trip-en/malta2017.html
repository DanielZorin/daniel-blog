Valletta and Mdina

During this time, the price of travel has risen sharply; if previously a ticket for the whole day cost 1.5 euros, now one trip costs 1.25 euros (if you buy a ticket for 12 trips), however, transfers within 90 minutes are free. I lived again north of Valletta in the city of St. Julian's, in winter the hotel costs 20 euros per day.

Cottonera

South of Valletta on the other side of the harbor are three cities: Birgu, Bormla and Senglea, known as the Three Cities or Cottonera (after the Grand Master Cottonera, under whom their fortifications were built). Birgu and Senglea occupy two narrow and long peninsulas, and Bormla seems to connect them. These cities also have Italian names: respectively, Vittoriosa ("Victory City"), Cospicua ("Notable") and Invicta ("Invincible").

Entrance to Birgu. The fortifications are really powerful.

Birgu was the capital of Malta in the 16th century, and withstood an Ottoman siege in 1565. After this, a new capital was built, named after the conqueror of the Turks, Jean de Valette.

Gozo

The state of Malta occupies two islands - the large island of Malta and the small Gozo. In Maltese it is called Għawdex, which is pronounced roughly like "Audesh". There is also a small island called Comino in the middle, but it is uninhabited. Gozo can be reached by ferry from the northern tip of Malta for just under €5 return.

Malta Ferry Terminal.

Along the way is Comino Island.

Gozo. Most of the coast is sheer cliffs.

The port is located in the city of Mgarr. From here you can take a bus to the center of the island. Tickets from Malta are valid here, moreover, I even managed to make it within 90 minutes from the moment I boarded the bus from the hotel, although the crossing took almost 30 minutes.
