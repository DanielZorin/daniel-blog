Turkey: Kemer

1 January - 11 September 2024

Kemer

In the first half of 2024, I spent quite a lot of time in Kemer - almost 5 months, minus trips to South Africa and France. The winter was even warmer than in 2023, I almost didn't even turn on the heaters.

This is what Kemer looked like on a normal day in January.

January 24, grilling meat on the balcony.

At the end of March, there were regional and local elections, in Turkey they are held simultaneously in all cities of the country. In February-March, campaign buses were constantly driving around the city. There was much less of this during the presidential elections, since the candidates were apparently well known anyway.

There were many candidates, not just one or two campaigners.

This is the current mayor of Kemer, who was eventually re-elected for a second term.

And this is a former mayor who has already ruled three times: in 1994-1999, and then in 2009-2019. Interestingly, he was in the CHP (one of the two main parties, the main opposition to Erdogan, and in the 1990s - the ruling party), and now he has rolled over to the nationalist Iyi parti. The slogan: "Love will win!"

There were also more local elections - here is a candidate for the post of head of the Camyuva district administration. A more modest campaign - by car instead of a bus.
