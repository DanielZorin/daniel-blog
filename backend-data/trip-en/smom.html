Order of Malta

12 January 2017

So, it's time again to discuss the favorite question of travelers: what is considered a country? The Order of Malta has a lot of signs of a state: in addition to its own currency, postage stamps, license plates and similar little things, it has diplomatic relations with most countries of the world, and its own passports, that are issued to the top knights of the order and are fully recognized in developed countries. It just has no territory of its own since Napoleon expelled the order from Malta, and then the British took the island. The territory of the Order of Malta is two buildings in the center of Rome, which, however, have the property of extraterritoriality, that is, their status corresponds to embassies. Why this order is needed is not very clear. Most likely, this is just a toy for lovers of sonorous titles, but, as they love in the modern world, with a claim to making the world a better place. Until recently, only people of noble origin were accepted there (and Russians, Chinese and other Papuans a priori cannot be nobles by European standards), now they can be accepted by recommendation. The information about the activities of the order is vague: they write about some supposed “charity”, but we all know that helping some suffering children in Africa is just a money funnel with little real benefit to the world.
