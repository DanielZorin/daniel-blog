Barbados

18-19 November 2016

Barbados became the 100th country I visited. Unlike St. Vincent and Trinidad, there are many tourists and the infrastructure is comparable to Curacao and St. Maarten. However, Barbados is very expensive. The cheapest hotel costs more than $50. Eating on a budget like in Trinidad is also not so easy.

The airport is large and filled with flocks of foreign tourists. Barbados is the third most popular cruise departure point in the Caribbean after Puerto Rico and Miami.

They don’t like to work here, just like on other islands. These are the opening hours of the exchange office at the airport.

Prices in a Chinese restaurant. Don’t rush to open your mouth, prices are still in Barbadian dollars, to get American ones, you need to divide them by two. But still 4-5 times more expensive than in China.

You can get some rice from the cart for three dollars.

In Barbados, I swam in the sea for the first time during my trip to the Caribbean.
