USA: Washington DC, Alexandria, Arlington, College Park, Philadelphia, Camden

21-24 September 2013

Washington DC

Arriving in Washington, the capital of the American empire, I, of course, expected to receive instructions there from the insidious State Department on how to overthrow the bloody regime of Putin, break spiritual bonds and destroy Russia, as well as appropriate funding, sufficient to at least dance in church, or even leave the tomatoes on Bolotnaya Square. My disappointment knew no bounds. It turned out that the State Department was tight on money. There is not enough for a revolution in distant and unnecessary Russia, but even for a normal building. So the world’s puppeteers huddle in a gray box that looks like a Soviet research institute.

The FBI building is nicer. There is also a CIA building, it is not in Washington, but in a neighboring city, and no one is allowed within a mile of it.

Obama's motorcade. During the passage, the street leading to the White House was blocked, people stood on the sidewalk and watched (when Putin is driving somewhere, they block all passages within a kilometer radius). The cortege is very long, many, many cars, more motorcycles, and helicopters on top.

Washington is one of the "blackest" cities in the USA. Blacks have been concentrated here since the Civil War, when their champion Lincoln was based in Washington. The metropolitan area firmly holds the second-highest crime rate in the United States after Puerto Rico, although some cities in other states are more dangerous.

The capital district is not a state, it does not have representatives in Congress, so the slogan “No taxation without representation” borrowed from the leaders of the war for American independence from England, is popular here.

Alexandria

In any American McDonald's there is a sign stating that the notorious “loitering” is prohibited here. In New York, 30 minutes are allotted for food, and in Virginia - only 20. In reality, when the hall is empty, no one is watching with a stopwatch, but if you try to sit for a couple of hours with a laptop when all the tables in the hall are occupied, you can quickly get an invitation to the exit. Also in the state of Virginia, McDonald's is twice as cheap as in New York and even a little cheaper than in Moscow.

Alexandria can be reached from downtown Washington by subway. Initially, this city was part of the capital district, then the capital had the shape of a perfect square, rotated 45 degrees. The territories on the right bank of the Potomac River returned to Virginia after the outbreak of the Civil War, and remained there.

Arlington

The next station is the Pentagon. The Pentagon is covered on all sides with “no photography” signs. Photography is permitted only at the 9/11 Memorial.

Arlington Cemetery.

Veterans of various wars are buried here. Each tombstone indicates the state, rank, branch of service, years of life and war in which the veteran participated.

Guard of honor.

The most popular place is the modest grave of John Kennedy.

Next to the cemetery there is an incredibly epic monument to the heroes of Iwo Jima, also known as the US Marine Corps Memorial.

The city of Arlington has Freedom Park. Initially, there were various exhibits related to the victory over communism, in particular, a headless statue of Lenin, but now everything has been removed from there and it is an ordinary park. I asked passersby, none of them even remembered about the Lenin statue.

The only thing left are photographs of the fall of the Berlin Wall.

The town is not remarkable for anything else.

Well, except that the trees are wrapped in knitted “clothes”.
