Angoulême

The small picturesque town of Angoulême is located in the Aquitaine region.

Bordeaux

Bordeaux is the capital of the Aquitaine region and the city is quite large. It has an extensive historical center, built in a neoclassical style, which visually distinguishes it from other French cities.

I rented a small apartment in the very center on the Garonne embankment. The balcony has a great view, typical of Bordeaux.

In the summer, everything here is filled with water so that there is a reflection.

A little further north is the gigantic Quinconces square.

Trams pass among these trees.

The square itself is quite empty.

In the background stands a monument to the Girondins.

During the French Revolution, the Girondins were a group of deputies in the convention, together with the Montagnards they formed the Jacobin Club, which strongly advocated for the republic. Then a conflict arose between the two groups of Jacobins, as a result of which Robespierre and his Montagnards won, the Girondins were executed, and from this moment a period of great revolutionary terror began.

Why is this monument here? The Gironde is the department (a unit smaller than the region) in which Bordeaux is located.

Saint-Émilion

The municipality of Saint-Émilion is included in the World Heritage List. This is a rare village that is easily accessible by public transport: it’s only 30 minutes from Bordeaux by direct train, and trains run quite often, since this line popular.

The station is located about one and a half kilometers from the town itself.

The road goes through picturesque vineyards. Saint-Émilion is famous for its winemaking, like all the lands around Bordeaux.
