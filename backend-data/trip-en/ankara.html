Turkey: Ankara, Konya, Safranbolu

20-24 December 2021

Ankara

Ankara became the capital of Turkey after the collapse of the Ottoman Empire as a result of the First World War. This provincial city was where the National Assembly was convened and Atatürk's headquarters were located during the War of Independence, as Istanbul/Constantinople was occupied. As a result of the capital move, Ankara began to grow and is now the second largest city in the country with a population of over 5 million. Most of the city was built in the 20th century. Conventionally, the railway divides the center into two parts, to the north is the old part of Ankara, and to the south is the new part, where government buildings are located. This part is built in a grid along the wings, similar to the capital of Brazil. In the middle there is Kizilay Square and the metro station of the same name, where several lines intersect. But the building on the left with the turquoise dome is more interesting.

This is the mausoleum of Rumi (also known as Mevlana), the founder of the Dervish order.

The main attraction of Konya, they even bring tourist groups here, I noticed a tour in Russian (apparently, tourists from Muslim regions).

Dervishes are monks who need to observe asceticism and not have any property. Turkish dervishes are known for achieving religious ecstasy through whirling dances while wearing a white dress. Under Ataturk, the dervish orders were banned, and now these dances are officially shown as a cultural performance, although there seem to be actual practitioners (not publicly).

Safranbolu

Safranbolu is the only small town in Turkey listed as a World Heritage Site for its well-preserved historical center. In almost all cities, historical buildings have not been preserved due to very strong urbanization in the 20th century, which continues today. I have already showed the rebuilding of historical quarters in Ankara, and now you can see what the original actually looked like.

Safranbolu is a three-hour drive north from Ankara. There are no other famous tourist cities closer, it takes 7 hours to get to Istanbul, so there are practically no foreign tourists.
