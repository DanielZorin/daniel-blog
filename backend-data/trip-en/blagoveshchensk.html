Russia: Blagoveshchensk

1 March 2015

Blagoveshchensk

Blagoveshchensk is yet another "best city on earth". It can probably be proud of the fact that it is the most remote from all the other best cities on earth. The nearest one is Khabarovsk.

Although for thousands of kilometers around Blagoveshchensk there is nothing but icy desert, this city is simply an oasis.

The secret is that it is on the border with China, and previously everyone on the Russian side went across the river to buy cheap goods.

Now, on the contrary, the Chinese go to Russia for cheap chocolate and cookies.

The Chinese influence is evident mainly in the signage.

The main shopping center is called "Heavenly Huafu". On the Chinese side - just "Huafu".

Rarely does a Chinese person work in snow removal.

Otherwise it is a typical Russian city.

Two monuments to Lenin were discovered.

All life is centered around brightly colored shopping centers.

The dominance of advertising.

Strange names of establishments.

Surprisingly for such a remote city, Blagoveshchensk has quite a lot of typically Russian architecture from the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

There is a triumphal arch in honor of the capture of these territories from China following the results of the second Opium War.

Typical Soviet architecture.

There are signs for rare tourists.

The airport, like in most Russian regional centers, is very small.

The main place is the river station. You can go to China, a ticket costs about 1,500 rubles (round-trip about 2,000), so going just for lunch turns out to be very unprofitable. It’s interesting that the Chinese have their own transport, where Russians are not allowed, and something tells me that their prices are much lower.

In summer there are ferries across the river, and in winter there are regular buses.
