Brazil: Manaus, Santarém, Belém

17-23 April 2021

Manaus

The northern part of Brazil is occupied by the Amazon rainforest - a region with exotic animals and plants, where instead of roads there is the Amazon river and its numerous tributaries. In the middle of the Amazon lies the city of Manaus with a population of more than 2 million. Manaus boomed at the end of the 19th century during the rubber rush - the Hevea tree was the only source for rubber production, and it grew only in South America. When Hevea began to be grown in other tropical countries, Manaus became impoverished, and now it is an ordinary city, serving primarily as a hub for a vast region.

I arrived in Manaus on Saturday evening. The coronavirus peaked in Manaus earlier than in the rest of Brazil, and by the end of April there were almost no restrictions. In the evening, street bars and kebab shops were open.

But the next day was Sunday - a day of silence, when the streets were empty.

Walking around the city was quite uncomfortable, but I spent half a day outside the city on an excursion, so everything turned out quite well.

From Manaus I went on a standard excursion to the Amazon and the surrounding area. A tour on a large boat costs about 120 reais, and for 300 I rented a small motor boat with a guide for two right in the port. In terms of time, it was much less than on a large boat, but mostly due to a much higher speed of movement the absence of stops for lunch, a long time to collect all passengers, etc. In general, I definitely recommend this option if you are not alone.

The journey begins at the port of Manaus.

The Amazon has floating restaurants, shops and gas stations.

The first place is the confluence of waters.

Here the Rio Negro River (with dark water) flows into the Amazon (with brown water), but the currents are so strong that 20-30 kilometers of the river flow in parallel, without mixing.

Interestingly, the two rivers also have different water temperatures, which you can feel with your own hands when the boat makes a small circle near the watershed.

In Brazil, it is believed that the Amazon begins here, and the part of the river before its confluence is called Solimões. In other countries, on maps, the Amazon begins in the Andes in Peru.

Cruise on the Amazon

Foreign tourists in Russia love to travel by train to Irkutsk, where the journey takes 4 days. Brazil has its equivalent of this epic trip - a boat trip through the Amazon. The distance, of course, is much shorter, but since ships are much slower than trains, it also turns out to be long and meditative. From Manaus to Belém it takes about 4 days to sail, to Santarem (the largest city along the way) - about 30 hours. Surprisingly, I couldn’t find any reliable information about schedules and prices on the Internet, let alone book one. Everything is done the old fashioned way, go to the port with your feet and ask what’s going on.

The port of Manaus looks something like this from the inside. As you can see, the ships here are far from being cruise ships.

My ship "Anna Carolina II".

Instead of a reserved seat, there is a common deck where you can hang a hammock (which you need to buy yourself).

At night. By the way, there are quite a lot of sockets - extension cords are under the ceiling.

It is forbidden for two people to sleep in the same hammock.

But there is also an analogue of a train coupe - a separate cabin. Travel costs 200 reais per person, cabin costs 400 reais for everyone. The cabin pleasantly surprised me. I modestly expected at least an outlet, and there was even an air conditioner and a refrigerator, like in a hotel.

Our ship had two decks with hammocks several cabins, the lower deck was occupied mainly by cargo the engine room.

I heard that in recent years in Russia on trains it has become possible to eat normal food in the restaurant car at a reasonable price. There is no such progress on Amazon yet, so the market fills the empty niche. On the ship itself there is only a stall where they sell drinks, cookies and noodles, which can be poured with boiling water. There seemed to be a dining room on the middle deck, where advertisements promised lunch and dinner, but it did not work. But at stops they sold food everywhere, as used to be the case everywhere in Russia at railway stations. The standard is lunch with chicken, pasta and rice, R$12. In the middle there is a local sweet - cassava macacheira pie.

Santarém

The city of Santarém is located in the middle of the Amazon, somewhere between Manaus and Belém. In Portugal there is a city with the same name near Lisbon. Santarém has about 300 thousand people, which seems like a lot, but it turned out to be the only Brazilian city where I couldn’t use Uber due to the lack of cars, but fortunately the alternative app 99 worked. The main attraction of Santarém is Alter do Chão beach.

It takes about 40 minutes by bus to get to Alter do Chão. Formally, this is part of Santarém, but it looks like a village. Most of the roads are dirt.

Belém

Near the mouth of the Amazon stands the city of Belém - the end point of the trans-Amazonian routes. The Amazon is so large that the water at the mouth is fresh even tens of kilometers from the coast, and Belém itself is still a little deeper. I was flying to Manaus with a transfer in Belém, so I saw the Amazon for the first time here.

Around Belém, as in other places in the Amazon, there are endless forests with winding rivers.

If you look closely, you can see houses along the banks, that is, the rivers are like roads here.

On the other side is the açaí market - açaí berries are brought here by river and sold in bulk, but this happens at 5 in the morning (like selling tuna at the market in Tokyo). During the day there is nothing here.

In Belém you can try açaí in its pure form - just crushed berries. Behind the glass you can see how a person is right now preparing this drink and pouring it into bottles. It is stored for only a few hours, so only frozen açaí is exported to other regions. In its pure form it’s not at all like what I’ve tried before - it doesn’t taste sweet, but like some kind of tart herbal mixture.
