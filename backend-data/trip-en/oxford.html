Oxford

Oxford is the city where perhaps the world's most famous university is located. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in England.

The structure of the university is complex. Oxford has 38 colleges. Each college has its own beautiful building (or rather, a whole block of neighboring buildings), but they do not specialize in any particular subject. There are centralized faculties that accept students, say, in the field of "mathematics", and the students are somehow distributed evenly across colleges. Teachers can also be from different colleges, that is, the function of a college in the modern world is a place where students live and communicate.

To confuse everyone, one of the colleges is called University College. Half of the colleges are named after various saints.

There are a lot of tourists in Oxford, quite noticeably a lot, so it is not surprising that all the colleges are closed to the public most of the time, otherwise it would be chaos.

Some colleges that are in the very center and have the greatest historical value, are open for a couple of hours one or two days a week, mainly you can see their chapels and assembly halls. You don’t just go to the audience.

Others are simply always closed.

I felt a little awkward looking into the courtyards - after all, unlike an ordinary tourist city, which receives most of its income from tourism, tourists here are more of a nuisance.

The Bodleian Library is the main library in Oxford. The round building is one of its buildings, the Radcliffe Camera. You can only get here with a guided tour.
