Buenos Aires

Is it true that people dance tango in the streets in Argentina? Not really, tango is danced only in Buenos Aires, because there are a lot of tourists there.

Buenos Aires is square in its squareness. The streets are laid out in a smooth grid. As in other countries of South America (as well as in Germany), it is not the houses that are numbered, but the entrances. For greater convenience, after each intersection the numbering begins with the next hundred, that is, the 262nd house can immediately be followed by the 300th.

La Plata

I definitely wanted to visit the city of La Plata. How could you not want to after seeing the city plan? In such a geometrically perfect city, everything should be beautiful, everything should bloom brightly and delight the heart.

You can get to La Plata in about an hour by train for 30 rubles, and in the same amount of time by bus, but five times more expensive. The path passes through the pampas. It sounds romantic but it looks quite ordinary.

At the entrance to La Plata you can see a ring of dirty slums.

As the map promises, the streets intersect at right angles and are called numbers.

In theory, streets have normal names, but they are not used.

Despite the layout, it is easy to lose your way, because the city is very gray and monotonous.

Typical street of La Plata. To its credit, compared to typical Russian town, there are a lot of trees and cars are not parked on the sidewalk. But there is a gray fence and bad tiles.
