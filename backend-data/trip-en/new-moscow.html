15, 17, 24, 28, 30 October 2020

Zelenograd

Before the expansion of Moscow in 2011, the farthest part of the capital from the center was the Zelenograd administrative okrug. Zelenograd was built in the 1960s from scratch, and almost immediately became part of Moscow, although it is 20 kilometers from the Moscow Ring Road. Zelenograd was initially created as a city of scientific institutes and high-tech industries, but over time there were more residents than jobs, and now many people go to the center of Moscow to work either by train in the Leningrad direction, or by bus to Khovrino, Planernaya or Mitino. Zelenograd officially consists of five districts, which are divided into numbered microdistricts.

Kryukovo station takes us to the Old Kryukovo area. The railway divides the city in half, behind it is located New Kryukovo, which was annexed to Zelenograd later.

* * *

New Moscow consists of two administrative districts: Novomoskovsky / NAO (closer to the Moscow Ring Road) and Troitsky / TAO (further from the Moscow Ring Road), which are divided not into districts, like other okrugs, but into settlements (before annexation - “rural settlements”). There are 10 settlements and one urban district (Shcherbinka) in the New Moscow Okrug, and in the Troitsky Autonomous Okrug there are 9 settlements and the Troitsk urban district. Before joining Moscow, most of the NAO was part of the Leninsky district, where the city of Vidnoye and Gorki Leninskie are. Most of the TAO was in the Podolsk district, and the rest was in the Naro-Fominsk district. In the settlements themselves, the territory is not further subdivided, but includes populated areas: villages, urban-type settlements and one city - Moskovsky (literally: "belonging to Moscow"). If earlier the addresses there began with “Moscow region, Moskovsky city,” now it has become even more fun: “Moscow city, Moskovsky city, Moskvitina street.” The Moskovsky settlement (of which Moskovsky is the capital) starts from the Moscow Ring Road and includes several metro stations: Govorovo, Rumyantsevo and Salaryevo. I still remember the first time I came to Salaryevo in 2016, when buses began to run from there to Vnukovo airport, and there was an open field around the station, and now there is the Salaris shopping center and new residential areas.

Troitsk is one of two (together with Shcherbinka) full-fledged cities in New Moscow. The Troitsk Administrative Okrug begins with it - the largest in area and the smallest in population. The Troitsk okrug makes up as much as 43% of the area of ​​Moscow, and only 130 thousand people live there, half of them in Troitsk itself. Troitsk is a science city, we can say that it is the southern sister city of Zelenograd.

Troitsk consists of two parts: the new center, in the south, where the institutes are, and the old center, near the former estate and worsted factory. I drove into the city from the south, here on the square there is a sign and the Trinity Church in Troitsk.
