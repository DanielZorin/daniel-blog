UAE: Sharjah, Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Fujairah, Khor Fakkan

5-8 May 2018

Dubai

The trip to the UAE turned out to be the most spontaneous of all time. I bought a last-minute tour for $400 for two, which is significantly cheaper than plane tickets, and a couple of days later I arrived in the now visa-free Emirates. The disadvantage of this approach is that it was not I who chose how much time to allocate for this or that city, but I adjusted my plan to three days, which, of course, was not enough. I hope I can visit this country again someday.

Sharjah

My hotel was located in the city of Sharjah. It is northeast of Dubai, but in fact these cities merge smoothly into each other.

I didn’t choose the hotel; it was inexpensive, but located far from the center and the bus station. Taking into account the fact that I constantly traveled around the country, if I had chosen it myself, I would have settled in a more expensive/worse place, but closer. But the hotel itself was quite good.

The area around is new, they have not yet had time to lay asphalt on all the streets except the main one. Only after a couple of kilometers there is asphalt not on the main road.

However, there are plenty of shops around, as well as small cafes, canteens, and shawarma shops.

The main road.

Public transport in Sharjah is much worse than in Dubai. There are few buses, and for the two of us, even in terms of money, it was more economical to take a taxi to the center.

The UAE is a country of honest taxi drivers; they have never tried to drive without the meter.

Fujairah

On the third day I visited the emirate of Fujairah, whose main city is not on the northern, but on the eastern coast of the country. There is a direct bus from Sharjah that does not go via Dubai. The road crosses the central desert part of the country.
