Assisi

Assisi is a small city included in the World Heritage List as the birthplace of the Franciscan friars.

Assisi is located in the Umbria region, most of which is occupied by the Apennines.

Landscapes of Umbria.

The station is located in the town of Santa Maria degli Angeli, and Assisi itself is a couple of kilometers away on a hill. Santa Maria has a lot of inns combined with restaurants, just like in the good old days. I stopped in one of these.

For dinner (since I couldn’t have a proper lunch in Ancona) I tried Strangozzi pasta, this is pasta from the Umbria region.

Perugia

Perugia is the capital of the Umbria region. The city is located on top of a hill, so there are many picturesque views.

The station is located at the bottom, to get to the center you need to climb to the very top of the hill. Minimetro will help us with this.

These are the kind of cars that automatically, without a driver, go up the hill using cable traction.

The only analogue of this transport is the cable tram in San Francisco.

And here I am in the center.

Pisa

Until recently, the Leaning Tower of Pisa was apparently the most popular attraction I hadn't seen. Now, probably, this title goes to Machu Picchu.

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is the bell tower of the cathedral. In the encyclopedia that I read as a child, it was written that it slopes every year by several millimeters, but this information is outdated - in 2008 it was stabilized.

Even if it weren't slanted, it would still be quite unique in terms of architecture.

It’s cool that there are always a bunch of people taking pictures around the tower, as if supporting it.

In the evening too.

In the photographs, it seems that people took pictures on this lawn, but now it is empty, so you can take such a beautiful photo.

There are fewer people here in the morning.

Apart from the tower, there is nothing particularly interesting in Pisa.

Well, maybe the square with the town hall.

This city has one unusual feature - it does not seem to be very residential (in the central part).

I wandered here during the day, and in the evening, and in the morning, and it was always empty.

It is noticeable that almost all the windows are battened down, and the balconies are also empty.

In principle, it’s similar in Venice, but it’s not so noticeable there, because Venice itself is unusual, there are all sorts of canals. And here it is just an ordinary Italian city, only empty.

Only rare restaurants become oases of partying.

Florence

Florence is the capital of the Tuscany region, one of the most popular cities in Italy. I was here a long time ago, in 2009.

It’s better to come here in winter; in summer there are really too many people. To even enter the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore, you have to stand in line for about 30 minutes.

The most recognizable landmark of Florence is this dome.

Venice

I was flying early in the morning from Venice airport. Since I was in Venice on my last trip, I planned to spend the night in a hostel, but of course I couldn’t help but walk a little around the center of such an epic city .

Venice is very expensive in the summer. A place in a hostel cost 30 euros, and this was not in Venice itself (on the island), but in Mestre (on the mainland). I have never been to Mestre before.

Railway station. It takes 10 minutes to get to Venice and costs one and a half euros.

I went towards the center. Before the Grand Canal there are quite a few people on the streets, although not zero, as there were at night in the winter.

Life here is mainly concentrated in the squares where the restaurants operate.
