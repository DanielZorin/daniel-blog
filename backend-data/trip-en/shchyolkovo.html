Losino-Petrovsky

It is unlikely that you have heard about the city of Losino-Petrovsky, even if you live in Moscow. This is a small city with a population of about 25 thousand, located quite close to the Moscow Ring Road, but without a railway. The nearest Monino station in the village of the same name belongs to the territory of the city of Shchyolkovo; it takes about an hour to get to Monino, since the train goes in an arc.

Monino was, is and will be a forge of heroes!!!

Rus.

After traveling a few kilometers by bus, I end up in Losino-Petrovsky. The city has such a strange name because it grew out of the Losin manufactory founded by Peter I. It became a city in Soviet times, and then it acquired its modern name (before that - Petrovskaya Podgorodnaya Sloboda).

There is a lot of Stalinist architecture in this city.
