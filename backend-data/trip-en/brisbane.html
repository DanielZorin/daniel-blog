Australia: Darwin, Adelaide, Brisbane, Gold Coast

18-24 March 2019

Darwin

The first city I visited in Australia was Darwin, the capital of the Northern Territory. This city is very different from other major cities in the country because of its climate, which is similar to neighboring Indonesia: very hot and humid. Around Darwin, tropical forests stretch for many kilometers, which in the interior of the mainland after some time turn into desert.

The area around Darwin is home to a huge number of saltwater crocodiles. Once upon a time, these very dangerous animals lived throughout Southeast Asia, but gradually people exterminated them in more densely populated areas so as not to have unpleasant encounters. Now they remain mainly in Northern Australia and off the coast of New Guinea. Swimming in the sea and rivers around Darwin is very dangerous. Not far from the city, for 30 dollars you can ride a boat down the river, where crocodiles will jump out of the water to catch meat on a fishing rod, however, getting there without a car is problematic. You can also see crocodiles in captivity in the very center of Darwin.

Here you can plunge into a pool with a crocodile in a glass container, although it is very expensive - about $100.

You can touch the little crocodile. It feels very smooth and cool to the touch.

You can also leave meat for small crocodiles on a fishing rod.

All in all, good entertainment for a few hours.

In Brisbane, as in Adelaide, there is an extensive system of suburban trains; they run quite often through the city center, then reaching distant suburbs.

Travel costs are reasonable (almost an hour to Glass House for $4), with the exception of the airport train for $20 AUD. If you have an arrival/departure during the day, you can take a free shuttle to a nearby shopping center, from where regular city buses depart.

Glass House Mountains

Just north of Brisbane, Glass House National Park features volcanic hills somewhat reminiscent of the karst of southern Thailand and Malaysia. Getting here is easy in an hour by train to Beerburrum or Glass House Mountains stations. I came to Beerburrum with the goal of climbing the hill of the same name.

Station.

The height is about 300 meters, the ascent is along a concrete path like this, but quite sharp.

Gold Coast

They are fed, so they hang out all the time, and when food is handed out, a lot of people gather. They usually swim in coastal waters.

Children swim, although this should be done carefully; the pelican's beak looks menacing.

Visually, the city is very similar to Miami.

The same white apartment buildings, palm trees, avenues and a long beach.
