In June 2022, I boarded a plane and flew Through the Looking Glass - a magical land where the Firebird lives in a palace and everyone always wins, no matter what happens. Just in case, I cleaned the phone, and I didn’t use VPN, so for all three days I was, like the inhabitants of Through the Looking Glass, in a wonderful suspended animation of isolation from the real world.

It's a strange feeling. There was an almost full bottle of Martini left at home from New Year's. There were fish and scallops in the freezer. It’s as if I’m looking at abandoned Pripyat, only in its role is the place where I myself once lived.

Russia, it turns out, is the birthplace of Pushkin, but I thought it was associated with another character with a similar surname.

In the grocery store, the assortment decreased somewhat and everything became more expensive (at that time Coca-Cola had not yet run out), and converted to dollars the price increased very much. At that time, it was theoretically possible to live in Russia and work remotely, but it was very unprofitable.

Beslan

Vladikavkaz airport is located in the neighboring small town of Beslan, which would still be a small town near the airport if not for the famous terrorist attack with the seizure of a school in 2004.

Z is right at the exit from the airport.
