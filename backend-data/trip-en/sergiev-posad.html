Russia: Sergiev Posad

26 July 2014

Sergiev Posad

It’s surprising that living in Moscow, in 24 years I have never been to the Trinity-Sergius Lavra. Even when I started touring Russian regions, I kept putting off a one-day trip here, because it would be done in due time. And finally, it happened, this city of the Golden Ring was also conquered.

At the Lavra there was a huge queue for the relics and a smaller queue for water. A little later, the line for water cleared up.

The frescoes clearly show what happens to sinners.

The design of the facades pleases with sophistication.

Spider-Man has settled on the outskirts of the Lavra.

Boris Godunov and the little-known first Generalissimo of Russia Shein are buried in the Lavra (Anton Ulrich, Suvorov and Stalin also held this title).

Surprisingly, right in front of the monastery there is a monument to the main atheist - Lenin.

The hammer and sickle hang over the administration.

And the streets have Soviet names.

But there is already spirituality (tm) in the design.

The signs are in the correct font.

Monasteries and elders are painted on transformer boxes.

There is an information center with a recognizable logo.

The signs were translated into English, but it was a little strange.

Otherwise, it’s nice provincial Russia, only more well-groomed than usual.
