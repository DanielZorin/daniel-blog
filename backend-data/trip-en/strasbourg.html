Strasbourg

Despite the German name, Strasbourg is located in France (but from 1871 to 1918 it was in Germany). It is symbolic that the institutions of the European Union were placed on the border of two long-time warring countries. The most recognizable is the European Parliament. It also has a second residence in Brussels. Parliament meets in Strasbourg once a month, with additional sessions held in Brussels, closer to the rest of the EU authorities.

The flags in front of the building are arranged according to the names of countries in alphabetical order, with the name of each country spelled in its language. So, the flags of Spain and Ireland are next to each other, because Spain = Espana, Ireland = Eire. Finland = Suomi next to Sweden = Sverige.
