Bolgar

Bolgar is the ancient capital of Volga Bulgaria and a World Heritage Site. It was added to the list in 2014, after my first trip to Kazan. The main problen is that it is not very easy to get here - it takes more than three hours by bus from Kazan, because you need to make a long detour to go around the confluence of the Kama and Volga. Buses rarely run. Therefore, it is very advisable to be here in the summer, during the period of river navigation, when passenger hovercraft run from Kazan to Bolgar every day. It takes about two hours to travel, and the schedule is especially convenient for tourists: in the morning to Bolgar, in the evening back. A round-trip ticket costs just over 700 rubles.

The boat ride itself is interesting and picturesque.

City of Nizhny Uslon.

Rocks on the banks of the Volga.

Bolgar is located near the confluence of the Kama and Volga. This is a very wide confluence, the mouth of the Kama is 15-20 kilometers in size. The port is located one and a half kilometers from the city of Bolgar, and 5-6 kilometers from the archaeological zone. After the ship arrives, you can take a bus to the museum for 30 rubles, then back at the end. Buses also run from the museum to the White Mosque, the distance between them is 3 kilometers. In general, it is very convenient and cheap for tourists.

The ferry terminal is empty, there is nothing there, tickets are sold at the entrance to the ship. There is a new ferry terminal right next to the museum, but for some reason it is not used for its intended purpose.

Bulgars are a Turkic people. Initially they lived in the steppes of the Black Sea region (eastern Ukraine and southern Russia), then some of them moved to the Danube, created the Bulgarian kingdom there, gradually mixed with the Slavs, and began to speak the Slavic language (albeit without cases) - these are the inhabitants of the modern country of Bulgaria. Other Bulgars moved east to the Volga region, where Volga Bulgaria existed in the 10th to 13th centuries. Back in the 10th century, they converted to Islam, in honor of which this monument now stands.

Volga Bulgaria was conquered by the Mongols in the 13th century along with Russia. Subsequently, it was part of the Golden Horde, after its collapse - the center of the Kazan Khanate (with the capital already in Kazan). At the beginning of the 15th century, the city of Bolgar was destroyed by Russian princes. Later, an Orthodox monastery was founded in its place, and ten kilometers from it - the new city of Spassk. In 1935, Spassk was renamed Kuibyshev, and in 1957 it was flooded during the construction of the Kama Reservoir. New Kuibyshev was founded on the site of the small village of Bolgar near the ruins of the ancient city, and in 1991 it was renamed back to Bolgar. Locals call it both Bolgar and Bulgar, with the emphasis in both cases on the first syllable.
