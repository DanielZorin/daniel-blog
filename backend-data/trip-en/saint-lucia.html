Saint Lucia

30 April - 2 May 2022

Saint Lucia is a country consisting of one island of the same name. The plan was simple - I fly to the main airport in the south of the island, spend the night in a hotel next to the airport, and on the next day I go to the capital (from where I leave the next morning by ferry), along the way visiting Soufriere - a city with famous coastal cliffs. But one serious obstacle arose - that day was Sunday. And Sunday in the Caribbean is a disaster, just like in Brazil and Oceania. Nothing works, transport doesn't work. I don’t know what they’re doing here on Sunday, apparently they’re lying on the sofa. In Europe, offices and some shops are also closed on Sunday, but all entertainment venues and restaurants are open so that people can go to them.

The room that I rented through the usual airbnb near the airport turned out to be a studio apartment.

I came to the bus stop to look for something going towards Soufriere. There were actually minibuses, but they all went only to the nearest suburbs within 5 km. Someone said that there was no chance at all to go to Soufriere, someone said that theoretically there are chances, but you need to wait a couple of hours. As a result, after an hour and a half, I despaired and went to another stop, from where buses went straight to Castries (the capital) along a shorter road along the eastern coast of the island.
