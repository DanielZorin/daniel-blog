Melbourne

Great Ocean Road

The Great Ocean Road is about 300 kilometers along the rocky ocean coasts from the outer suburbs of Melbourne to Port Campbell. The easiest way to get there is to take a tour from Melbourne, which costs about $70.

Although the route officially starts in Torquay, the first stop is in the town of Anglesey, where the road actually approaches the ocean (beach in the distance).

Hobart

Tasmania is a small island south of Australia, named after Abel Tasman, the discoverer of New Zealand. Tasman started from Mauritius and passed through the sea to the south of the whole of Australia, without noticing it, only got to Tasmania, went around it from the east, found the strait north of it with strong winds, and turned further east to New Zealand. The next time Europeans returned here was 150 years later.

Tasmania was also settled by convicts initially. The climate here is milder than in most of Australia. Tasmania is approximately at the latitude of the South Island of New Zealand. At the end of April there was a golden autumn here.

Hobart is a relatively small, cozy city of 250 thousand people.
