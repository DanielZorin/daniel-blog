Thailand: Pattaya, Chanthaburi, Trat, Koh Chang

17-29 November 2015

Pattaya

Good guys go to heaven, bad guys go to Pattaya. If you are already 50 years old, then this is good advice.

There are two main categories of tourists in Pattaya. The first is older men who meet Thai (as well as visiting Burmese and Cambodian) girls here for the purpose of relationships of varying degrees of duration. The photo shows them hanging out. If you see a young man or woman of any age, then this is almost certainly a tourist from Russia, because only Russian travel agencies thought of selling tickets to a sex resort in the beach holiday category.

There is also a small third category - those who live here for months. That's reasonable: if you absolutely need to live near the seashore, then in Thailand Pattaya will be the best option for the price. This is essentially a big city with all the benefits of civilization and a beach. Phuket is also well developed, but it is objectively more expensive there, and the other resorts are very small and distant, and also expensive. Those who do not need the beach go to live in cities in the north instead of Pattaya.

Pattaya is quite similar to the Philippine sex resort Angeles, only the scale is larger, and there is no beach in Angeles, so there are no beach tourists. Everything is as usual: the cheapest prostitutes can be found on the long promenade (Beach road), the more expensive ones work in bars, and the most expensive ones work in strip clubs and go-gos.

The offer is truly extensive.

In Pattaya I witnessed one of the main Thai holidays - Loy Krathong.

The point is to launch a krathong boat into a river or sea, thereby symbolically letting go of negative emotions.

Krathongs are made from leaves, flowers, bread and rice so that they do not clog the water for a long time.

In the evening, at low tide, a fleet of krathongs can be seen.

Koh Laan

Those who don't like Pattaya beach go to the neighboring island of Koh Laan (or Larn). The ferry costs 30 baht one way, but many do not want to wait an hour before departure and rent a boat for much more.

Laan Island is small, there is a town near the ferry terminal and a couple of roads to the beaches in the southern and eastern parts.

There is a Thai temple here too.

At the entrance there are figures of soldiers with machine guns.

This is Tawan Beach. There is not a single large hotel on it, but it is still very crowded.

And in the northeast it is even better, although there is no road indicated on the map - perhaps you can only get here by water. And maybe during the day there are also a lot of people here.

Koh Laan is a good island to be “in nature” after the densely built-up Pattaya, although if you just need to swim, then in my opinion the Pattaya city beach is not much worse.

Trat

Trat is the last large coastal city before the border with Cambodia (although the crossing still takes more than an hour along a narrow strip of Thai territory off the coast) and a transit point for trips to the islands of Chang, Kut and Mak. Koh Chang

I went to Koh Chang Island one day from Trat before crossing the border into Cambodia with the goal of lying on the beach. Koh Chang is a large and mountainous island with civilization only along the shore.

The ferry arrives on the east coast, and all the beaches are on the west. Minibuses on this island (as on the rest of the Thai islands) are not cheap and essentially operate like taxis, and not as regular public transport.

The main place is called White Sand Beach.

There are mostly more or less large hotels here.

Beach.

There is almost no space on the beach, the strip of sand is terribly narrow.

Swing for taking photos for Instagram.

The beach is normal, the water is clean, but I wouldn’t hang out here for a long time.

The food is ok; in the evening they sell pancakes, pad thai and other classic dishes along the road.

We hitchhiked back to the port, fortunately there is only one road and there is decent traffic. Hitchhiking on the islands of Thailand is good.
