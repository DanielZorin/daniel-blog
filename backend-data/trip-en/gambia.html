Gambia: Serekunda, Banjul

5-6 August 2024

Serekunda

Gambia is a small country occupying a strip of land along the river of the same name. The British colonized this piece of land, surrounded on both sides by French territories (modern Senegal). The population and tourists are concentrated near the mouth of this river, where there are beaches on the Atlantic Ocean.

The largest city is called Serekunda. It has an airport and most of the beaches (the beaches continue to the neighboring city of Sukuta, but for simplicity I will not separate them). The hotel was slightly off the main road in a residential area. It looks like this.

The border between Gambia and Senegal is legendarily trashy, some say it's the worst border in West Africa. That's one of the reasons (besides speed) I flew to Gambia. But the way back was fun too. About 15 kilometers before the border, there was a checkpoint. There, the men from our shared taxi were sent for inspection (women and children were ignored). I had the biggest backpack, which the officers inspected thoroughly for drugs, pulling everything out to the very bottom. When they found a bag of medicine at the bottom, they said that in Gambia you must have a prescription for all medicines, tsk-tsk-tsk. I read about this scam, that they extort money like this when entering from Senegal at customs. I reacted somehow very indifferently, like "come on sir, it can't be". And in the end they didn't insist, they just gave me all my things and let me go. At the border itself they took me to a Gambian security officer, who was sitting in a house in the back of the border checkpoint. We had a heart-to-heart talk about life. He quickly figured out that I was lying in response to his question about whether I liked Putin. I had to sing "I am Russian" to be convincing. After that they let me go and I went to Senegal.
