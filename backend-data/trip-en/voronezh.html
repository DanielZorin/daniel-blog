Voronezh

I was in Voronezh back in 2005, but I had no photographs or special knowledge about the city itself (I was there for the school Olympiad). You can get to Voronezh by fast train in 6 hours or fly by Pobeda plane for 1000 rubles. This is a fairly large city, about 10 years ago it cleverly broke into the honorable category with a population of more than a million due to the expansion of the city limits. As in other cities of the Black Earth Region, the historical center was almost completely destroyed during the War, so now in Voronezh there is a triumph of the Stalinist Empire style.

Modern Voronezh is one of the most uncomfortable cities in Russia, similar to Chelyabinsk or Volgograd. Most of the streets in the center have 5 lanes, cars drive along them at high speed, there are very few pedestrian crossings, you need to constantly make detours. Unlike Chelyabinsk, there is no “Arbat” here. Well, more precisely, there is, but it’s kind of empty.

Voronezh is located on the hills, thanks to this, firstly, a significant part of the center consists of the private housing, and not of high-rise buildings, as in other cities: on the hills it is simply not very possible to build high-rise buildings according to a template. Secondly, in the city you can find observation platforms with good views, such as on Victory Square.

A visit to Voronezh would be incomplete without a trip to the other side of the Voronezh Reservoir, glorified by the Sektor Gaza group in the song of the same name. Sektor Gaza is the Russian name of the Gaza Strip, and also sounds like "The sector of gas", and this district got this unofficial name because of the air pollution. The song's chorus started like "Sektor Gaza - you won't survive until 40! Sektor Gaza - put on your gas mask!" Since 1992, the chances of living to forty in this area have increased significantly, since, unlike the Soviet era, factories at least minimally monitor harmful emissions. The industrial complex was built immediately after the war, so aesthetically the area looks good - there are many classic cultural centers and decent residential buildings (at least in the central part).
