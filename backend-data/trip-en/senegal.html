* * *

The main attraction of Dakar is Gorée Island. It is listed as a World Heritage Site, as it is an example of a colonial city of the 18th century. There were few colonies in Africa at that time, so such places are rare. The rare colonies and coastal forts at that time were mainly used for the slave trade - slaves from the mainland were collected here and then transported by ships to America.

Ferries go to the island, but not very often - about once every 2 hours. And you have to come well in advance, otherwise you won't get on the next ferry, because places are limited.

The island is very small, you can walk from one end to the other in about 20 minutes, and across in 5-10.

Mbour

Mbour is a large city south of Dakar. I stopped there to see Senegal outside the capital, and to visit the nature reserve nearby. The city turned out quite bad...

I went from the border in this shared taxi. It is called sept-place (seven seats).

From the inside, it looks something like this. This is the most common form of transport in West Africa. In English, they are also called bush taxis.

A Senegalese village.

You can often see traditional houses with thatched roofs.

The road goes through the Saloum River delta - a World Heritage Site, by the way.

Arriving in Mbour, I spent about an hour and a half looking for a hotel. There was nothing around the bus station. There were several hotels on the Internet, but they were very far away and expensive. In the end, I found this trash. The worst part was that the water didn't work. They gave me a couple of large canisters to pour water from. Well, at least the air conditioning worked, but for $35 I would have liked something better. Perhaps the only really bad and expensive hotel in Africa for all my trips so far.

Bandia

Not far from Mbour there is a small reserve called Bandia. It is easy to get to it, since the entrance is right on the main highway going to Dakar, you can just take any passing bus, and walk from the highway in 10 minutes. So I didn't miss the opportunity to go on safari in West Africa too.

Entrance gate.

Prices. Entrance costs $20, plus you need to hire a car with a guide. I quickly teamed up with other tourists, and in the end I paid 40 euros for everything - it was the cheapest safari ever.

There are several enclosures near the entrance. Turtles live in one.

Hyenas live in another. Predators are not allowed to roam freely, because they could eat too much.

The nature is beautiful, everything is green.
