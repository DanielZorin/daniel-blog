Iran: Rasht, Masuleh, Fouman, Ardabil, Tabriz

28-31 August 2022

Rasht

The north of Iran, off the coast of the Caspian Sea, is quite popular among local tourists due to the climate - it is not a desert, but rather a damp mountainous area, similar to Transcaucasia. You can see it very clearly when you turn from Tehran onto Rasht, the road goes down sharply, and dense forests begin around you. Rasht is the largest city in the region, although it's 20 kilometers from the seashore (there are only small towns on the shore).

The city has some European (or rather Turkish) flair, thanks to the presence of a pedestrian tourist center.

The benches are standing.

But there are practically no restaurants, which distinguishes very poor countries from middle and rich ones. The entire center is full of shops, and only in the side streets do shawarma shops open, where you can buy something to eat, mostly for takeaway.

Masuleh

There are two interesting places in the mountains near Rasht. The first is the historical village of Masuleh. There are not many places in Iran that have preserved traditional architecture, and this is one of them. The drive from Rasht takes about an hour.

The village is located on the slope of a mountain, somewhat reminiscent of Jiufen.

Eagle on the hill.

The main unique feature of Masuleh is that here the roofs of the houses form a sidewalk for the street above.

That's why everyone takes pictures right on the roofs.

Masuleh turned out to be almost the most touristy place in Iran; all the streets are simply packed with local tourists taking selfies.

Ardabil

Moving further from Rasht towards the Turkish border, I initially planned to go back a little towards Tehran to the city of Qazvin, and from there along the main highway to Tabriz. But I changed my mind and decided to go only forward, along the seashore through another regional center - Ardabil. It's a little shorter, the road is more interesting, but there is little public transport, on the other hand, taxis in Iran are super cheap.

The shore of the Caspian Sea!

The road runs along the seashore to the north to the border with Azerbaijan. In the city of Astara, divided by the border into two cities of the same name, I changed to another taxi to Ardabil. After this, the road goes along the border through the mountains for a long time. On the right is Azerbaijan.

Tabriz

Tabriz is one of the largest cities in Iran and the capital of Iranian Azerbaijan. Half of historical Azerbaijan is located in Iran; even more people speak Azerbaijani here than in independent Azerbaijan. Interestingly, Azerbaijani was the lingua franca in this region until the 20th century, and many dynasties of Iranian rulers spoke Azerbaijani, and Persian was the language of the common people (much like the nobility spoke French in the Russian Empire).

I left the taxi on the outskirts of Tabriz near the final metro station, there is also a park with El Goli Lake and a small pavilion.
