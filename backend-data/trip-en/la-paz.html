Bolivia: Copacabana, Coroico, La Paz

6-15 April 2021

Copacabana

A couple of hours drive from La Paz is the famous Lake Titicaca - the highest lake in the world (altitude about 3800 meters). Titicaca lies on the border of Bolivia and Peru, and the Peruvian part is more popular, but I could not get there due to the closure of the borders. The main Bolivian city on the lake is called Copacabana and is located on a fairly large peninsula with a narrow isthmus along which the border with Peru passes. From Bolivia, in order not to take a detour and also cross the border, you can get to the peninsula by ferry across the lake.

The road from La Paz to the ferry is almost flat and level, only at the very end there is a descent to the shore along a serpentine road.

Coroico is the case when it is not the city itself that is interesting, but the road to it. The drive from La Paz takes about two hours, and the elevation difference is colossal - from 3500 to 1500 meters. It was especially difficult to go down.

* * *

The main feature of La Paz, which makes this city so unique and unlike any other, is the cable cars. In recent years, 10 cable car lines have been built, they are united into a single network with transfer nodes, and cover all the main areas of La Paz and neighboring El Alto. One ticket costs 3 bolivianos, each transfer 2 bolivianos. Thanks to so many cable cars, in La Paz you can see a truly innumerable variety of panoramic views; the observation decks that I would have gone to 10 years ago, when there were no cable cars, have practically lost their meaning - every second of the trip around the city is like an observation deck. I visited all the stations on all the lines, but started with the Blue and Green lines leading to the southern part of La Paz.

* * *

The cities of El Alto and La Paz form a single entity. El Alto, as the name suggests, is located at altitude - namely on the Altiplano plateau, while La Paz is in a valley. The difference in altitude is very large - El Alto is at 4100 meters, and the center of La Paz is somewhere at 3600-3700. Since in Bolivia the lower the more prestigious, it turns out that El Alto is the poorest area, and this is true. Almost all the houses here are without paint. El Alto has a very long blue line, half of the purple line, and a short silver line that connects three other lines, and has no separate stations of its own.

I was passing through El Alto, on the way from Oruro to Copacabana. All buses going south and west pass through El Alto or even start from there.
