I have not yet reviewed a supermarket in Spain, but now it is very important to look at the new prices, since much has changed compared to 2018-2019, when I reviewed Germany, France, England, Ireland and Switzerland. This will be the Mercadona supermarket in the city of Murcia. Mercadona is a fairly large supermarket, but inexpensive compared to El Corte Ingles. But it’s also not as cheap as Lidl, where the concept is that they sell one brand of each product.

The selection of vegetables is ordinary. Tomatoes and cucumbers cost around 2 euros, potatoes at least 1.30.

There are quite few fruits, apparently because it’s winter. The cheapest orange is 1 euro per kilo.

Bananas 1.45 per kilo.

Champignons cost 5 euros per kilo. In Turkey, all vegetables and fruits are two times cheaper.

Meat and fish are the strong point of Spain and Europe in general. Chicken costs only 3.25 euros per kilogram, one and a half times cheaper than in Germany.

Fillet 7.40 per kilo. Not bad, although still one and a half times more expensive than Turkey.

By the way, grilled chicken is also inexpensive - 5.30.

Turkey 8.30.

Fattier for 6.10.

Large selection of fish. Salmon - 39 euros per kilo.

Elche

Near Alicante there is a small town called Elche (Elx in Valencian). It has a World Heritage Site, the palm grove (Palmeral).

I got off at the Elx Carrus train station to approach the center from the west. Closer to the center there is another station, Elx Parc. The western part is an ordinary Spanish city with a grid of streets.

The church is more like a Greek one.

From here you can clearly see the palm grove, it is very close.

The palm forest is more than 1000 years old and was built by the Arabs before the Reconquista, following the model of northern Africa.

This is the largest palm grove in Europe.

There is only one part of the palm forest near the cathedral; there are others in different parts of the city. Visible on the horizon.
