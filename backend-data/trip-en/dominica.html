Dominica

2-4 May 2022

Dominica's brand is "Nature island", as it is the Caribbean island with the lowest population density, and a significant part of it is occupied by jungle. In terms of landscapes, Dominica is more similar to Indonesia or Brazil than other countries in the region.

COVID-19 exists, get vaccinated! I also got sick: in St. Lucia I already didn’t feel well, and by the evening of the first day in Dominica I started having a strong cough; in fact, the symptoms were similar to omicron. First thing in the morning I went to get tested, but fortunately the result was negative. On the second day I felt generally better, but I still not strong enough to go around the island or rent a car (I was considering this scenario). During the first half of the day I wandered through the streets of Roseau in search of some place where I could have a tasty meal.

This turned out to be a non-trivial task - I did not find a single restaurant in the entire capital. Only a few fast food joints and canteens, something like this:

Most of the places indicated on the map do not actually exist - they probably closed during the pandemic. I went in, there were burgers, the seller ignored me for about five minutes, tenaciously making burger buns, I got tired of it and left.

Restaurant "Italy" sounds encouraging.

As a result, the most decent place in all of Dominica is good old KFC. No wonder the queue is the longest there; I had to stand for about 30 minutes.

In the afternoon, mustering my strength, I went to the center of the island. This is the village of Trafalgar.

The views are very beautiful, tropical.

Reminds me a little of Hawaii, you don’t see this often in the Caribbean.

Trafalgar Falls.

Prices in supermarkets in Dominica, as in neighboring countries, are jaw-dropping. A small apple, for some reason called Large, costs more than a dollar.

A bottle of knockoff Dr. Pepper is $7! I think this is the most expensive soda I've ever seen.

A dozen eggs cost almost $3.5! (as it turned out later, this is not the limit).

Local cucumbers are not very expensive.

Two kilograms of rice for 5 dollars.

And poor man's pasta for a dollar, and a better one for three.
