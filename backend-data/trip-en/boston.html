New York

New York is not the city I want to return to, so on the way from Boston to Chicago I did not stay overnight here, but left about 5 hours for the transfer.

In this building (Jacob K. Javits Convention Center) on that day and hour, Hillary Clinton was going to give a post-election speech admitting defeat.

People crowded to watch her drive up.

Then I turned right and walked to Fifth Avenue.

Chicago

How well I know Moscow skyscrapers in the Stalinist Empire style! It's time to look at the original, the source of inspiration. This is Chicago - the third largest city in the United States, not so often visited because it is located far from the coasts and national parks.

If you want a city with more than 10 skyscrapers, then there are two of them in America - New York and Chicago.

The stretch of Michigan Avenue where the most magnificent buildings of the first half of the 20th century are concentrated is called the Magnificent Mile.

These turrets with pointed roofs are clearly recognizable. I looked at them almost every day for 10 years.

Fire protection.

The decorations on the lower floors are magnificent.

After Trump won the election, there were protests by dissatisfied people. I found this one in Chicago.

Protesters walked along the central streets and chanted slogans. Mostly around the northern edge of downtown because Trump Tower is there.

The police did not disperse anyone, but were invisibly present the entire time. They carefully stopped traffic when the crowd was walking along the roadway; a little later, everyone stopped walking only on the sidewalks.

A squad of police officers on bicycles followed the protesters.

And the homeless veterans looked at all the events with indifference.
