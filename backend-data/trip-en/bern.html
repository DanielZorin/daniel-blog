Interlaken

The city of Interlaken, as the name implies, is located between two lakes - Thun and Brienz, although neither one is visible from the city center.

The city has an unusual layout: there are two stations, and near each there is a cluster of buildings (the western one has more), and between them there is a large field.

Paragliders periodically land on the field, jumping from the surrounding mountains.

Opposite the course are only a couple of historic hotels and casinos, from the days when Interlaken was a resort for the elite in the 19th century.

Grand Hotel.

Funicular to the top.

In 2019, Interlaken proved to be a very popular destination among Chinese tourists. There are many groups here, signs in Chinese, Chinese cafes, and hotels are also owned by the Chinese, I stayed in one for about 50 euros, which is cheap for Switzerland.

Lauterbrunnen

From Interlaken you can go to Lauterbrunnen - one of the most famous and beautiful places in Switzerland.

This small village in a valley between high cliffs became the prototype of Rivendell for John Tolkien.

In winter, everything here probably looks even greater than in summer, thanks to the snow-white trees.

In general, such places usually look brighter in photographs than in reality, since filters are added to the photographs, contrast is increased, etc. But here it’s the other way around, especially indicative of winter photographs, which on Google are somehow completely inexpressive. Maybe I was lucky enough to get a good snow day.

Almost in the city there is the Staubbach Falls.

I didn't have much time, so I didn't walk very far through the valley, just a couple of kilometers.

Bern

Finally, I visited not only all the countries of Europe, but also all the capitals: the capital of Switzerland last eluded me. Bern is not the largest city in the country (fifth after Zurich, Geneva, Lausanne and Basel), and does not even have its own full-fledged airport, fortunately it is in the middle between Zurich and Geneva, and you can get directly to both airports in an hour and a half.

Lausanne

As you approach Lausanne, you can see terraced vineyards along the shores of Lake Geneva.

These terraces are listed as a World Heritage Site.

Lausanne is the Olympic capital, as it is the headquarters of the Olympic Committee.

Looking at the map, I thought that the city was located near the lake shore that could be easily reached. But in reality it turned out that Lausanne is located on the slope of a very steep hill, almost all the streets here are at a steep angle, and walking is not so easy - the constant changes in elevation take a lot of energy.

Lausanne has a metro similar to the Haifa metro.

It also goes up and down the hill.

Geneva

The last city in Switzerland was Geneva, the main French-speaking city located right on the border with France at the end of a narrow ledge of Swiss territory. Before Switzerland joined the Schengen zone, the local airport even had two different exits - one through the Swiss border guards, the other through the French. Geneva is similar to Zurich in that here, too, Swiss show-offs such as expensive watches and boutiques immediately catch the eye.
