Ruta de las Flores

In El Salvador there is a tourist route called Ruta de las Flores (Road of Flowers). This is the road between the towns of Ahuachapan and Sonsonate, which passes through a beautiful hilly area. Conveniently, due to the high population density in El Salvador, buses between villages on this route run every 10 minutes, making it very easy to get around by public transport. In Ahuachapan I took a bus to the village of Apaneca.

The first section of the route has the most beautiful views from the window (further is also good, but trees often block the view).

San Salvador

In the nineties they killed people, and everyone ran around completely naked. There was no electricity anywhere, just fights over jeans and Coca-Cola.

As in other Latin American countries, crime in El Salvador is concentrated primarily in the capital, San Salvador. The essence of crime in El Salvador is that there are large gangs that are so powerful and influential that they control social and economic life in competition with the state. Simply put, "Mara Salvatrucha" has a number of guns comparable to the Salvadoran police/army, has a solid income from the business of transporting drugs between Colombia and the United States, and at the same time 'taxes' small businessmen who work in their territories (such as taxi drivers or street traders). In principle, this situation is not alien to the residents of Russia - in the 90s it was about the same: if you have a stall, you have to pay for the 'roof' with some kind of "Orekhovsky" or "Medvedkovsky" gang.

The practical conclusion from this is the following - even in cheap hotels for $10, there are iron bars on the door that are closed by default even during the day. And in a more expensive hotel for 30 dollars there is a whole defensive system with a remote-controlled iron door, and of course the windows are blocked.

On the streets, police officers stand in bulletproof vests and with machine guns at the ready.

Or at least with pistols.
