Bronnitsy

Bronnitsy is a city of central subordination in the Moscow region. A rare example of a city in Moscow Region where there is no railway (you need to take a minibus from the neighboring town of Ramenskoye, or directly from Moscow).

The city is small, but has certain attractions. Here is a pond with a gazebo.

There are houses with carved platbands.

Huge portrait of Messi.

Formally, the village of Bykovo also belongs to the city of Ramenskoye, although between them stands the city of Zhukovsky, which is a separate administrative unit. In Bykovo you have to walk along the streets between two rows of blank fences.

Graffiti

Architect Vasily Bazhenov, 18th century.

Zhukovsky

I have already visited the city of Zhukovsky twice in transit - on the way to Zhukovsky airport and to the MAKS air show. But I decided to see the city itself, since it was on the way. The train station is called Otdykh/"Rest".

The station itself has a children's railway, quite functional (but it seems that it was closed then due to Covid).
