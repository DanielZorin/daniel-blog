Russia: Tutayev, Yaroslavl, Pereslavl-Zalessky

10-11 September 2018

Tutayev

Tutayev is an unusual ancient city in the Yaroslavl region on the banks of the Volga. Initially, these were two cities called Romanov and Borisoglebsk on different banks, then they united and began to be called with a hyphen: Romanov-Borisoglebsk. In Soviet times, it was renamed in honor of some communist. At the same time, there is still no bridge in Tutaev; ferries cross the Volga, and the two parts are very different from each other. Buses run from Yaroslavl to both parts of Tutaev, but to the left bank quite rarely, 5 times a day, and to the right bank every 15 minutes from the northern outskirts and every hour from the station.

I arrived on the right bank (ex-Borisoglebsk). Along the highway there are Soviet panel houses. Now this part is much more populated, although initially Romanov was the more ancient and more important city.

For some reason the bus station is completely abandoned and collapsing. Buses stop just on the highway.

The central square, where the administration is, is striking in its emptiness, only parades with tanks are held.

Yaroslavl

Yaroslavl is a city where it’s nice to return. This is one of the most touristy cities in Russia; here you can easily meet guests from distant countries and the menu is in English.

The central streets are pedestrian, just like in Europe.

Pereslavl-Zalessky

Pereslavl-Zalessky for a long time remained the last city of the “Golden Ring” where I had not been. Probably because there is no railway here. On the other hand, you can get there from Moscow by bus in just an hour and a half, if you’re lucky, from the VDNKh metro station.

The main street has been turned into the main highway leading through the entire city. It’s good that at least there is a bypass road, otherwise trucks would drive here. But it still looks unnatural - the main front street and the long wait at the traffic light.
