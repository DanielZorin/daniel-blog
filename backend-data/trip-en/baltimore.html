USA: Washington DC, Baltimore

13-15 October 2021

Washington DC

The trip to Central America began in the USA, for some reason cheap tickets to Belize were only available from Baltimore (despite the fact that there was still a transfer in Miami), so I took a direct flight from Moscow to Washington DC, where I was for the first time in 2013.

On the plane they handed out a questionnaire with questions about whether I had recently been to Wuhan. I don’t know whether Aeroflot has accumulated questionnaires from two years ago, or whether someone else is not right in the head. Upon arrival, no one looked at the questionnaire or PCR tests.

There are two airports in Washington DC, international flights mainly fly to the more distant Dulles, you need to take the bus, then the metro to the center. There is a Google office near the metro.

I had the next day free to get over the side effects of the vaccine. I didn’t feel well, but I walked around the city center a little, fortunately the weather was good. Near the metro stood Flixbus, a friend from Europe, which now also goes to New York.

First of all, I took a PCR test for the flight to Belize. Passing the test in the USA is a huge problem if you are a tourist. Almost all hospitals accept only patients with local insurance or require an SSN number. There are no laboratories like Invitro in Russia, where you can enter from the street and get a test. Only at some airports it is possible to take the test, and it is very expensive (100-150 dollars). There were several other points in Washington where you could take the test right on the street for free, including in this square in the center. This is a salvation (although I had to wait almost two days for the result), but it is not a fact that this exists in other cities.

Baltimore

Baltimore is the largest city in Maryland, about an hour's drive from Washington DC. This is a large port city, which consistently ranks among the top cities in terms of crime rates (in 2019, it was in 11th place in the world for murders and second in the USA after St. Louis).
