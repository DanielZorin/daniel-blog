Georgia: Tbilisi

12-22 June 2022

Tbilisi

Although Georgia is an absolutely beautiful country, I have only been to it once eight years ago. This year has given many people a reason to visit Georgia for one reason or another.

Georgia is among the top 10 countries in the world for ease of doing business. For public services, in the very center of Tbilisi there is such a fashionable building - the "House of Justice".

The inside also looks nice.

In the spring of 2022, drawings of such a cat began to appear everywhere in Tbilisi. There has even been a theory that Russian agents mark missile targets and the location of bomb shelters this way. It turned out that it was just the bartender who spent the night drawing his cat named Shisha (hence the inscription on the side). The court fined him 10 thousand lari and banned him from painting on Georgian territory.

In mid-June, the European Union gave candidate status to Ukraine and Moldova, but refused to Georgia. Because of this, there were several protests in Tbilisi; I attended the largest one.

* * *

I decided to explore Tbilisi thoroughly district by district, like other large and important cities in the world. Tbilisi historically has 10 districts, some of which are now formally united into one. On the western bank of the Kura: Mtatsminda (old town), Krtsanisi (south of the old town) and Vake-Saburtalo (northwest of the old town). On the eastern bank: Didube-Chugureti (most of the coast opposite the center, railway station, bus station), Gldani-Nadzaladevi (northern Soviet housing), Isani-Samgori (historical Avlabari district south-eastern Soviet housing). Tourists usually hang out in the Mtatsminda area.

The most ancient part of the city is Narikala Castle on the hill and the adjacent streets below. The castle is not very high, it is quite easy to climb on foot, but you can also take the cable car, which starts from Rike Park on the other side of the river.
