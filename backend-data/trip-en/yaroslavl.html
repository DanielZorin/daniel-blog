Russia: Yaroslavl, Rostov

17-18 October 2012

Yaroslavl

Having visited many cities in central and northern Russia, I cannot say that in Yaroslavl I encountered something that I had never seen anywhere else before, but the city pleasantly stands out for its livability. The main street is similar to the main street of some city in the center of Europe. There’s even a mime (I saw it for the first time in Russia).

The embankment is polished.

There is a park on the Volga and Kotorosl spit.

It says who sponsored it.

On the stele in honor of the millennium of Yaroslavl, you can see where the sculptors had a lot of time on details, and where the deadline came and everything was completed on the last night.

On top of the confluence there is a local Cathedral of Christ the Savior, which fits very well into the monument of another popular religion.

Rostov

Here it is, Mother Russia: weeds, a path, and multi-colored domes on the horizon.

Rostov is fabulous and delightful. The Rostov Kremlin is technically not a Kremlin at all (it is a monastery, not a fortress), but it's not worse than any Kremlin.

When I come here again (and someday I will probably return), I will try to stay in the hotel located right inside the Kremlin, for the sake of authenticity.

The feeling of fabulousness arises due to the very high concentration of domes.

There are so many of them that at least a dozen can be seen from any angle.

Here, as in the Zaraisk Kremlin, you can look into the backyard.

Rostov also has another side. The border between them runs exactly in the middle of this street.
