Peterhof is an administrative part of St. Petersburg, a small city on the southern side of the Gulf of Finland, where the famous palace is located. I was there only once in 2002. There is a railway in Peterhof, but it is a bit far from the palace (and the second station is in Old Peterhof, where the buildings of the St. Petersburg State University are located). There are buses from the Baltiysky Station to Peterhof, but if you do not live near this station, it is more convenient to go from the Avtovo metro station.

Peterhof, in addition to the palace, is quite interesting; historical buildings have been preserved here. In 1944, the city was renamed Petrodvorets (apparently, so as not to have a German name - at the same time they renamed Shlisselburg to Petrokrepost), the old name was returned in 1997, but the administrative district is still Petrodvortsovy.

As in Peterhof, I was there only back in 2001 or 2002; my memories needed to be refreshed. Trains run to Pushkin from Vitebsky Station - once this section was the very first railway in Russia.
