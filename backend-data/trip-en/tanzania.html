Zanzibar

Zanzibar is an island off the coast of East Africa with an unusual history. Due to its strategic location, Zanzibar was colonized before most of Africa, and remained the main Arab outpost in the region. Yes, it wasn't only the Europeans who were involved in colonialism. Zanzibar was ruled from Oman, then became a British protectorate. In 1963 it became independent, and a year later there was a revolution, and all the Arabs were expelled or killed, and Zanzibar united with neighboring Tanganyika to form the new state of Tanzania (short for Tanganyika + Zanzibar). Now it is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Africa.

The main city is officially called Zanzibar City, but everyone calls it Stone Town, after the name of the central historic district (a World Heritage site).

Stone Town is a labyrinth of narrow pedestrian streets.

Dar es Salaam

Dar es Salaam is not the capital of Tanzania (that's the city of Dodoma in the center of the country), but the largest city. It turned out to be quite an interesting and in some places nice city.

I flew from Zanzibar by plane, only half an hour. There were great views along the way. This seems to be a rich area in the north of Dar es Salaam.
