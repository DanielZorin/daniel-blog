In addition to museums and attractions, I visited restaurants in Antalya. At first I ate next door to the place where I lived on the outskirts, and there, as usual, there was exclusively Turkish food. I ate my fill of Iskender kebabs.

After that, I started looking on maps for restaurants with different cuisines. Since Antalya is a large and touristy city, there are such restaurants, although not very many (there are more in Istanbul). The first was a Korean restaurant in the Lara area.

The food is excellent and authentic. Dishes cost 200-250 liras (7-9 dollars).

Tteokbokki.

The Uzbek-Kyrgyz restaurant is simply excellent! It is located in the very center, although a little in the side streets.

The prices are downright low, at the level of a Turkish food court.

Lagman.

I didn’t find a Chinese restaurant in Antalya (there was one, but it was closed, perhaps for the winter). There are Japanese ones in the form of sushi, although they are called pan-Asian. I chose My Asia on Konyaalti beach and enjoyed the views along the way.

The restaurant turned out to be closed, but I found another one next door.

The rolls were quite good, and the ramen was very good. The prices turned out to be low - for all this I paid 600 liras ($20), usually sushi in Turkey is much more expensive.

Finally I visited an Indian restaurant. I noticed it a year and a half ago, walking around Antalya, but it has a strange schedule - works only after 14-30. I came later on purpose.

There is excellent authentic Indian and Pakistani food here. The prices are reasonable - dishes cost about 200-250 liras. Chicken karahi:

Paneer butter masala.

There is another Indian restaurant five minutes from here, but it is in a pass-through location and the prices there are one and a half to two times higher.

Close to where I lived was Dokuma Park (and the tram station of the same name). This park was made on the territory of a former silk-spinning factory, and is now literally filled with all sorts of museums and other attractions.

A bust of Ataturk greets you at the entrance.
