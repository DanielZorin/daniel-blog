Russia: Saransk, Penza, Saratov

12-13 January 2018

Saransk

Saransk is the capital of Mordovia, a region in central Russia infamous for its endless prisons and endless election fraud. As it turns out, they also like big, kitschy buildings here, much like in Yoshkar-Ola, only more modest.

After many decades, our descendants will study the architectural heritage of the early 21st century and give this style an official name.

Well, for now it’s called kitsch.

The university building was made in the form of a parody of Moscow State University. Although Mordovian State University is also MSU.

They have more or less learned how to make temples, this area looks normal.

And then they stuck absurd antique columns on top of high-tech glass.

The local history museum is reminiscent of Romanian Parliament.

The station was painted in the brightest color they found.

Penza

Another regional center where I have never been before. This is probably one of the most inconspicuous regions - there are no sayings about Penza like “Into the wilderness, to Saratov” or “Bomb Voronezh”, and the city has not become famous for anything weird either (unlike Saransk or Yoshkar-Ola).

Saratov

Saratov is one of the most interesting cities in the Volga region with an extensive historical center.

The building of the conservatory is very atypical for Russia; in general, I would expect to see something similar in Romania or Hungary.

Nearby is a church whose domes imitate St. Basil's Cathedral.

As expected, the central street is pedestrian.

Everything on it is clean and restored.

We return to Saratov.
