Çeşme

Çeşme is a city an hour's drive from Izmir on the Aegean Sea. It was interesting to me as the departure point for ferries to Greece, but it is also quite a tourist town in itself, where people come to swim on the surrounding beaches.

Bergama

The city of Bergama contains the ruins of the ancient city of Pergamon, listed as a World Heritage Site. I want to try to visit all such places in Turkey, so I went there too, since it’s not very far from Izmir.

It took me a long time to get there - first by train to the final station of Aliaga, and from there by bus (pay with an Izmir travel card). I thought about going back the same way, but I found a minibus standing straight to Izmir and took it. But I didn’t find out where it was leaving from in Izmir, since I got out when it was passing by one of the metro stations. Intercity buses to the north pass through Bergama, but stop only at the bus station, from which it is still 20 kilometers to the city, inconveniently.

The main attraction is clearly visible from a distance - this is the Acropolis on a high hill in the north of the city.

Marmaris

From Izmir I went home by bus. So as not to have to travel all day, I split the trip into two parts and stopped for a day in the city of Marmaris. This is one of the most popular resorts in Turkey for residents of England and other European countries; There are Russians too, of course, but not as many as in the vicinity of Antalya, and the signs in shops and travel agencies here are mostly in English. Marmaris belongs to the province of Muğla (as does Fethiye).

There are a lot of small hotels in the central part of Marmaris, but there seem to be no large ones here.
