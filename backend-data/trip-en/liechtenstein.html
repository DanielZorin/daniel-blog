Liechtenstein

8 July 2013

There is a small country behind the mountains, behind the forests.

There are animals with kind eyes, there life is full of love.

There the miracle lake sparkles, there is no evil or grief.

There, the Firebird lives in the palace and gives light to people. (Four lines above are taken from a famous Russian pop song)

You can get to Liechtenstein from Switzerland by crossing the bridge over the Rhine (!), or from Austria by road.

Liechtenstein is so small that you can cross it on foot in a day from west to east. It's longer from north to south. A sign somewhere near the Austrian border, the town of Mauren.

The largest city in Liechtenstein is Schaan, where 10 thousand people live, and the capital Vaduz has half that number.

Oddly enough, there are wooden houses here.

Moreover, people have reserves of firewood in their yards, apparently for a rainy day.

Schaan has its own license plates. The red and blue flag of Liechtenstein = the flag of Vaduz, and the yellow and red flag is the flag of Schaan.

Street sign ("Customs", leads to the border).

Public toilet.

Oddly enough, in Liechtenstein people are quite religious and place crosses along the roads.

Cross and mountains.

There are vandals here too.

Someone paints hydrants like Lego.

Public transport is provided by buses.

There is one railway line connecting Austria to Switzerland via Schaan.

Pedestrian wearing a hat.

In Liechtenstein, contemporary art is everywhere.

The highest concentration is on the main street of Vaduz.

Vaduz itself.

It's livelier here.

Busloads of tourists are brought here. There is also a McDonald's on the southern end of the city.

There are apartment buildings here too, although it would seem that in Liechtenstein there should be enough space for everyone.

In churches there are books with prayers in the pews.

The small country has its own vineyard and one brand of wine.

Like any self-respecting small country, Liechtenstein makes extra money from postage stamps.

There're not many other things here.
