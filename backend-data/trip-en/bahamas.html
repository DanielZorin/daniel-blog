Bahamas: Nassau

11-12 November 2021

Nassau

Unfortunately, I didn’t manage to get to the Caribbean islands before the pandemic began (I was just about to go there in 2020), and then visiting them became problematic. But I still decided to go to one country that is almost impossible to fly into without a transfer in the USA (while I still have a visa). Actually, I thought about going on a cruise, but once again it turned out that cruises are useless. Not only does the search for cruises look as if I typed “sex porn xxx” into Google, it also turned out that all the short cruises go to the islands of Grand Bahama or Bimini, where there's nothing to see, and short cruises going to Nassau are very rare, a couple of times a month. And I didn’t see any point in paying 700 dollars for a five-day cruise where two days will be wasted at sea. So I just hopped on a plane from Miami to the Bahamas. Almost the entire population is concentrated on the capital island of New Providence.

The Bahamas is expensive - a bed in a hostel costs $50 (still cheaper than in Hawaii), however, I was there alone. In the morning I woke up early and went to explore the city. Near Nassau there is Paradise island, where expensive hotels and golf courses are located. Tourists are either there or in hotels away from the city. The islands are connected by a high bridge from which you can see a panorama of Nassau.
