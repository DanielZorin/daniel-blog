Austria: Braunau am Inn, Salzburg, Feldkirch

29 June, 4, 7-8 July 2013

Braunau am Inn

This sleepy little town of 16 thousand is located right on the German border. From Germany to Austria you can cross the bridge over the Inn River.

Braunau am Inn is famous mainly for the fact that Adolf Hitler was born here. This is the street, this is the house.

Rear view of the house. There was a hotel in the house. In those days, there were almost no short tourist trips; people often lived in hotels for years, as they do now in rented apartments. Hitler's father lived there for several years when he worked at Austrian customs on this section of the border.

The city is trying with all its might to wash itself away from such an “honorable” fellow countryman, no excursions related to Hitler are conducted, and this person is practically not mentioned in advertising brochures. Near the house there is a memorial plaque “in memory of the victims of fascism.”

Apart from its symbolic significance, the town is not particularly noteworthy and is similar to many other small towns in Germany and Austria.

Salzburg

When you visit a large number of cities in Europe, they cease to impress as much as the first time. I now often stop by somewhere just to break up a three-hour train ride in half, or to look at one particular place. At the same time, I will say that Salzburg is definitely worth a trip, as it is one of the most picturesque cities in central Europe.

It is worth going here primarily to climb to the top of the castle.

The city center is very small, just two streets sandwiched between a river and a cliff.

The cliff is almost vertical.

Rooms are being knocked out in it.

And they erect statues there.

As in Vienna, Salzburg has horse-drawn carriages for tourists.
