Liepaja

Liepaja is the third largest city in Latvia on the western edge of the country. It's a three hour drive from Riga. In the summer people come here to the beach, and in the fall the city empties out.

The emptiness here is felt for another reason: since 1991, the population of Liepaja has decreased by 40%. On the street leading from the station, half the houses look abandoned.

On the outskirts of Liepaja there is a former closed military town of Karosta. The withdrawal of military units from here made a significant contribution to the decrease in the city's population in the 90s. Now this place is publicly accessible, which makes it possible to take a short trip back in time through a semi-abandoned residential area and the remains of fortifications dating back to the times of the Russian Empire.

Before the revolution, Karosta was called Port Alexander III (and Liepaja was called Libava), the border with Germany was close, that’s why fortifications were made. In Soviet times, the military base was built with perfectly recognizable Khrushchev buildings.

There are areas with five-story buildings throughout the Baltic states, including on the outskirts of the capitals, and they are often disadvantaged, so Karosta is not unique in this regard. It looks no worse here than in any other similar area.
