6, 16-17 March 2023

Ankara

I flew to the UAE via Ankara, and since there were no convenient connections, both times I spent the night in the city and had time to take a walk. I liked Ankara since last time, it was interesting to see what has changed in a year and three months.

The weather was great, although chilly.

On the second day I walked around the museums in the center of Ankara. The most popular museum here is archaeological (Museum of Anatolian Civilizations).

It contains exhibits related to the most ancient states in Turkey, mainly those that existed before the Roman Empire.

In particular, there are many items from the two most famous ancient sites of central Turkey - Çatalhöyük (near Konya) and Hattuşa (near Ankara), which are quite difficult to get to.

The elegant building houses an art museum - not such a common occurrence in Turkey (there is none in Antalya, for example).

Painting in Turkey appeared quite late, during the era of Tanzimat modernization (1840-1860s), when imitation of Western Europe became popular (at the same time they began to build palaces like Dolmabahce in Istanbul).

You can easily discern different influences in the paintings of Turkish artists.

For example, Nurullah Berk starts with a still life, then something similar to Picasso.

And then it turns out something in the spirit of Malevich and Kandinsky.

Cemal Tollu from the same crowd is also influenced by Guernica.
