15 March - 28 April 2022

Kemer

Kemer is the city where people go on vacation to all-inclusive resorts. But still, this is not purely a village with hotels, but a small town where there is (almost) everything you need. And it's really beautiful there.

When I arrived in mid-March, it was completely off-season, there were few people in the city, and the hotels were cheap - less than 20 dollars. View from my hotel.

Çamyuva

I live not in Kemer itself, but in the neighboring village of Çamyuva, from where it’s about 5-7 kilometers to Kemer. Here is my aprtment site (about a week later the pool was already filled).

The houses are two-story, each with one apartment per floor.

It's a 20-minute walk to the sea, but all the infrastructure is close - shops, cheap cafes. Buses run to the center of Kemer every 15-20 minutes.

Sign at the entrance.

In the surrounding area, some of the buildings are the same communities as mine, and some are private houses. My neighbors are distinguished farmers; they have chickens, geese, and goats.

Kiriş

Literally on the other side of the river from Çamyuva is the village of Kiriş, separated from Kemer itself by Çalış Hill. From the very beginning I had my eye on the hill in order to somehow climb it

There is a short road from Kemer to Kiriş, passing through a small pass near the mountain. It then abuts the river, and passage for cars there is only open in the summer, when the river dries up, but in principle in the spring you can wade barefoot or ride a bicycle. The road looks like this.

Moving across the river.

Mount Tahtali

One of the main attractions of the Kemer region is Mount Tahtali, which can be climbed by the Olimpos Teleferik cable car.

The climb costs 35 euros for tourists, 120 liras for locals (about 8 euros), plus 40 liras for a transfer from the main road to the start of the cable car and back (there are transfers from the center of Kemer that are more expensive, but it was still more convenient for me to get to the turn off the main road than first to the center of Kemer).

The view is already good from here.

Somewhere here there is a hike, you can even buy a one-way ticket for the cable car, but the distance there is long, and the climb is one and a half thousand kilometers.
