Portugal: Lisbon, Porto, Guimaraes, Cascais, Sintra

28 July - 10 August 2011

Lisbon

Portugal in the summer of 2011 was my first independent trip (without a travel agency). For the first time I bought a ticket myself and used booking.com, everything was new and unusual. I had just graduated from university and started earning decent money - I could start traveling, and Portugal was practically the dream of my childhood, well, after Rio de Janeiro, of course. From the perspective of the years I have lived, the organization of this trip seems to me extremely unsuccessful - it was possible to see more. I also flew to Lisbon on a direct flight from Moscow, the ticket cost, as I remember now, 450 euros. In 2019, for that kind of money, I would fly somewhere to America, and to get to Portugal I would spend no more than 200. Portugal was and remains beautiful. I was in Lisbon for four days: two at the beginning and two at the end.

Cascais

I included as many as 5 days of beach holiday in Cascais in the itinerary. This, of course, was very stupid, I realized how pointless a beach holiday is, especially in places not intended for this. Although Cascais is considered a resort, the beach there is small and half combined with the port, and the water there is cold even in August.

The city itself is beautiful, but you don't need much time to explore it.
