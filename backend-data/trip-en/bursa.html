Istanbul

I started 2023 with a short trip around Turkey - I flew to Istanbul and returned from there by land, visiting cities along the way. I haven't been to Istanbul for more than six months. Since then, hotels there have become cheaper, and food has become more expensive (as elsewhere in Turkey). Bursa

Bursa is the fourth largest city in Turkey, the largest I had not been to. Bursa is quite close to Istanbul, you can go there even as a day trip. From the European part of Istanbul, the fastest way is by ferries departing from the central piers to the northern suburbs of Bursa, from where the center is 20-30 kilometers away. From the Asian side it may be faster to take a bus, depending on how far it is to the bus station.

The ferry arrives in the city of Mudanya, famous for the fact that the armistice between Turkey and Greece was signed here in 1922.

Afyonkarahisar

I didn’t expect much from the city of Afyonkarahisar, but it turned out that it is one of the best preserved historical cities in Turkey, no worse than Safranbolu or Urfa.

It was a very foggy morning.

Visibility is only a few houses ahead.

The monument to the dervish is the same as in Kutahya, only it does not rotate.

I moved towards the castle, and soon found myself in a quarter of Ottoman houses

The houses here are unusually colorful, usually in Turkey houses are white, black, yellow, but not pink or green.

This street is especially colorful with its paving stones.

The terrain is also picturesque; some streets run along slopes.

Isparta

Another city starting with the letter I, just two hours drive north from Antalya. Isparta was destroyed by an earthquake, so there are few historical buildings here, but the city is pleasant.

For once, the bus station in a Turkish city is not in the middle of nowhere, but only 2 kilometers from the center. But, alas, a new one is already being built at the end of the world, judging by the maps.
