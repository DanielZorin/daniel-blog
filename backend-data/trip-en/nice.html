France: Nice, Cannes, Marseille

11-14 March 2014

Nice

Nice is considered an expensive luxury resort for the elite. As for the high cost, I would argue, in the city center I rented an excellent hotel for 30 euros, which is inexpensive for France (not on the beach, yeah), but there is enough elite here.

Only in Nice do chandeliers with rhinestones hang on the streets.

Lanterns with a crown.

Typical landscape of the Cote d'Azur.

Majestic shopping experience.

All this splendor is destroyed by the fact that the residents of Nice fundamentally do not clean up after their dogs.

Externally, Nice looks more like Italy.

Actually, this was Italy, until the rulers of Piedmont gave it to France in exchange for help in the fight against Austria in the mid-19th century. The most famous native of Nice was unhappy with this, because he was made into a foreigner in Italy.

There is also a local Provençal language, in which the inscriptions are sometimes duplicated.

Old Nice is a small triangle under a hill.

New Nice stretches to the horizon.

Promenade des Anglais.

As a beach, this place is not very good, there are pebbles and there is a highway nearby. But all the infrastructure is there.

But as a promenade place it’s excellent.

Buddha statues in the main square.

Musicians play Hawa Nagila.

The Statue of Liberty is also here.

In the old days, Nice was the main Russian health resort. Now there are still a lot of Russians here, but not as many as in Cyprus.

Several artifacts remain from the Russian nobles. Firstly, the Orthodox Church.

Secondly, the street where it stands.

Thirdly, there are several shops.

There is also a grocery store.

I must say that the prices here are more decent than in Russian stores in Cyprus.

But there’s still not much to buy here, except perhaps Russian delicacies.

Cannes

In Cannes, I finally saw the stereotypical Provence: people wear berets here.

And they play petanque.

A conference on real estate sales was taking place in the city, the buffet reception had just begun, and the whole center was packed with people in jackets with wine glasses

The Cannes steps are occupied by a conference poster.

Russia is the guest of honor.

Next to the stairs is a long row of handprints of famous actors and film directors. Although I’m not a film expert, I recognized many.

In such places, I am always happy to realize that my handwriting is super neat and legible. Many people's cursive Latin script is hard to understand. Tarantino can be called a champion in this matter, however, his initials are so recognizable that it is not necessary to write down the remaining letters.

Unlike Nice, the beach in Cannes is sandy.

It's called Croisette.

From above you can see that Cannes is now primarily a cinema on the beach.

The city is extensively built up with villas.

It used to be a small fishing village on a hill. It was famous mainly due to the fact that here (more precisely on the island of St. Margaret, which can be seen in the distance in the photo from the beach above) the man in the iron mask was imprisoned. This is reflected in Dumas' novel. Although many confuse the Iron Mask and the Count of Monte Cristo, believing that he lived in the Chateau d'If in Marseille.

Painting is not the theme of the festival.

The monument to heroes is understandable.

Garbage cans, although from afar I would have mistaken this building for a stall.

Marseille

For some reason, Marseille has a very bad reputation. The city is associated with garbage, crowds of migrants and shootings, although this is completely untrue. Of course, this is not the resort town of Nice or a quiet Provençal village, but a city with a population of a few million, but for its size, Marseille looks decent and very nice.

There are no more migrants in Marseille than in Paris. In the city center they are concentrated in the market area. Of course, I lived there because there are cheap hotels there.

For some reason, Ukraine has found its way into the warm company of African countries.

Prices on the Marseille market are unusually low for Europe. 25 rubles a bottle of cola is a good price for Russia, for example.

Of course, if you look in a dark corner, there will be garbage and the smell of urine.

But let's look at this tree planter right across from this corner. If this happened in Brussels, it would be full to the brim with garbage. So Marseille is certainly much cleaner than Brussels.

There is an old piece of Marseille that is beautiful, like small French towns.

Napoleon lived here when he was an officer.

As in Paris and Vienna, the city is divided into districts that are numbered from 1.

The cathedral is closed for reconstruction.

The newer part of the city has long, straight streets.

A lot of cars.

All sorts of fashionable entertainment.

Branded French trams.

Modern Art.

A giraffe with books inside.

A small beach called Catalan in honor of the Catalan fishermen who lived in the area (this fact was also mentioned in the book “The Count of Monte Cristo”).

By the way, about the Count of Monte Cristo. He was imprisoned in the Chateau d'If, located on an island near Marseille. For some reason, many people mistakenly believe that the man in the iron mask was held there a century earlier (in fact, it was in Cannes) , so we can say that the Chateau d'If is famous for two prisoners, one of whom has never been there, and the other is completely fictional.

A visit to Marseille would be incomplete without a panoramic view of the city. It can be seen closest to the center from the entrance to the harbor, where the palace of Napoleon III stands.

But it is better to go up to the Notre-Dame de la Garde church.

Fishermen have long blessed their boats in the church, and now they hang special signs with gratitude.

This is the best view.

The city is located on the hills. In the vicinity of Marseille there are French "fjords" called calanques.

City center.

Residential areas.

Chateau d'If and neighboring islands.

Stadium.

The large white rectangular building in the center is Le Corbusier's "Rainbow City", the very first house that implemented the concept of life under one roof, when home, school, hospital, work, etc. everything is located in one building. About the same as in the Main Building of Moscow State University. We can say that this is the world's first commie block.
