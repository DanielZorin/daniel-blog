27 October - 1 November 2013

Kunming

Many people are probably thinking, how can 1.3 billion people live on top of each other in China? The shocking truth is that China is much freer and more spacious than Russia. In Russia, people still live in communal apartments, Russia has the worst traffic jams in the world, and public transport is so crowded that in Tula I could barely fit into the minibus standing bent over. And this is what an ordinary hotel room in China looks like (costs about 700 rubles / $20 per day).

Stone Forest

The Stone Forest is an absolutely fantastic place, yet surprisingly empty. Maybe the rain played a role, because of it I had to clean the lens from drops every five minutes, but I doubt it, since most tourists come here on excursion buses, that is, their program is fixed in advance and does not change due to the rain. Groups of tourists are driven through one section of the park; the rest are so deserted that you can walk for half an hour and not see a single living soul, only huge stones.

Entrance to the Stone Forest costs more than a thousand rubles (175 yuan), but this is the case when it is worth it. It costs another 25 yuan to take the minibus from the ticket office to the park entrance, although you can walk when the weather is better.

Shangri-La

Shangri-La is not Tibet yet, but it’s almost there. You don’t need to obtain special permits to travel here; you can get there by bus from the southeast. The city was renamed relatively recently to attract tourists. By Chinese standards, this city is microscopic, with a population of only 130 thousand. Let's see what a small city in China looks like.

For the first time I was traveling in a sleeper bus with beds. Before this, I either slept sitting or rode with a reclining seat.

On the horizon are the Himalayas!

Lijiang

Lijiang is probably the most tourist place in China if we compare the number of tourists with the city's population. 99.9% of tourists are Chinese, and this figure is not a figure of speech, there really is a thousand Chinese for every foreigner.
