Liverpool

Liverpool is just an hour's drive from Manchester. In the 19th century it was the second most important city in England after London, and the main port. The world's first regular passenger railway ran from Liverpool to Manchester and opened in 1830. Later, when the importance of seaports greatly decreased, the city fell into decay. But then The Beatles appeared, thanks to them everyone still knows about this city. What remains from Liverpool's 19th century prosperity is pretentious Victorian architecture, which was for some time in poor condition (as in Yangon or Mumbai), but now mostly restored.

Instead of a main square, there is a main pier - what people saw when arriving in Liverpool by sea.

Next to the equestrian monument to King Edward VII there is now a Beatles monument.

Like many other former industrial and port cities, the docks in the city center are now being converted into bars and offices.

Let's look a little at a supermarket in the UK. The most common brand in this country is Tesco, just like in good old Thailand. England happens to be the birthplace of this network, and there are more than 3,000 of them (in Thailand - a little less than 2,000; there are also in Malaysia, Ireland, the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary and Poland, with a total of less than 1,000). In England, there are especially many Tesco stores in the format of a small supermarket rather than a hypermarket. I visited one of these in the center of Liverpool. A distinctive feature of these stores is that they often write simply gigantic numbers on the price tags. Also, if the product costs less than a pound, then instead of 0.49 they write 49p (49 pence). 1 GBP is slightly more expensive than 1 EUR. Bananas are sold individually for 25p.

Apples - 40 pence, kiwis - 35, pineapples - 115, melons - 210.

Tangerines 1.55, cucumbers 1.15 (half a kilo), tomatoes 0.75 (360 grams), cherry tomatoes - 1 pound.

Onions 0.30, carrots 0.40, potatoes 1.40 per kilo or 1.70 per 2 kilos.

Peaches 1.25, plums 1.75.

Apricots 1.15, strawberries 2.

Mushrooms for 1 pound - I constantly bought different types.

Meat. The store is small, so the selection is modest, but basically everything is there. Steak for 2.60, 400 grams of beef for 4.10, what else do you need?

Bacon costs 7.5 per kilo, the same price for burgers.

Chicken fillet costs 2.50 per 580 grams, minced meat - 7.70 per kilo.

I often bought ready-made or semi-ready dishes for the oven, all of them were on sale - two pieces for 4 pounds. There are both Indian and Italian stuff there.

There are also these sandwiches for 2-3 pounds.

Rolls for 2.50, cold salads for 2.20.

Caesar salad for 2.80, fruit slices for 1-2.

Eggs are cheaper than in Ireland - 80 pence for 6 regular or 1.05 for 6 free range.

Milk. It’s tricky here - there are bottles of 1 or 2 liters, and there are bottles in pints - this is more than half a liter. The cheapest is 4 pints (2 liters) for 1.10.

Yogurts. I used to buy Onken - two huge yoghurts for two pounds, one of which replaces the whole breakfast.

A good chocolate mousse for 1.15 (4 pieces), other sets of 4-6 are also inexpensive.

Butter costs £1.60-2, sliced ​​cheeses are around £2. Cheap brie cheese for £1 hasn't made it into the picture.

Bread. Toast is quite popular here - from 65 pence to 1.30. There is also fresh bread, unlike America, although by the evening at Tesco they are almost always sold out.

Sunflower oil costs 80 pence, olive oil costs £2.7. Vinegar - 0.83.

Sauces and ketchups for £1-2. There is horseradish sauce, tartar sauce, mustard, and barbecue sauce.

Canned food. There is tuna, beef, beans, peas, potatoes.

Chocolates. There's not a very large selection of them here, and they all cost 70 cents or three for 1.20.

Pringles cost £2.50 - more than usual.

Coca-Cola costs 5 cents more than a two-liter Pepsi.

My favorite Dr. Pepper is sold everywhere in the UK, I usually bought it.

Juices for 1.40.

Coffee from Starbucks costs 1.90, regular coffee here costs 1.60, but in general it is often sold cheaper, even in small stores. Iced coffee is popular here.

Beer. There is Guinness here for £5.25 for 4 cans, which by the way is cheaper than in Ireland. Though in this store you can't buy one can, only a pack.
